## Claim 1: "On Wednesday 8 June 2016, Access Now and nearly seventy civil-society organisations from five continents publicly launched the #KeepItOn coalition"

Source: https://www.accessnow.org/press-release/access-now-launches-global-keepiton-campaign-nearly-70-organizations-committing-fight-internet-shutdowns/
Source content: "PUBLISHED: 9 June 2016" and opening line "Today Access Now, along with nearly 70 organizations from five continents, launched the #KeepitOn campaign to fight internet shutdowns worldwide."
Comparison: The cited primary source explicitly dates the launch as 9 June 2016 (both in the publication metadata and within the body sentence "Today... launched"). Cross-reference: the Digital Rights Watch Australia announcement URL also encodes 2016/06/09. The body's claim of "Wednesday 8 June 2016" is contradicted by its own primary source. Note: 8 June 2016 was indeed a Wednesday and 9 June 2016 was a Thursday, so either the date or the day-of-week label is wrong relative to the source.
Decision: discrepancy
