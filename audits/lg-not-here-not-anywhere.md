## Claim 10: "The campaign's published demands are for a moratorium on new data centres in Ireland until three conditions are met: a national cap is set on data-centre energy demand aligned with Irish climate targets; new facilities operate entirely on renewable electricity (through on-site renewable generation with storage, or a dedicated renewable grid connection); and no new fossil-fuel infrastructure is built to power them."

Source: https://notherenotanywhere.com/campaigns/data-centres/
Source content: The data-centres page articulates four numbered conditions, not three: (1) "Moratorium until a national policy sets a cap on data centre energy demand aligned with renewable energy and climate targets"; (2) "New data centres must be powered entirely by one of the following" — on-site renewable generation with storage, or off-site renewable power with dedicated grid connection; (3) Grid flexibility, "not allowing data centres to use fossil fuel generation as the means"; (4) "New data centres should have infrastructure in place to enable heat generated from them to be utilised for district heating systems".
Comparison: The body presents the demands as exactly "three conditions" and lists three. The source lists four conditions, with condition (4) — district-heating reuse — absent from the body. Characterising the demands as a complete set of three contradicts the cited source, which carries four. This is a body/source content mismatch, not paraphrase variation.
Decision: discrepancy
