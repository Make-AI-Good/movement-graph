## Claim 23: "first European national-court judicial ruling against a deployed governmental algorithmic decision-making system on human-rights grounds" / "first such ruling under Article 8 ECHR"

Source: https://academic.oup.com/hrlr/article/22/2/ngac010/6568079
Source content: the Ranchordás & Schuurmans HRLR article frames the SyRI judgment as a significant precedent for human-rights-based litigation against digital-welfare-state systems but does not categorically state it is "the first" European national-court ruling of its kind.
Comparison: this is a superlative-shape comparative claim ("first such ruling"). Per the Auditor profile's source rule, superlative/precedence claims fall in the Wikipedia-tiebreaker-only category and require ≥1 independent canonical source for direct support. No canonical source I consulted in this pass directly endorses the "first" framing; the precedent value is described in several places but without a categorical "first" claim. The Polish Constitutional Tribunal judgment K 53/16 (June 2018) — referenced in this entity's own body as eighteen months earlier — is itself a European national-court ruling against a deployed governmental data-processing system on constitutional-rights grounds; whether "civil-litigation forum" vs "constitutional-litigation forum" is the controlling distinction that preserves "first" for SyRI is a judgment-loaded edge.
Decision: uncorroborated
