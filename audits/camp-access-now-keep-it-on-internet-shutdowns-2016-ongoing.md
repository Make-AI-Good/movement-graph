## Claim 17: Anuradha Bhasin v. Union of India (January 2020) "laid down the three-step test of legality, legitimacy, and proportionality"

Source: https://www.lawctopus.com/clatalogue/clat-pg/anuradha-bhasin-v-union-of-india-internet-shutdown/ (via web search aggregation)
Source content: "the proportionality test... stands on four legs: firstly, the restriction should serve a legitimate goal, secondly, it must be a suitable means of furthering this goal, thirdly, there must not be any less restrictive but equally effective alternative, and fourthly, the measure must not have a disproportionate impact on the right-holder."
Comparison: The Anuradha Bhasin judgment (10 January 2020) applies the K.S. Puttaswamy four-pronged proportionality test, not a "three-step test of legality, legitimacy, and proportionality." A "legality / legitimacy / proportionality" formulation exists in international-law commentary on rights-restriction tests but is not what Anuradha Bhasin laid down. The body's framing is not directly corroborated by the canonical sources for the judgment available in this pass.
Decision: uncorroborated
