## Claim 1: "In November 2006, Jac sm Kee, the then-Women's Rights Programme Manager at the Association for Progressive Communications (APC), launched the inaugural Take Back the Tech! 16-day campaign"

Source: https://www.apc.org/en/pubs/facts-takebackthetech ; https://www.takebackthetech.net/about
Source content: "Take Back the Tech! was launched in 2006 by a group of young women in Southeast Asia" and "Women's Rights Programme Manager, Association for Progressive Communications" (Jac sm Kee's titled role). takebackthetech.net/about: "Take Back the Tech! was initiated in 2006 by the Association for Progressive Communications (APC) Women's Rights Programme."
Comparison: 2006 launch confirmed; Jac sm Kee's title as WRP Manager confirmed; her role as the campaign's principal organiser is supported by the campaign-contact attribution on facts-takebackthetech.
Decision: corroborated

## Claim 2: "co-founded with — among others — Chat Garcia Ramilo, the future APC Executive Director who at that point was managing the APC Women's Rights Programme"

Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chat_Garcia_Ramilo ; https://www.apc.org/en/pubs/facts-takebackthetech
Source content: Wikipedia: "She is a co-founder of the Take Back the Tech! campaign" and "She became the executive director of the Association for Progressive Communications after serving as manager of its Communications Women's Rights Programme for seven years" and "Since April 2017, she leads the Association for Progressive Communications (APC)". Facts page: Jac sm Kee held the WRP Manager title at the 2006 launch.
Comparison: Co-founder claim verified. But the body's claim that Chat Garcia Ramilo "at that point [2006] was managing the APC Women's Rights Programme" is in tension with the facts page (which credits Jac sm Kee as WRP Manager in 2006) and with Wikipedia's "seven years" tenure (placing her WRP-Manager start ~2010, not 2006). The word "managing" is judgment-loaded — it could mean held the WRP Manager title (contradicted by sources) or simply be on the WRP management team (not contradicted, not confirmed).
Decision: uncorroborated
