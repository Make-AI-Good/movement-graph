## Claim 9: "The legislative history identifies the precipitating incident as a late-2022 deepfake video falsely showing President Yoon Suk-yeol endorsing a county mayoral candidate during a municipal-election cycle"

Source: https://www.koreaherald.com/article/3329716; corroborated by https://www.france24.com/en/live-news/20220214-deepfake-democracy-south-korean-candidate-goes-virtual-for-votes and https://en.cryptonomist.ch/2022/03/14/south-korea-yoon-suk-yeols-deepfake-garners-votes/
Source content: Korea Herald: "in 2022, a deepfake video falsely showed President Yoon endorsing a county chief candidate in South Gyeongsang Province." Independent sources place the manipulated endorsement video in the lead-up to the 1 June 2022 local elections — i.e. circulating in May 2022, ahead of the 8th nationwide local elections that June. (The Cryptonomist's separately-dated Yoon deepfake material from March 2022 is the earlier presidential-campaign avatar, not this incident.)
Comparison: Korea Herald does not support "late-2022"; it places the incident generically "in 2022". Independent sources specifically place it in May 2022 ahead of the 1 June 2022 local elections — i.e. mid-2022, not late-2022. The body's temporal specifier is contradicted by the available record. "County mayoral candidate" is a defensible paraphrase of "county chief" (Korean gunsu) but the date specifier is the load-bearing factual element here.
Decision: discrepancy
