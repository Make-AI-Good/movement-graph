## Claim 16: "the Karnataka High Court interim order of 19 October 2020 holding that the State of Karnataka and its instrumentalities cannot deny any service or benefit to any citizen for not having installed Aarogya Setu, and finding prima facie that no informed consent had been obtained from users for the data-sharing arrangements set out in the Aarogya Setu Data Access and Knowledge Sharing Protocol 2020"

Source: https://www.thequint.com/cyber/aarogya-setu-karnataka-high-court-restrains-centre-nic-data-sharing-without-informed-consent (the body's own cited source); https://www.covid19litigation.org/case-index/india-high-court-karnataka-two-judge-bench-no-7483-2020-2021-01-25 ; https://www.livelaw.in/news-updates/karnataka-high-court-privacy-article-21-constitution-aarogya-setu-app-168950
Source content: The Quint article cited in the body states: "The Karnataka High Court, on Monday, 25 January restrained the Central government and the National Informatics Centre (NIC) from sharing the data of Aarogya Setu tracking application users without obtaining their informed consent. … It prima facie held that there was no informed consent taken of users for sharing response data as provided in the Aarogya Setu Data Access and Knowledge Sharing Protocol, 2020." LiveLaw and the COVID-19 Litigation database both date this informed-consent prima facie finding to 25 January 2021. The 19 October 2020 order in the same case was a distinct earlier order holding that "in the absence of legislation, central and state government or their agencies or instrumentalities cannot deny any benefit or service to a citizen for not installing Aarogya Setu" — the cannot-deny-services half of the body's claim.
Comparison: The body conflates two separate Karnataka HC orders. The 19 October 2020 order held the cannot-deny-services point but did not include the informed-consent prima facie finding; the informed-consent finding came on 25 January 2021 from the Oka–Shetty Division Bench. The body's source citation (The Quint URL) reports the 25 January 2021 order, not a 19 October 2020 informed-consent order. The body further narrows "central and state government or their agencies or instrumentalities" to "State of Karnataka and its instrumentalities", which is a tighter (and inaccurate) framing of the actual 19 October 2020 holding.
Decision: discrepancy
