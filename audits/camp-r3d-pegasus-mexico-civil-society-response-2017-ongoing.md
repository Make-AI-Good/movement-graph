## Claim 15: "the Federal Prosecutor's Office made one named criminal arrest in 2021 — a former Mexican prosecutor charged with illegally selling Pegasus access to a private operator"

Source: https://www.jornada.com.mx/notas/2021/11/08/sociedad/vinculan-a-proceso-a-empleado-de-empresa-privada-por-espionaje-con-pegasus/ (and corroborating coverage from The Washington Post, Times of Israel, Haaretz, Proceso)
Source content: The November 2021 arrest was of Juan Carlos García Rivera, a technician/employee of a private company (KBH business group / Proyectos y Diseños VME — an NSO Group intermediary firm in Mexico). He was charged with the illegal interception of communications against a journalist (later identified as Carmen Aristegui), not with selling Pegasus access. He was a private-sector employee, not a former Mexican prosecutor.
Comparison: Three elements of the body's sentence are contradicted by the canonical record: (a) García Rivera was a technician at a private company, not a former Mexican prosecutor; (b) the charge was illegal interception of communications against a journalist, not "illegally selling Pegasus access"; (c) the structural framing ("selling access to a private operator") inverts the actual fact pattern (a private-firm employee using Pegasus to spy on a journalist).
Decision: correction
