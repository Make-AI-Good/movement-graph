## Claim 20: `outcomes:` (scalar): "the campaign has provided the documented evidentiary basis for the Dutch Data Protection Authority's €290 million fine against Uber for unlawful transfers of driver data to the United States"

Source: https://www.autoriteitpersoonsgegevens.nl/en/current/dutch-dpa-imposes-a-fine-of-290-million-euro-on-uber-because-of-transfers-of-drivers-data-to-the-us (the DPA's own statement — fetch timed out on first attempt; verified via secondary canonical reporting: Library of Congress Global Legal Monitor and The Record from Recorded Future News, both citing the DPA statement)
Source content: The fine itself — €290 million, August 2021–November 2023 unlawful transfers — is fully corroborated. On the investigation's origin, the Dutch DPA's public communication (per the Library of Congress summary and the Recorded Future reporting) records that the investigation "was spurred by complaints from more than 170 French drivers, who sought help from the French human rights interest group the Ligue des droits de l'Homme (LDH). That organization then sent a complaint to the French DPA, which worked closely with the Dutch on the probe." Neither the DPA's own publication nor the secondary canonical record names Worker Info Exchange or ADCU as the evidentiary source.
Comparison: The fine's existence, amount, and grounds are verified. The causal attribution — that *this campaign* "provided the documented evidentiary basis" — is not supported by the entity's cited sources (none of sources 1-10 cover the DPA fine) and is not supported by the canonical record on the DPA investigation, which names a different complainant chain. This is the audit's most consequential edge: a causal claim about the campaign's role in producing a regulator's specific decision, against a canonical record that documents a different procedural origin. Per the source rule, causal claims need ≥1 canonical source beyond Wikipedia; none exists for the WIE-as-evidentiary-basis claim. The Auditor does not pick a winner where canonical sources do not support the claim.
Decision: unverifiable
