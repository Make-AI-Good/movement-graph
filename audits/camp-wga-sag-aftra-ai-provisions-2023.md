## Claim 15: "SAG-AFTRA and the AMPTP reached a tentative agreement at 12:01 am PST on 9 November 2023."

Source: https://www.sagaftra.org/sag-aftra-national-board-approves-tentative-agreement-recommends-ratification-2023-tvtheatrical
Source content: SAG-AFTRA's own announcement describes the tentative agreement as "reached with the Alliance of Motion Picture and Television Producers (AMPTP) on November 8, 2023." Wikipedia's article on the strike quotes the strike-suspension notice — "As of 12:01am on November 9, our strike is officially suspended and all picket locations are closed" — which is the time the strike ended, not the time the tentative agreement was reached.
Comparison: The entity conflates the strike-suspension time (12:01 am PST on 9 November, when picketing ended) with the tentative-agreement reach date (November 8, 2023, per SAG-AFTRA's own announcement). The agreement was reached on Nov 8; the strike was then suspended at midnight transitioning into Nov 9.
Decision: correction

## Claim 16: "the SAG-AFTRA National Board approved it on 10 November by 86%… the membership ratified the contract on 5 December 2023 by 78.33% on a 38.15% turnout."

Source: https://www.sagaftra.org/sag-aftra-national-board-approves-tentative-agreement-recommends-ratification-2023-tvtheatrical (corroborated by Wikipedia)
Source content: SAG-AFTRA's own statement confirms National Board approval by "86% to 14%" on November 10, 2023 (in a video conference plenary). Wikipedia confirms "December 5, 2023 – 78.33% approval with 38.15% turnout."
Comparison: National Board date (10 November), approval percentage (86%), ratification date (5 December 2023), ratification percentage (78.33%) and turnout (38.15%) all match. Wikipedia's article presents the National Board approval inside the November 8 section without an explicit calendar header for the Nov 10 plenary, which is a Wikipedia internal ambiguity; SAG-AFTRA's own press release and major industry coverage place the Board action on Nov 10.
Decision: corroborated
