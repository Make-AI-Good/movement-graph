## Claim 1: "incorporated in the District of Columbia in 2017 and publicly launched in February 2019"

Source: https://www.causeiq.com/organizations/internet-society-foundation,823285688/ ; https://www.isocfoundation.org/about/ ; https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Internet_Society_Foundation
Source content: CauseIQ — "Year of Formation: 2017"; about page — "The Internet Society Foundation is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit corporation organized under the laws of the District of Columbia, USA"; Wikipedia — "The Internet Society Foundation was created in 2017 as its independent philanthropic arm". None of the cited sources state a February 2019 launch month. The cited November 2019 launch announcement is dated 22 November 2019 and calls the Foundation "newly-launched" without specifying February 2019.

Decision: uncorroborated

## Claim 5: Internet Society "serves as the sole member of the Internet Society Foundation, the Public Interest Registry ..., the IETF Administration LLC, and the Connected Giving Foundation"

Source: https://www.internetsociety.org/transparency/structure-and-related-organizations/
Source content: "Internet Society Foundation ... is a subsidiary of the Internet Society"; "PIR is a subsidiary of Internet Society"; "Internet Society is the sole member of the Internet Engineering Task Force Administration LLC (IETF LLC)"; "CGF is a subsidiary of Internet Society".
Comparison: Source uses "subsidiary" for ISF / PIR / CGF and "sole member" for IETF LLC. In US 501(c)(3) supporting-organization structures, "sole member" is the standard legal mechanism by which one nonprofit becomes a subsidiary of another, so the body's blanket "sole member" is the more precise technical paraphrase of the source's "subsidiary" framing. Substantive ISOC-controls-all-four claim matches.
Decision: corroborated
