## Claim 8: Women constitute 58.5% of complainants (presented as a 2023 figure)

Source: https://digitalrightsfoundation.pk/wp-content/uploads/2024/04/DRFs-Cyber-Harassment-Helpline-Report-2023.pdf
Source content: PDF page 8: "Consistently, women have been the largest group to report instances of online harassment, and overall, they make up 58.5% of the people we have assisted." This sentence sits within the prose framing the cumulative 7-year caseload of 16,849. The 2023-specific gender breakdown on PDF page 18 (Fig 5.1) shows FEMALE 1,371; MALE 1,054; TRANSGENDER 39; OTHERS 9 — i.e. 1,371 of 2,473 = 55.4% women in 2023 alone.
Comparison: The entity body presents "Women constitute 58.5% of complainants" in a paragraph anchored to "2,473 new complaints received in 2023". Per the report, 58.5% is the **cumulative** women's share across the seven-year caseload, not the 2023 single-year figure (which is 55.4%). The secondary press sources (Friday Times, Dawn) repeat the 58.5% figure without explicit time anchoring, but the report itself locates 58.5% at the cumulative line, not the 2023 single-year line. The body's framing reads the 58.5% as a 2023 statistic, which the report does not support.
Decision: correction

## Claim 9: Women aged 18-30 are the most-represented single demographic at 1,371 complaints

Source: https://digitalrightsfoundation.pk/wp-content/uploads/2024/04/DRFs-Cyber-Harassment-Helpline-Report-2023.pdf
Source content: PDF page 15 (Fig 3.1: Age distribution of complainants): the 18-30 age band has 1,371 complaints — this is the count across **all genders** in that age band. PDF page 18 (Fig 5.1: Gender of Complainants) also shows 1,371 — but this is the count of female complainants across **all ages**. The two figures coincide numerically but measure different things. The prose on page 15 reads only that "young adults, particularly women, between the ages of 18 to 30, are most prone to facing some type of online harassment"; no gender-specific count for the 18-30 band is stated.
Comparison: The body's claim "women aged 18-30 are the most-represented single demographic at 1,371 complaints" attributes the 1,371 to the intersection of female + 18-30 — but 1,371 in the source is either the 18-30 age band (all genders) or the female total (all ages), depending on which figure is being read. The report does not publish a gender-and-age cross-tabulation that gives 1,371 women aged 18-30 specifically. The body conflates two unrelated 1,371s into a single intersected demographic the source does not actually report.
Decision: correction
