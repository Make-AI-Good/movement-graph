## Claim 21: "in the middle of a joint strike that would close 32 days later for the WGA and seven weeks later for SAG-AFTRA"

Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2023_Writers_Guild_of_America_strike ; https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2023_SAG-AFTRA_strike
Source content: WGA strike "ended on September 27 at 12:01 a.m. PDT"; "Union leadership voted to end the strike on September 26". SAG-AFTRA strike "officially began on July 14 and was suspended on November 9, 2023, at 12:01 a.m. PST".
Comparison: Three sub-claims fail. (a) "Joint strike" on 4 October 2023: the WGA strike had ended seven days earlier on 27 September 2023, so only SAG-AFTRA was actively on strike on 4 October 2023 — not a joint strike. (b) "Would close 32 days later for the WGA": the WGA strike had *already closed* before the roundtable; the body's forward-looking framing is inverted. (c) "Seven weeks later for SAG-AFTRA": 4 October 2023 to 9 November 2023 is 36 days ≈ 5 weeks and 1 day, not seven weeks (~49 days). All three subclaims are factually wrong against Wikipedia primary-date records, which are sufficient alone for dates of public events per the source rule.
Decision: discrepancy
