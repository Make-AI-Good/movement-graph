# EDRi-gram

EDRi-gram is the bi-weekly newsletter of [European Digital Rights (EDRi)](../organizations/org-edri.md), [published since 29 January 2003](https://edri.org/our-work/edri-gram-number-1/) — seven months after EDRi's founding in June 2002. It [collects and summarises](https://edri.org/take-action/edri-gram/) the most important digital rights news from across EDRi's network of European civil-society organisations, covering privacy, surveillance, artificial intelligence, data protection, copyright, net neutrality, and platform regulation. Active and bi-weekly as of 2026, EDRi-gram is among the longest-running newsletters in the European digital-rights field and the primary publication channel through which EDRi communicates developments in European digital rights law and policy to its member organisations, allies, and the broader public.

## Origins and first issue

The [first issue](https://edri.org/our-work/edri-gram-number-1/) appeared on 29 January 2003, addressing five categories of threat to digital rights that would remain characteristic of the newsletter's subsequent coverage: copyright and internet infrastructure challenges; EU-level data-retention proposals then targeting the ePrivacy Directive; software-patent legislation moving through the European Parliament; UK government plans for national identity cards; and internet censorship actions by German authorities. A report on online restrictions in Russia rounded out the first issue, placing European digital rights in an international frame from the outset.

EDRi-gram framed its purpose from the first issue as monitoring and reporting on threats to "freedom of speech, freedom of information, privacy" across Europe. Fifteen years later EDRi used that framing as a retrospective anchor — running a comparison piece noting how closely those original five categories mapped onto contemporary digital rights concerns, the persistent shape of the agenda (surveillance, content control, data protection, intellectual property) across successive EU legislative cycles unchanged in essentials since 2003.

## Format and structure

EDRi-gram's [current format](https://edri.org/our-work/edri-gram-30-april-2026/), stable in its essentials across the newsletter's publication history, structures each issue around a lead editorial on the most pressing current policy development, followed by several shorter linked articles covering legislative, regulatory, or campaign news from across the EDRi network and beyond. A "Recommended" section highlights external resources from partner organisations — reports, analyses, and commentary from outside EDRi's own output. Each issue carries an upcoming events calendar relevant to the digital rights community and job listings in the digital rights sector. The newsletter is distributed by email subscription and published on the EDRi website, where a [complete archive](https://edri.org/edri-gram) of past issues is maintained.

## Topics and coverage cadence

Across more than twenty-three years of bi-weekly publication, EDRi-gram has covered the full arc of European digital rights law and policy. Early issues tracked the 2003 software-patent battles and the EU data-retention campaigns; later editions covered ACTA mobilisation (2010–2012), GDPR negotiations (2012–2018), and the European Citizens' Initiatives of the 2020s. In recent years the newsletter has foregrounded EU AI Act developments, AI policing systems, age verification legislation, biometric surveillance, and platform accountability — reflecting the same policy areas as EDRi's own campaign programme but surveyed across a wider range of sources than EDRi's own output alone.

The newsletter's bi-weekly cadence makes it a primary-source archive of the European digital rights movement's agenda across more than two decades. Individual issues serve as date-stamped records of what civil-society organisations were tracking and responding to at specific moments in EU legislative cycles — a function relevant to researchers and advocates tracing the development of the European digital rights movement.

## Place in the corpus

EDRi-gram is [EDRi](../organizations/org-edri.md)'s primary outbound communication channel to the European digital-rights community — a function it has served continuously since shortly after EDRi's 2002 founding. In the corpus's terms, it is the first newsletter-type entry and anchors a structural gap: the corpus documented EDRi's campaigns (Reclaim Your Face, the EU AI Act coalition coordination) and its convening role without yet having an entry for the publication that is the continuous tissue connecting those campaigns — the channel through which EDRi builds and sustains the network's shared understanding of what is at stake in EU legislative processes.
