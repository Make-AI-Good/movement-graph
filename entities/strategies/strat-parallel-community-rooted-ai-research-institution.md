# Build a parallel community-rooted AI research institution as a counter to extractive Big Tech AI research

This Strategy describes the institutional approach of building an *independent, community-rooted AI research institute* — funder-independent of Big Tech AI companies — as the structural response to the incentive distortions of Big Tech corporate AI research labs. The strategy is defined by its *institutional form*, not by any one organisation that adopts it. Verified adopter currently in the corpus: the [Distributed AI Research Institute](../organizations/org-dair-institute.md).

## What the strategy is — grounded observations

*Facts in this section restate what is established in the entities listed in `grounded_in` and verified in their audit files; no claims rest on entities outside that list.*

The strategy has three structurally-distinct components:

1. **Institutional form.** An independent research institute — whether direct 501(c)(3), fiscally-sponsored project, or analogous local form — built outside both the Big Tech corporate lab and the academic department dependent on Big Tech research funding. The adopter's launch is itself an act of the strategy: a visible institutional alternative becomes a public artefact.
2. **Funder posture.** No corporate AI-company support in the funder mix; independence from the entities whose products the institute studies is a structural prerequisite of the form, not a stylistic preference. Foundation philanthropy is the substitute revenue source.
3. **Research method.** Research grounded in community expertise and the communities most affected by the systems being studied, rather than in questions set by the products of the companies the institute counters. This is what makes the institute *community-rooted* rather than merely *independent*.

The verified adopter, DAIR (`org-dair-institute`), instantiates all three: independent fiscally-sponsored launch on 2 December 2021 under Code for Science & Society; founding funding from four foundations (Ford, MacArthur, Kapor, Open Society Foundations) with no corporate AI support; research method anchored to community-led programmes. Adopter-specific execution detail — team distribution, programme map, board, fellows — lives in [`org-dair-institute`](../organizations/org-dair-institute.md), not here.

## What it accomplishes — interpretive claims

*This section is judgment, marked as judgment. The claims rest on the observations above and on the labelled-attribution effects in frontmatter.*

**Judgment:** the strategy substitutes an *organising-and-research-infrastructure investment* for the policy-advocacy or strategic-litigation tactic the corpus's other algorithmic-accountability entries lead with. Where advocacy and litigation produce outputs measured per-campaign, this strategy produces an *institution* whose effects accumulate at the field level over institutional timescales. It is slower, more capital-intensive, and more institutionally-load-bearing than the alternatives — and complementary rather than competing within the broader movement.

**Judgment:** the launch itself is a load-bearing act of the strategy, not a precondition for it. An independent institute's founding announcement is a public signal that an alternative to Big Tech AI research exists — and the documented effect of attracting senior research staff *out of* Big Tech labs into the new institute is the mechanism by which the form starts to shift where AI research can be done.

**Judgment, low-confidence:** the strategy *may* counter the structural incentives of Big Tech AI research only at the margins — producing high-quality critical commentary that runs alongside, not in place of, the corporate-lab research it counters. Whether the parallel-institution form moves where AI research is centred at the field level is unproven (see `counter_read`).

## Caveats on what is claimed here

`grounded_in` currently lists one audit-verified adopter, `org-dair-institute`. Per the interpretive trust contract, the analysis does not extend to entities outside that list. As the Auditor advances coverage of other plausible adopters in the corpus — other independent, community-grounded AI research entities — an effects-update extending `grounded_in` is queueable. The strategy's value as a connective entity grows with the number of verified adoption edges; one is the floor, not the ceiling.
