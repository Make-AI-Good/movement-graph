# Mercy Mutemi

Mercy Mutemi is the [managing partner](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/ford-foundation-announces-30-new-members-of-ford-global-fellowship-302418390.html) of [Nzili & Sumbi Advocates](../organizations/org-nzili-sumbi-advocates.md) and the founder and executive director of [Oversight Lab Africa](../organizations/org-oversight-lab-africa.md), and the most-cited African public voice on strategic litigation against Big Tech (see [Person entry](../persons/person-mercy-mutemi.md)). She is tracked here as a Voice because her sustained public output — counsel-of-record statements across the Foxglove / Nzili & Sumbi Kenya docket, named press profiles in TIME, the Coda Story long-read, the Ford Foundation interview, the International Bar Association feature, and Global Voices, named appearances at the [King's Place London panel](https://time.com/6188272/frances-haugen-daniel-motaung-facebook-whistleblowers/) (June 2022) and [re:publica 2024](https://re-publica.com/en/news/rp24-speaker-mercy-mutemi-shaping-africas-digital-future-through-litigation), and the standing institutional output of the Oversight Lab as the public-facing vehicle for the wider campaign — has done as much as any single individual's to install into international press, regulatory, and civil-society discourse the framing that Big Tech's African operations can be litigated in African courts on their merits, and that the labour and data-protection conditions under which those operations run are a matter for African public law.

She is the corpus's first African strategic-litigation Voice. Where the worker-side Voice [Daniel Motaung](voice-daniel-motaung.md) carries the case for outsourced content moderation through the courts and the union from the plaintiff side, Mutemi's voice carries the same cluster's argument from the litigator and institution-builder side — the Kenyan counsel of record on the Motaung suit, the 185 former Facebook content moderators' suit, the Tigray hate-speech petition, the OpenAI/Sama parliamentary petition, the [Oversight Lab's ODPC petition](../campaigns/camp-oversight-lab-meta-ray-ban-glasses-2026.md) over Meta's Ray-Ban smart glasses, and the worker challenge to Kenya's Business Laws (Amendment) Bill 2024 — and the named external voice through which the docket's procedural victories have been translated into international press and policy discourse. She is structurally complementary to the [Foxglove](../organizations/org-foxglove.md) UK / Global-North litigation-director voice [Cori Crider](../persons/person-cori-crider.md) — Crider's standing voice is the convener-strategist of the same cluster from London, Mutemi's is its Kenyan litigator and Africa-rooted institution-builder.

## Signature framings

Three framings in Mutemi's public output have travelled beyond the case file and the Oversight Lab's own materials.

- **"Building big tech on the backs of broken African youth."** Mutemi's signature anti-extractive framing of the Big-Tech / outsourcing-vendor / African-labour relationship is delivered in her [Coda Story long-read](https://www.codastory.com/authoritarian-tech/mercy-mutemi-meta-lawsuit/) (October 2024): "It's extraction of resources, with not enough coming back in terms of value, whether it's investing in people, investing in their growth and well-being. It's a classic 'use and dump' model." Her most-cited single sentence in the same piece — "This is the next frontier of technology, and they're building big tech on the backs of broken African youth, to put it simply" — and her firm's earlier press-cycle line on the [parliamentary petition](https://truthout.org/articles/kenyan-workers-who-trained-chatgpt-demand-govt-investigate-work-conditions/) ("the fortunes of Big Tech are currently built on the broken backs and minds of African youth") install the moderation- and data-labelling workforce as load-bearing infrastructure of the global AI economy rather than as a marginal cleanup function, and supply the working frame inside which the corpus's deepest Big-Tech-accountability cluster — [Foxglove](../organizations/org-foxglove.md), [Nzili & Sumbi Advocates](../organizations/org-nzili-sumbi-advocates.md), the [African Content Moderators Union](../organizations/org-african-content-moderators-union.md), the [Data Labellers Association](../organizations/org-data-labellers-association.md), the [Africa Tech Workers Movement](../organizations/org-africa-tech-workers-movement.md), and [Oversight Lab Africa](../organizations/org-oversight-lab-africa.md) — has made its public case.
- **"These companies purely exist for the cover of escaping liability."** Mutemi's most-cited single legal-architecture framing, also delivered in the [Coda Story long-read](https://www.codastory.com/authoritarian-tech/mercy-mutemi-meta-lawsuit/), names the platform-side jurisdictional move that the [Motaung case](../campaigns/camp-foxglove-motaung-meta-sama-kenya.md) and the [185-moderators case](../campaigns/camp-foxglove-185-moderators-meta-kenya.md) have been litigated against: "There's no registered office in Kenya for companies like Meta, TikTok, OpenAI ... it's important that companies have a presence in a country so that there can be discussions around accountability. These companies purely exist for the cover of escaping liability." The framing carries forward from Mutemi's [post-Motaung-filing press conference](https://www.opendemocracy.net/en/5050/facebook-meta-sama-daniel-motaung-court-kenya/) — at which she described the lawsuit as "seminal because it's one of the first against Facebook outside the West" — into the doctrinal core of the Kenyan Court of Appeal's [20 September 2024 ruling](https://www.foxglove.org.uk/2024/09/23/facebook-content-moderators-kenya-meta-appeal/) that Meta is amenable to jurisdiction in Kenya notwithstanding its place of registration, and is the framing through which Mutemi has made the procedural arc of the Foxglove / Nzili & Sumbi docket legible to international press audiences.
- **"How do we arrest the harm before it happens?"** Mutemi's signature theory-of-change framing is delivered in her [Ford Foundation interview](https://www.fordfoundation.org/news-and-stories/stories/when-technology-meets-justice-nettrice-gaskins-and-mercy-mutemi-on-building-equitable-digital-futures/) (February 2026): "How do we arrest the harm before it happens? ... How do we change behavior before another person has been harmed in the exact same way?" The same interview pairs the harm-prevention framing with the structural argument she names as the founding rationale of [Oversight Lab Africa](../organizations/org-oversight-lab-africa.md): "It's not just one person being harmed but entire communities, [so] the response that is necessary is not reactive but, rather, systemic. And that was the reasoning behind coming up with The Oversight Lab." The formulation is the corpus's clearest single statement of the move from case-by-case strategic litigation into standing institutional advocacy, and it is the framing through which Mutemi has positioned the Oversight Lab — and herself, as its founder and executive director — as the standing African-led response to systemic Big-Tech harm, complementary to and not contained within the case-anchored work of the law firm she also runs.

## Public output and venues

Mutemi's public-facing work runs across four overlapping channels.

- **Counsel-of-record on the Foxglove / Nzili & Sumbi Kenya docket.** Mutemi is the named counsel of record across the [Motaung lawsuit against Meta and Sama](../campaigns/camp-foxglove-motaung-meta-sama-kenya.md), the [185 former Facebook content moderators' suit](../campaigns/camp-foxglove-185-moderators-meta-kenya.md), the [Mutemi / Meta / Meareg & Tekle Tigray hate-speech petition](../campaigns/camp-mutemi-meta-meareg-tekle-tigray.md), and the [Mutemi / Mathenge, Okinyi, Kairu and Mulinya parliamentary petition against OpenAI and Sama](../campaigns/camp-mutemi-chatgpt-sama-parliamentary-petition.md) — work she runs from her [self-described "Kenyan counsel"](https://www.foxglove.org.uk/2024/09/23/facebook-content-moderators-kenya-meta-appeal/) position alongside [Foxglove](../organizations/org-foxglove.md). The procedural arc she has carried the docket through — Meta named as a "proper party" in Kenya; the 20 September 2024 Court of Appeal ruling; the 26 May 2025 consolidation of the two Meta petitions before Justice Mathews Nduma Nderi — is the substantive procedural achievement on which the rest of her public output rests, and the single body of work most frequently cited by other commentators when naming the African strategic-litigation field's distinguishing contribution.
- **The Oversight Lab as standing institutional vehicle.** Since founding [Oversight Lab Africa](../organizations/org-oversight-lab-africa.md) in mid-2025, Mutemi has been the organisation's named external voice — quoted on the [6 March 2026 ODPC petition over Meta's Ray-Ban smart glasses](../campaigns/camp-oversight-lab-meta-ray-ban-glasses-2026.md) ("we are deeply concerned by the development of harmful technology through exploitation of vulnerable communities"), on the [Oversight Lab's response to the April 2026 Sama redundancies](https://weetracker.com/2026/04/30/kenyan-workers-meta-smart-glasses-sex-layoffs/) ("our current strategies are harming our youth, hurting our economy and in no way advance Kenya's participation in the AI ecosystem"), and on the [English-language bias of AI content-moderation systems across Africa's 2,000+ languages](https://globalvoices.org/2026/04/20/africa-has-2000-languages-ai-content-moderation-covers-fewer-than-20/). The Oversight Lab is the channel through which her named-spokesperson voice reaches publics — Kenyan content moderators, ride-hailing drivers, data labellers, and members of the public captured by ambient surveillance devices — that the case-anchored work of [Nzili & Sumbi Advocates](../organizations/org-nzili-sumbi-advocates.md) reaches only through individual plaintiffs.
- **Named press profiles and international recognition.** The [Coda Story long-read](https://www.codastory.com/authoritarian-tech/mercy-mutemi-meta-lawsuit/) and the [Ford Foundation interview](https://www.fordfoundation.org/news-and-stories/stories/when-technology-meets-justice-nettrice-gaskins-and-mercy-mutemi-on-building-equitable-digital-futures/) are the two most substantive single profiles on the public record, and the source of the strongest direct quotes through which her signature framings have been delivered. The [TIME100 Next 2023 listing](https://www.codastory.com/authoritarian-tech/mercy-mutemi-meta-lawsuit/), the [Business Daily Top 40 Under 40 2023 profile](https://top40.businessdailyafrica.com/candidates/mercy-mutemi/), and the [2025 Ford Global Fellow recognition](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/ford-foundation-announces-30-new-members-of-ford-global-fellowship-302418390.html) are the most-cited international and Kenyan-press recognitions of her work, and the venues through which her name is reliably introduced in subsequent reporting; the [International Bar Association feature](https://www.ibanet.org/You-cant-outsource-responsibility) ("You can't outsource responsibility") is the single most comprehensive cross-jurisdictional legal-press inventory of her docket and her own framing of the practice as a movement vehicle.
- **International convening and named conference appearances.** The single most-cited public-platform appearance is the [June 2022 King's Place London panel](https://time.com/6188272/frances-haugen-daniel-motaung-facebook-whistleblowers/) convened by [Foxglove](../organizations/org-foxglove.md), at which Mutemi appeared alongside [Daniel Motaung](../persons/person-daniel-motaung.md), Frances Haugen, and [Cori Crider](../persons/person-cori-crider.md) — the founding public moment of the public-facing campaign on the Motaung case and the venue at which the Foxglove / Nzili & Sumbi public-facing posture was first introduced to a UK and US press audience. Her [re:publica 2024 speaker profile](https://re-publica.com/en/news/rp24-speaker-mercy-mutemi-shaping-africas-digital-future-through-litigation) — "Shaping Africa's digital future through litigation" — anchors her appearance at one of Continental Europe's largest digital-rights gatherings and is the venue at which the docket has been put before a Continental European civil-society audience.

## Why this is a Voice entry

A Voice entry is created here, rather than additional structure on the [Person entry](../persons/person-mercy-mutemi.md), because Mutemi's public-facing output is itself the load-bearing object the corpus needs to track: the working international vocabulary of African strategic litigation against Big Tech — the [extraction / "use and dump" framing](https://www.codastory.com/authoritarian-tech/mercy-mutemi-meta-lawsuit/), the [jurisdictional-cover-for-escaping-liability framing](https://www.codastory.com/authoritarian-tech/mercy-mutemi-meta-lawsuit/), and the [harm-prevention / systemic-not-reactive framing](https://www.fordfoundation.org/news-and-stories/stories/when-technology-meets-justice-nettrice-gaskins-and-mercy-mutemi-on-building-equitable-digital-futures/) that anchored the founding of [Oversight Lab Africa](../organizations/org-oversight-lab-africa.md) — is the framing she has installed into Kenyan, UK, and international press coverage of AI labour, surveillance, and platform-accountability litigation across the four-year arc of the docket since 2022. The corpus's African strategic-litigation Voice slot — the lawyer-and-institution-builder pole structurally complementary to the worker-plaintiff side anchored in [voice-daniel-motaung](voice-daniel-motaung.md) and to the UK / Global-North convener-strategist side carried by [Cori Crider](../persons/person-cori-crider.md) at [Foxglove](../organizations/org-foxglove.md) — carried no African Voice before this entry; this entry gives that pole its first first-person voice. Affiliation, prior employment, and biographical structure are recorded on the linked [Person entry](../persons/person-mercy-mutemi.md) per the corpus's Person/Voice split.
