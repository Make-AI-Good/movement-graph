# Cathy O'Neil

Cathy O'Neil is the [American mathematician and CEO of ORCAA (O'Neil Risk Consulting and Algorithmic Auditing)](https://cathyoneil.org/) — the algorithmic-auditing practice operationalising the argument of [*Weapons of Math Destruction*](../publications/pub-weapons-of-math-destruction.md) into working compliance infrastructure — based in Cambridge and Somerville, Massachusetts (see [Person entry](../persons/person-cathy-oneil.md)). She is tracked here as a Voice because her sustained named public output — [*Weapons of Math Destruction*](../publications/pub-weapons-of-math-destruction.md) (Crown, 2016) and [*The Shame Machine*](https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/606203/the-shame-machine-by-cathy-oneil/) (Crown, 2022); the mathbabe.org blog and Bloomberg View column that have been her continuous public-register channels; the ORCAA auditing practice translating the books' arguments into auditable standards; and the policy-influence register running through her [November 2023 Senate AI Insight Forum testimony](https://www.schumer.senate.gov/download/cathy-oneil_-statement?download=1) and ORCAA's [inaugural membership in the US AI Safety Institute Consortium](https://orcaarisk.com/in-the-news) — occupies a register the corpus's other algorithmic-accountability voices leave empty: the mathematician-practitioner's critique, built on the authority of someone who produced quantitative models at D. E. Shaw & Co. and then turned that insider expertise toward naming the harms those models cause.

## Voice anchor

The Voice closes the US data-scientist-mathematician algorithmic-critique public-voice slot. Three distinctions from adjacent voices already in the corpus:

- **Distinct from Joy Buolamwini's Voice.** [Buolamwini](../persons/person-joy-buolamwini.md) anchors the algorithmic-bias-audit register — the *Gender Shades* experimental-audit lineage and the [Algorithmic Justice League](../organizations/org-algorithmic-justice-league.md)'s advocacy practice around facial recognition. O'Neil's anchor is the applied-practitioner register: the quantitative analyst who built financial models at D. E. Shaw & Co., then carried that insider credential into naming the systematic harms of algorithmic decision-making across hiring, credit, criminal justice, and social media, and then operationalised the critique as a commercial auditing practice servicing regulators, public agencies, and private firms. O'Neil's is the practitioner-economist's critique; Buolamwini's is the civil-society-advocate's audit.
- **Distinct from Safiya Noble's Voice.** [Noble](../persons/person-safiya-noble.md) anchors the library and information science critical-information-studies register — the LIS-grounded empirical account of how knowledge-organisation and search infrastructure encode racial and gender hierarchy, rooted in the humanities and social sciences. O'Neil's register is the applied data scientist's: the case-study-driven practitioner account of how quantitative models fail in specific high-stakes deployment contexts (teacher evaluation, recidivism prediction, credit scoring), drawn from industry knowledge rather than the LIS tradition.
- **Distinct from Kate Crawford's Voice.** [Crawford](../persons/person-kate-crawford.md) anchors the extractive and material AI-critique register — the planetary-costs, colonial-genealogy, and power-and-extraction analysis of AI's physical and institutional infrastructure. O'Neil's register is the applied-compliance layer: the auditor building the bias-testing and certification infrastructure, the regulator-facing testimony, and the legally-oriented compliance frameworks (NYC Local Law 144, NIST AI Risk Management Framework operationalisation) — the practical-accountability work downstream of the structural critique.

## Signature framings

Four framings recur across O'Neil's public output and have done the most work in the algorithmic-accountability movement's vocabulary.

- **The three WMD criteria — opacity, scale, damage.** The central contribution of [*Weapons of Math Destruction*](../publications/pub-weapons-of-math-destruction.md) is naming a class of algorithmic system by three criteria that together constitute a "weapon": [the model is opaque (hidden from those it affects and often from its operators), operates at scale (processing large populations so small biases compound into mass effects), and produces damage (those misclassified have no route to contest the classification)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Weapons_of_Math_Destruction). This tripartite taxonomy gave the algorithmic-accountability field its most widely-circulated non-technical diagnostic vocabulary — a framework a legislator, journalist, or organiser could apply without mathematical training, which is why the book reached audiences the purely academic literature did not.
- **"Models are opinions embedded in mathematics."** O'Neil's [most-propagated single formulation](https://www.goodreads.com/author/quotes/6928121.Cathy_O_Neil) — the short-form rebuttal to the claim that mathematical systems are inherently objective — has been carried across the algorithmic-accountability literature as the canonical counter to algorithmic-neutrality claims. The framing does the distinctive work of a mathematician saying it: the critique of mathematical objectivity lands differently when the person dismantling the claim built the kind of models being claimed as objective.
- **Practitioner-to-auditor: critique operationalised as a compliance service.** Where most of the corpus's algorithmic-accountability voices produce critique, research, or advocacy, O'Neil has pursued a specific next step: translating the critique into auditable standards that can be enforced in regulatory contexts. [ORCAA's Explainable Fairness methodology](https://orcaarisk.com/) — identify protected groups, specify outcomes of interest, measure and explain disparities — and its NYC Local Law 144 bias audit services are the commercial form of that operationalisation. The structural tension O'Neil names from this vantage is [the "really bad and destructive algorithms tend to be made by companies who would never hire me to audit them"](https://algorithmwatch.org/en/cathy-oneil-orcaa/), which is the argument for enforceable regulatory mandates rather than voluntary auditing markets. Her [Senate AI Insight Forum testimony](https://www.schumer.senate.gov/download/cathy-oneil_-statement?download=1) pressed this into the legislative register: regulators should specify "what it means for an algorithm to be compliant" in each deployment context, with audit as the enforcement mechanism rather than industry self-certification.
- **Shame as weaponised technology.** [*The Shame Machine*](https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/606203/the-shame-machine-by-cathy-oneil/) (Crown, 2022) extends the WMD analysis into a different register — not the quantitative model whose bias is measurable, but the recommendation algorithm and the healthcare and carceral system whose shared structure is to [shift institutional responsibility for social problems onto individuals through shame](https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/606203/the-shame-machine-by-cathy-oneil/). The book's specific contribution to the algorithmic-accountability vocabulary is naming recommendation algorithms as shame infrastructure: platforms whose engagement-maximising design surfaces shaming content because shaming produces the strong reactions that engagement signals reward — the WMD logic applied to the social layer rather than the decisional one.

## Public output and venues

O'Neil's named public-output channels run through four overlapping registers.

- **Books.** [*Weapons of Math Destruction*](../publications/pub-weapons-of-math-destruction.md) (Crown, September 2016) is the primary corpus anchor — a New York Times bestseller, [longlisted for the 2016 National Book Award for Nonfiction, winner of the Mathematical Association of America's 2019 Euler Book Prize](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Weapons_of_Math_Destruction), and named a best book of the year by The New York Times Book Review, The Boston Globe, Wired, Fortune, and The Guardian. [*The Shame Machine: Who Profits in the New Age of Humiliation*](https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/606203/the-shame-machine-by-cathy-oneil/) (Crown, March 2022) is O'Neil's [second trade book — New York Times Editors' Choice, named among the best books of the year by The Times (UK), 272 pages](https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/606203/the-shame-machine-by-cathy-oneil/) — extending the WMD analysis into shame economies. The earlier *Doing Data Science* (O'Reilly, 2013, co-authored with Rachel Schutt) was O'Neil's first book-length public output, aimed at practitioners learning applied data science.
- **Blog and opinion column.** The [mathbabe.org blog](https://mathbabe.org/) is O'Neil's continuous personal-publishing venue, active since the Occupy Wall Street period and the venue on which the WMD arguments were rehearsed across the years leading to the book. Her Bloomberg View opinion column was the named mainstream-press venue for extending the book's argument into current policy debates.
- **Policy testimony and standards work.** O'Neil's [November 2023 testimony at Senator Chuck Schumer's AI Insight Forum](https://www.schumer.senate.gov/download/cathy-oneil_-statement?download=1) — framing algorithmic auditing as "pre-flight testing and inspection to catch major issues before deployment" and calling for rule-based compliance definitions in each regulated context — is the most prominent named recent policy-audience appearance. Her [October 2023 NIST workshop talk on measuring AI risks](https://orcaarisk.com/in-the-news), alongside ORCAA's [February 2024 designation as an inaugural member of the US AI Safety Institute Consortium](https://orcaarisk.com/in-the-news), extends this into the federal AI-governance-standards infrastructure. The Summer 2024 [MIT Sloan Management Review article "Auditing Algorithmic Risk"](https://sloanreview.mit.edu/article/auditing-algorithmic-risk/) (co-authored with ORCAA's Sam Tyner-Monroe) is the most recent named peer-facing publication articulating ORCAA's auditing methodology.
- **Keynote speaking.** O'Neil is [represented as a keynote speaker by the Harry Walker Agency](https://www.harrywalker.com/speakers/cathy-oneil) and has spoken extensively on algorithmic accountability, big data ethics, and AI governance.

## Organisational vehicles

ORCAA (O'Neil Risk Consulting and Algorithmic Auditing) is O'Neil's primary organisational vehicle — the [algorithmic-auditing consulting practice](https://orcaarisk.com/) founded to operationalise [*Weapons of Math Destruction*](../publications/pub-weapons-of-math-destruction.md)'s argument into working compliance services. Its named [current service portfolio](https://orcaarisk.com/) spans Algorithmic Audits using the Ethical Matrix framework, the Explainable Fairness bias-testing methodology (identify protected groups → specify outcomes → measure and explain disparities), AI Governance + Risk Management infrastructure, real-time "Cockpit Design" monitoring, and education and training; clients span hiring, insurance, credit, education, and healthcare, including NYC Local Law 144 compliance work for automated hiring tools. ORCAA was [designated an inaugural member of the US AI Safety Institute Consortium in February 2024](https://orcaarisk.com/in-the-news), and [published a report for Uber on AI governance systems in December 2024](https://orcaarisk.com/in-the-news). O'Neil also serves as a board member of [OCEAN](https://cathyoneil.org/) — a public charity defending public interests against algorithmic misuse.

## Why this is a Voice entry

A Voice entry is created here, rather than additional structure on the [Person entry](../persons/person-cathy-oneil.md), because O'Neil's public-facing output is itself the load-bearing object the corpus needs to track: [*Weapons of Math Destruction*](../publications/pub-weapons-of-math-destruction.md) and the WMD three-criteria vocabulary it installed in algorithmic-accountability discourse; [*The Shame Machine*](https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/606203/the-shame-machine-by-cathy-oneil/) extending that analysis into the shame economy of recommendation algorithms; ORCAA's compliance infrastructure translating the critique into auditable standards; and the policy-influence register running through Senate testimony and NIST RMF operationalisation work. The corpus's algorithmic-accountability voices had coverage on the academic-critique side — [Noble](../persons/person-safiya-noble.md) on algorithmic oppression and search infrastructure, [Buolamwini](../persons/person-joy-buolamwini.md) on facial recognition bias, [Crawford](../persons/person-kate-crawford.md) on extractive material costs — but no voice occupying the practitioner-mathematician slot: the person who built the models, named the harm through a book that put the term *algorithmic accountability* into the popular lexicon, and then built the audit practice that operationalises the accountability claim into legally-enforceable compliance contexts. Affiliation, training, and biographical detail are recorded on the linked [Person entry](../persons/person-cathy-oneil.md) per the corpus's Person/Voice split.
