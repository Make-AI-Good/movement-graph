# Bastien Le Querrec

Bastien Le Querrec is the legal officer anchoring [La Quadrature du Net](../organizations/org-la-quadrature-du-net.md)'s litigation track against algorithmic systems in French public administration, and the principal French civil-society public voice on welfare-state AI accountability. He is tracked here as a Voice because his written public output — the LQDN campaign analyses framing the algorithmic-public-administration portfolio for French-language civil-society readers, the verbatim spokesperson role in the highest-profile French civil-society AI case currently before the administrative courts, and the ongoing Mastodon and journal-publication register carrying his own analytical framing beyond the LQDN institutional pipeline — has done more than any other individual's to install into French public discourse the argument that algorithmic welfare scoring and unemployment profiling are instruments of social control targeting the economically vulnerable, not neutral administrative tools (see [Person entry](../persons/person-bastien-le-querrec.md)).

## Public output and venues

Le Querrec's public-facing output runs through four overlapping channels.

**LQDN campaign blog and analysis.** The sustained written output on the LQDN blog is the headline register. The [June 2024 analysis "À France Travail, l'essor du contrôle algorithmique"](https://www.laquadrature.net/2024/06/25/a-france-travail-lessor-du-controle-algorithmique/) — dissecting the French unemployment agency's "anticipation of disengagement", "rupture risk", and "employability score" algorithmic-profiling systems — exemplifies the register: technical precision about scoring architecture combined with a civil-liberties argument that the categorisation logic is a political choice about who the welfare state treats as deserving, not an efficiency optimisation. This analysis runs alongside the parallel CAF case output and a continuing stream of LQDN posts on algorithmic public administration, platform regulation, and French digital rights.

**Spokesperson in the Conseil d'État filing.** The [16 October 2024 challenge to the CNAF family-benefits scoring system](https://www.laquadrature.net/en/2024/10/16/french-family-welfare-scoring-algorithm-challenged-in-court-by-15-organisations/) — a 15-organisation coalition filing to France's supreme administrative court arguing that the algorithmic fraud-risk scoring of approximately 13 million households violates GDPR Article 22 and anti-discrimination law — is the highest-profile case in which Le Querrec functions as the public spokesperson. His verbatim framing of the algorithm as "the manifestation of a policy of persecution of the poorest people — because you are in an economically vulnerable position, you will be suspect in the eyes of the algorithm, and therefore checked — it's a double punishment" is the single most widely circulated civil-society counter-framing on the case. That formulation has appeared in French and English-language coverage as the human-rights argument against the CNAF system, translating a complex Article 22 challenge into a moral claim legible outside the courtroom.

**Academic and journal publication.** Le Querrec's 2023 article in [*Après-demain*](https://shs.cairn.info/publications-de-bastien-le-querrec--765495?lang=fr) — a French public-affairs journal — on the failure of centralised social networks and the case for federated alternatives anchors the scholarly register alongside the litigation work. The Cairn.info author page's institutional description — "juriste en droit public et membre de La Quadrature du Net depuis 2019, travaille notamment dans le groupe contentieux de cette association" — carries both registers on the same anchor. His PhD programme at Université Grenoble-Alpes on algorithmic decision-making by public-administration agencies and its human-rights impacts is the long-horizon scholarly formation from which the LQDN litigation work draws its analytical depth.

**Mastodon commentary.** Le Querrec's [Mastodon account](https://mastodon.social/@blequerrec), where he identifies as "Legal officer at La Quadrature du Net", is the real-time public-commentary register extending beyond the formal LQDN publication pipeline. His 2024 focus areas there — platform interoperability as a decentralisation lever, encryption rights, algorithmic-control litigation, digital ecology — sketch a programmatic posture on digital rights that runs through the litigation work but is not contained by it.

## Signature framing

Le Querrec's most widely circulated public framing is the "policy of persecution" characterisation of the CNAF welfare-scoring algorithm. The formulation does two things simultaneously: it names the algorithmic profiling of welfare recipients as a political choice rather than a technical one, and it frames the economic-precarity-as-suspicion logic of the scoring system — the selection mechanism that disproportionately flags the poor for anti-fraud inspection — as a structurally discriminatory feature that makes the case a human-rights claim rather than an administrative-law technicality. The framing is also the primary public vehicle for the Article 22 GDPR argument that individuals should not be subject to consequential automated decisions without adequate recourse — a legal claim that, in Le Querrec's hands, becomes legible outside the courtroom as a proposition about what kind of welfare state France is choosing to build.
