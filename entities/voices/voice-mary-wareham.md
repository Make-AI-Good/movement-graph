# Mary Wareham

Mary Wareham is the longest-running civil-society voice on the case against lethal autonomous weapons and the founding global coordinator of [Stop Killer Robots](../organizations/org-stop-killer-robots.md) from the coalition's [2012 founding through March 2021](https://www.stopkillerrobots.org/news/passing-the-baton/) (see [Person entry](../persons/person-mary-wareham.md)). She is tracked here as a Voice because her sustained on-record output — HRW op-eds and reports, a long-running [Just Security column](https://www.justsecurity.org/author/warehammary/), UN statements at the Convention on Certain Conventional Weapons in Geneva and at the UN General Assembly First Committee in New York, congressional briefings, the [Stay in Command](https://www.audible.com/pd/Killer-Robots-Mary-Wareham-at-Human-Rights-Watch-Podcast/B08KKX3V32) podcast series at HRW, and the working speakership of the Stop Killer Robots coalition through its first decade — has done more than any single individual's to install into multilateral, parliamentary, and mainstream-press discourse the framing that the question of lethal autonomous weapons is fundamentally about removing human control from the use of force.

She is the corpus's first humanitarian-disarmament Voice and the first Voice anchored in the [autonomous-weapons / military-AI](../organizations/org-stop-killer-robots.md) movement area. She sits at the longest-running civil-society edge of the make-AI-good landscape: the question of whether and how machines should ever apply lethal force without a human in the loop is the field's most concrete site of treaty advocacy, and the [Stop Killer Robots](../organizations/org-stop-killer-robots.md) coalition's organising lineage runs through the same humanitarian-disarmament tradition that produced the Mine Ban Treaty (1997) and the Convention on Cluster Munitions (2008) — both campaigns Wareham was personally and centrally involved in, with [the 1997 Nobel Peace Prize on her record as joint recipient](https://europeanleadershipnetwork.org/person/mary-wareham/) through the International Campaign to Ban Landmines.

## Signature framings

Three framings in Wareham's public output have travelled beyond the campaign's own organising materials and into the working language used by states, UN bodies, and the mainstream press.

- **"Removing human control from the use of force."** This is the single most-cited Wareham line and the framing under which the campaign's entire treaty-advocacy posture operates. In the [January 2024 HRW dispatch](https://www.hrw.org/news/2024/01/03/killer-robots-un-vote-should-spur-action-treaty) on the UN First Committee resolution she frames the resolution as addressing "the urgent need for the international community to deal with the dangers raised by removing human control from the use of force", and in the [December 2024 HRW coverage](https://www.hrw.org/news/2024/12/05/killer-robots-un-vote-should-spur-treaty-negotiations) of UNGA Resolution 79/62 she names "fundamental concerns that come with removing human control from the use of force" as the substance the resolution's "wide and growing state support" is responding to. The line has been deliberately tuned to map onto the working diplomatic vocabulary of "meaningful human control" that has anchored the coalition's preferred treaty text.
- **"A future of automated killing that needs to be stopped."** Wareham's framing of the urgency is consistently temporal: that the threat is emergent and the regulatory window is closing. In the [January 2024 dispatch](https://www.hrw.org/news/2024/01/03/killer-robots-un-vote-should-spur-action-treaty) she calls it "a future of automated killing that needs to be stopped"; in the [March 2023 Just Security piece](https://www.justsecurity.org/86063/latin-america-and-caribbean-nations-rally-against-autonomous-weapons-systems/) co-authored with [Bonnie Docherty](../persons/person-bonnie-docherty.md) she and Docherty close with "after a decade of debate, it is urgent to start drafting new legally binding rules to prevent the automation of killing". The framing is what allowed the campaign to make the case that a preemptive ban — regulating weapons not yet fully deployed — was both possible and necessary.
- **"Preemptive ban" / treaty-driven humanitarian disarmament.** The campaign's structural argument from its 2013 launch onwards has been that the autonomous-weapons file is the next item in the humanitarian-disarmament tradition that produced the [Mine Ban Treaty (1997)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ottawa_Treaty) and the [Convention on Cluster Munitions (2008)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Convention_on_Cluster_Munitions). Wareham's [2014 article in *Revue internationale et stratégique*](https://shs.cairn.info/article/E_RIS_096_0097?lang=en) is the earliest sustained published statement of this case, and her [Just Security 2015 piece](https://www.justsecurity.org/author/warehammary/) "US Needs to Stop Tiptoeing Around the 'Killer Robots' Threat" carries the working argument into US foreign-policy discourse. The frame has been load-bearing on the campaign's strategic shift, captured in the [March 2023 Belén Communiqué piece](https://www.justsecurity.org/86063/latin-america-and-caribbean-nations-rally-against-autonomous-weapons-systems/), of moving the file out of the consensus-blocked CCW track and into the [UN General Assembly First Committee](../organizations/org-stop-killer-robots.md) track that produced [the November 2023 164-state vote](https://www.hrw.org/news/2024/01/03/killer-robots-un-vote-should-spur-action-treaty).

## Public output and venues

Wareham's public-facing work spans four overlapping channels.

- **HRW Arms Division op-eds, dispatches, and reports.** Wareham's authored output for [Human Rights Watch](../organizations/org-human-rights-watch.md) sits at the centre of her public corpus. The August 2020 report [*Stopping Killer Robots: Country Positions on Banning Fully Autonomous Weapons and Retaining Human Control*](https://www.hrw.org/report/2020/08/10/stopping-killer-robots/country-positions-banning-fully-autonomous-weapons-and) is the most-cited single Wareham-authored campaign artefact, providing the country-by-country negotiating-position map on which subsequent UN cycles were planned. From 2024 onwards her output has been concentrated in a steady run of HRW dispatches around each UN cycle — including the [January 2024 First Committee dispatch](https://www.hrw.org/news/2024/01/03/killer-robots-un-vote-should-spur-action-treaty), the [December 2024 UNGA Resolution 79/62 dispatch](https://www.hrw.org/news/2024/12/05/killer-robots-un-vote-should-spur-treaty-negotiations), and the [May 2025 informal-consultations dispatch](https://www.hrw.org/news/2025/05/21/un-start-talks-treaty-ban-killer-robots) — and a parallel cluster on landmines and cluster munitions that uses the same humanitarian-disarmament register.
- **Just Security column.** Wareham has been a [recurring Just Security author since 2014](https://www.justsecurity.org/author/warehammary/), with the column functioning as her primary outside-HRW long-form venue. The most-cited piece is the [March 2023 "Latin America and Caribbean Nations Rally Against Autonomous Weapons Systems"](https://www.justsecurity.org/86063/latin-america-and-caribbean-nations-rally-against-autonomous-weapons-systems/), co-authored with HRW colleague [Bonnie Docherty](../persons/person-bonnie-docherty.md), that framed the [Belén Communiqué](https://www.justsecurity.org/86063/latin-america-and-caribbean-nations-rally-against-autonomous-weapons-systems/) as the regional-bloc commitment that broke the multilateral logjam. Earlier pieces include the May 2015 "US Needs to Stop Tiptoeing Around the 'Killer Robots' Threat" and the September 2015 "A Weapon That Keeps on Killing" on landmines.
- **UN and parliamentary advocacy as named spokesperson.** From the campaign's [global launch in April 2013](https://www.stopkillerrobots.org/news/passing-the-baton/) through her March 2021 step-down, Wareham was the named civil-society interlocutor of the [Convention on Certain Conventional Weapons](https://www.stopkillerrobots.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/09/KRC_StatementCCW_28June2019_Delivered.pdf) cycle in Geneva, of the UN General Assembly First Committee cycle in New York, and of [congressional briefings in Washington](https://thehill.com/business-a-lobbying/lobbyist-profiles/376851-fighting-the-rise-of-the-machines) — she held the first congressional briefing on the topic in 2014. Even after the [March 2021 handover](https://www.stopkillerrobots.org/news/passing-the-baton/) to the coalition's dedicated secretariat, she has continued as a named [Stop Killer Robots spokesperson](https://www.stopkillerrobots.org/spokespersons/) and as HRW's working public face at each UN cycle.
- **Podcasts, press, and academic venues.** Wareham anchors the [*Stay in Command* podcast series](https://www.audible.com/pd/Killer-Robots-Mary-Wareham-at-Human-Rights-Watch-Podcast/B08KKX3V32) produced through HRW, has appeared on the [Future of Life Institute podcast](https://futureoflife.org/2018/07/31/podcast-six-experts-explain-the-killer-robots-debate/) (July 2018) and other long-form interviews, and is the named subject of the [March 2018 *The Hill* lobbyist profile](https://thehill.com/business-a-lobbying/lobbyist-profiles/376851-fighting-the-rise-of-the-machines) "Fighting the rise of the machines". Her [2014 *Revue internationale et stratégique* article](https://shs.cairn.info/article/E_RIS_096_0097?lang=en) is her sole single-authored peer-style academic piece and the earliest sustained published statement of the campaign's case.

## Organisational vehicle

Wareham's public output runs through two named organisational vehicles. [Human Rights Watch](../organizations/org-human-rights-watch.md)'s Arms Division — where she joined as senior advocate in 1998, served as advocacy director from the early 2010s, and is now [Deputy Crisis, Conflict and Arms Director](https://www.hrw.org/about/people/mary-wareham) — has been her continuous employer since 1998 (apart from a 2006–2008 Oxfam New Zealand stint that produced her contribution to the Convention on Cluster Munitions). [Stop Killer Robots](../organizations/org-stop-killer-robots.md), the coalition she founded-coordinated from 2013 through [March 2021](https://www.stopkillerrobots.org/news/passing-the-baton/), is the campaign vehicle inside which her decade of autonomous-weapons advocacy was structured; after the handover she has continued as a named [coalition spokesperson](https://www.stopkillerrobots.org/spokespersons/) while the day-to-day coordination passed to the dedicated Geneva secretariat.

## Why this is a Voice entry

A Voice entry is created here, rather than additional structure on the [Person entry](../persons/person-mary-wareham.md), because Wareham's public output is itself the load-bearing object the corpus needs to track: the working civil-society framing of the entire autonomous-weapons / military-AI movement area — "removing human control from the use of force", "a future of automated killing", "preemptive ban", and the inheritance of the [Mine Ban Treaty / Convention on Cluster Munitions](../organizations/org-stop-killer-robots.md) tradition — is the language she installed into multilateral, parliamentary, and mainstream-press discourse over more than a decade as Stop Killer Robots' founding global coordinator and as the [HRW Arms Division's](../organizations/org-human-rights-watch.md) named public face. The corpus's [Stop Killer Robots cluster](../organizations/org-stop-killer-robots.md) — its single deepest org / event / campaign thread, and the longest-running humanitarian-disarmament strand inside the AI-good landscape — carries no other Voice anchor; this entry gives that cluster its first first-person voice. Affiliation and biographical structure are recorded on the linked [Person entry](../persons/person-mary-wareham.md) per the corpus's Person/Voice split.
