# Silkie Carlo

Silkie Carlo is the [Director](https://bigbrotherwatch.org.uk/team/) of [Big Brother Watch](../organizations/org-big-brother-watch.md) since January 2018, the London-based civil-liberties organisation that has become the British anchor of the European civil-society campaign against live facial recognition (see [Person entry](../persons/person-silkie-carlo.md)). She is tracked here as a Voice because her sustained named-spokesperson public output — the [May 2018 *Face Off*](https://bigbrotherwatch.org.uk/wp-content/uploads/2018/05/Face-Off-final-digital-1.pdf) report on the Metropolitan Police's automated facial-recognition trials; the [May 2023 *Biometric Britain*](https://bigbrotherwatch.org.uk/2023/05/new-report-says-government-should-urgently-stop-live-facial-recognition-in-the-uk/) report on the 89%-wrong-match rate across the Metropolitan Police and South Wales Police operational deployments; the [6 October 2023 cross-party Joint Statement](https://bigbrotherwatch.org.uk/press-releases/65-parliamentarians-call-for-immediate-stop-to-live-facial-recognition-surveillance/) signed by 65 parliamentarians and 31 rights and race-equality organisations; the named co-claimant role alongside [Shaun Thompson](https://bigbrotherwatch.org.uk/campaigns/stop-facial-recognition/) in the resulting legal challenge against the Metropolitan Police, through to the [21 April 2026 High Court judgment](https://bigbrotherwatch.org.uk/press-releases/responding-to-live-facial-recognition-judgment/) and the announced appeal; the sustained [named-byline output](https://bigbrotherwatch.org.uk/author/silkie-carlo/) on Big Brother Watch's own publishing channel; the [*Coded Bias* (2020)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silkie_Carlo) and [*2073* (2024)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silkie_Carlo) documentary-appearance register; the recurring named-spokesperson register in *The Times* and the British mainstream press; and the sustained long-form podcast register including [*The Hive*](https://www.nathalienahai.com/4-surveillance-and-the-state-silkie-carlo/), [TalkRadio](https://bigbrotherwatch.org.uk/videos/extended-interview-with-director-silkie-carlo-on-talkradio-about-the-state-of-online-surveillance/), and [*TRIGGERnometry*](https://podtail.com/podcast/triggernometry/your-government-is-spying-on-you-silkie-carlo/) — carries the working frame that live facial recognition in publicly accessible spaces is the AI-policy file on which British civil liberties stand or fall, that the AI Act question is at its core a question about whether the state and the private sector may identify members of the public in real time without consent, and that the parliamentary-lobby, strategic-litigation, investigations, and public-campaigns surfaces are best run as integrated tracks rather than as separate registers.

The Voice anchors three movement-area registers that the corpus's voices slice had previously left underweight.

- **The UK director-and-investigator named voice anchor on live facial recognition and biometric mass surveillance.** Structurally distinct from the corpus's existing UK voice anchors — [Reema Patel](voice-reema-patel.md) (Ada Lovelace Institute / participatory-data-stewardship register), [Andrea Miotti](voice-andrea-miotti.md) (ControlAI / AI-safety-pause federation register), and [Cori Crider](voice-cori-crider.md) (Foxglove / strategic-litigation register) — Carlo's distinctive register is the director-and-investigator of a single dedicated civil-liberties NGO whose named-spokesperson output runs as the British civil-society reference voice on live facial recognition in publicly accessible spaces. The integrated four-pillar mode the org [describes](https://bigbrotherwatch.org.uk/team/) as "parliamentary lobbying, strategic litigation, investigations and public campaigns to successfully change policies and laws" sits structurally beside Crider's strategic-litigation-led register at Foxglove rather than substituting for it. The Voice closes the UK biometric-mass-surveillance public-voice register that the corpus had previously left without a named British anchor and complements the corpus's pan-European biometric-mass-surveillance public voice through [Ella Jakubowska](voice-ella-jakubowska.md) on the EDRi Brussels-secretariat side and [Katarzyna Szymielewicz](voice-katarzyna-szymielewicz.md) on the [Panoptykon Foundation](../organizations/org-panoptykon-foundation.md) Polish national-civil-society side.
- **The pre-AI-Act journalism-information-security and post-Snowden register.** Carlo's voice carries a register that pre-dates the AI Act civil-society conversation: her July 2014 [co-authorship with Arjen Kamphuis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silkie_Carlo) of *Information Security for Journalists* (commissioned by the Centre for Investigative Journalism in the aftermath of the [Edward Snowden disclosures](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silkie_Carlo)); her work on [Edward Snowden's official legal defence fund at the Courage Foundation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silkie_Carlo); and her organising of the [CryptoPartyLDN](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silkie_Carlo) series of public information-security workshops, alongside CryptoParties across the UK and Europe, since 2013. The journalism-information-security side anchors the substantive case that the live-facial-recognition file is best understood inside a wider mass-surveillance lineage running through the post-Snowden disclosures, the Investigatory Powers Act 2016 legal challenge that Carlo led at Liberty as Senior Advocacy Officer for Technology and Human Rights, and the bulk-interception case-law that Big Brother Watch under her later directorship took through the [European Court of Human Rights](https://hudoc.echr.coe.int/eng?i=002-12080).
- **The documentary-film and named-spokesperson mainstream-press register on biometric mass surveillance.** Carlo's named appearance in [*Coded Bias* (2020)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silkie_Carlo) — the Shalini Kantayya-directed Netflix documentary on the social and racial bias of automated facial recognition systems that featured the work of [Joy Buolamwini](voice-joy-buolamwini.md) and the [Algorithmic Justice League](../organizations/org-algorithmic-justice-league.md) — and in [*2073* (2024)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silkie_Carlo) — Asif Kapadia's documentary-fiction hybrid on the trajectory of authoritarian mass-surveillance — places her on a small list of corpus Voices who anchor the documentary-film register on biometric mass surveillance. The mainstream-press register, anchored on Carlo's verbatim [*Orwellian street surveillance*](https://bigbrotherwatch.org.uk/facialrec/the-times-live-facial-recognition-could-enable-orwellian-street-surveillance-campaigners-say/) framing in *The Times* and her recurring named-spokesperson commentary in *The Guardian*, *The Telegraph*, *Sky News*, *BBC*, and the British and international press across the live-facial-recognition, online-safety, predictive-policing, and digital-ID files, anchors the on-the-record reach through which Big Brother Watch's under-her-directorship campaign positions enter the British general-readership public conversation.

## Public output and venues

Carlo's public-facing work runs through four overlapping channels.

- **Named lead-spokesperson voice on Big Brother Watch's investigative-research and campaign-statement archive.** The most load-bearing channel of Carlo's voice is her named lead-spokesperson role on Big Brother Watch's own investigative-research and campaign-statement archive — anchored on the [May 2018 *Face Off*](https://bigbrotherwatch.org.uk/wp-content/uploads/2018/05/Face-Off-final-digital-1.pdf) report on the "less than 2 per cent accuracy" finding on the Metropolitan Police's automated facial-recognition trials and on the [May 2023 *Biometric Britain*](https://bigbrotherwatch.org.uk/2023/05/new-report-says-government-should-urgently-stop-live-facial-recognition-in-the-uk/) report on the 89%-wrong-match rate across the Metropolitan Police and South Wales Police operational deployments, the Home Office £50 million centralised facial-matching platform, and the corpus's clearest single artefact of the UK biometric-mass-surveillance investigative-research register. Her verbatim 23 May 2023 *Biometric Britain* launch framing — that UK live facial recognition puts "Britain at great risk of following in the footsteps of China and Russia's high-tech surveillance states, with no democratic mandate" — is the campaign's principal on-the-record framing of the divergence between the UK's operational deployment trajectory and the European post-AI-Act constraint trajectory. The named-byline archive at [bigbrotherwatch.org.uk/author/silkie-carlo](https://bigbrotherwatch.org.uk/author/silkie-carlo/) records the sustained authoring register through which Carlo's voice carries Big Brother Watch's director-level line on the live-facial-recognition, online-safety, predictive-policing, and digital-ID files.
- **Parliamentary-lobby spokesperson register through the cross-party Joint Statement and the JHA Committee inquiry.** Carlo is the named convener-spokesperson on the [6 October 2023 cross-party Joint Statement on UK Live Facial Recognition](https://bigbrotherwatch.org.uk/press-releases/65-parliamentarians-call-for-immediate-stop-to-live-facial-recognition-surveillance/) signed by 65 parliamentarians — including former Cabinet ministers, the Mayor of London, and members of the SNP, Liberal Democrat, Green, and Conservative parties — and 31 rights and race-equality organisations including [Foxglove](../organizations/org-foxglove.md), the [Joint Council for the Welfare of Immigrants](../organizations/org-jcwi.md), Liberty, [Privacy International](../organizations/org-privacy-international.md), Open Rights Group, Amnesty International, [Access Now](../organizations/org-access-now.md), Article 19, Index on Censorship, and [Human Rights Watch](../organizations/org-human-rights-watch.md). The verbatim Carlo framing of the statement as "the greatest involvement parliamentarians have ever had in Britain's approach to facial recognition surveillance" anchors the cross-party-parliamentary-lobby register of the campaign's on-record convening voice. The same parliamentary-lobby register runs through Big Brother Watch's written evidence to the [UK Parliament Justice and Home Affairs Committee inquiry into Live Facial Recognition](https://committees.parliament.uk/work/8031/live-facial-recognition/) — the corpus's most load-bearing UK parliamentary inquiry on the substantive question of whether live facial recognition in publicly accessible spaces is lawful at all.
- **Strategic-litigation co-claimant register through the Met Police legal challenge.** The most visible British-civil-society-audience-facing surface of Carlo's voice is her named co-claimant role alongside [Shaun Thompson](https://bigbrotherwatch.org.uk/campaigns/stop-facial-recognition/) — the Black anti-knife-crime community volunteer misidentified by Metropolitan Police live facial recognition at London Bridge in February 2024 — in the resulting legal challenge against the Metropolitan Police, the corpus's most visible UK live-facial-recognition test case. The challenge was heard at the High Court in early 2026 and judgment was handed down on [21 April 2026](https://bigbrotherwatch.org.uk/press-releases/responding-to-live-facial-recognition-judgment/), with the Court dismissing the claim; Carlo's named on-the-record framing of the judgment as "a deeply disappointing decision that risks turning Britain into a checkpoint society" anchored Big Brother Watch's announcement of the appeal. The strategic-litigation register sits alongside the parliamentary-lobby register that runs through the JHA Committee inquiry and the cross-party Joint Statement — anchoring the inside-outside-game pair that the [Stop Facial Recognition](https://bigbrotherwatch.org.uk/campaigns/stop-facial-recognition/) campaign under Carlo's directorship runs on the UK national surface.
- **Documentary-film, mainstream-press, and long-form podcast register.** Carlo's documentary-film register anchors on her named appearance in [*Coded Bias* (2020)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silkie_Carlo) — the Shalini Kantayya-directed Netflix documentary on the social and racial bias of automated facial recognition systems — and in [*2073* (2024)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silkie_Carlo), the Asif Kapadia documentary-fiction hybrid on authoritarian mass surveillance. The British and international mainstream-press register runs most visibly through the verbatim ["Orwellian street surveillance"](https://bigbrotherwatch.org.uk/facialrec/the-times-live-facial-recognition-could-enable-orwellian-street-surveillance-campaigners-say/) framing carried in *The Times* and recurring named-spokesperson commentary in the British general-readership and policy-readership press across the live-facial-recognition, online-safety, predictive-policing, and digital-ID files. The long-form podcast register runs through [*The Hive* episode 4](https://www.nathalienahai.com/4-surveillance-and-the-state-silkie-carlo/) on surveillance and the state with Nathalie Nahai, the [extended TalkRadio interview](https://bigbrotherwatch.org.uk/videos/extended-interview-with-director-silkie-carlo-on-talkradio-about-the-state-of-online-surveillance/) on online surveillance, and the [*TRIGGERnometry* episode *Your Government is Spying on You*](https://podtail.com/podcast/triggernometry/your-government-is-spying-on-you-silkie-carlo/) — anchoring the long-form named-spokesperson register through which Carlo's voice carries Big Brother Watch's substantive surveillance-and-civil-liberties line into the wider British and international podcast field.

## Signature framings

Three formulations recur across Carlo's public output and have done the most to install her register into the British and European biometric-mass-surveillance public conversation.

- **"Following in the footsteps of China and Russia's high-tech surveillance states, with no democratic mandate."** Carlo's verbatim 23 May 2023 [*Biometric Britain*](https://bigbrotherwatch.org.uk/2023/05/new-report-says-government-should-urgently-stop-live-facial-recognition-in-the-uk/) launch framing — that UK live facial recognition puts Britain at risk of "following in the footsteps of China and Russia's high-tech surveillance states, with no democratic mandate" — is the corpus's clearest single articulation of the UK-divergence-from-Europe framing of the live-facial-recognition file. The framing refuses the framing widely used by Home Office and Metropolitan Police spokespeople that UK operational live facial recognition is a constrained, lawful, and democratically authorised tool, and reads the absence of statutory legal basis and the absence of parliamentary debate as the substantive content of the trajectory rather than as an oversight to be corrected. The framing carries through the campaign body as the [UK Stop Live Facial Recognition Coalition's](../campaigns/camp-bbw-uk-stop-live-facial-recognition-coalition-2023.md) reference public verdict.
- **"The most Orwellian street surveillance system this country has ever seen."** Carlo's verbatim framing in [*The Times*](https://bigbrotherwatch.org.uk/facialrec/the-times-live-facial-recognition-could-enable-orwellian-street-surveillance-campaigners-say/) — that "combined with mandatory ID cards, live facial recognition could enable the most Orwellian street surveillance system this country has ever seen" — and the paired framing that "the question the Home Office should really be asking is whether police should be spying on the public with live facial recognition cameras around the country at all" anchor the substantive critique that the live-facial-recognition file is best read as the leading edge of an integrated mass-surveillance infrastructure rather than as a discrete policing tool. The framing displaces the proportionality-and-safeguards framing of the Home Office and Metropolitan Police register with a substantive integrated-mass-surveillance framing that connects live facial recognition to mandatory digital-ID, predictive policing, and bulk-interception infrastructure.
- **"A deeply disappointing decision that risks turning Britain into a checkpoint society."** Carlo's named on-the-record framing of the [21 April 2026 High Court judgment](https://bigbrotherwatch.org.uk/press-releases/responding-to-live-facial-recognition-judgment/) in the Carlo and Thompson v. Commissioner of Police of the Metropolis challenge — that the dismissal of the claim is "a deeply disappointing decision that risks turning Britain into a checkpoint society" — anchors the substantive case that the unfavourable judgment is a transient setback inside a longer parliamentary-lobby, strategic-litigation, investigations, and public-campaigns trajectory rather than a closure of the file. The framing carries through into Big Brother Watch's announced appeal and into the named on-the-record convening voice that Carlo continues to run on the strategic-litigation surface alongside the parliamentary-lobby and public-campaigns surfaces of the [Stop Facial Recognition](https://bigbrotherwatch.org.uk/campaigns/stop-facial-recognition/) campaign.

## Organisational vehicle

Carlo's public output runs primarily through [Big Brother Watch](../organizations/org-big-brother-watch.md) — the London-based civil-liberties organisation, founded in 2009, where she has been [Director](https://bigbrotherwatch.org.uk/team/) since January 2018, leading the named portfolio on state surveillance, policing technology, big data, internet regulation, and AI. Her training — a [Politics and Psychology degree from the University of Cambridge](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silkie_Carlo) — anchors the academic background from which her pre-Big-Brother-Watch career as Senior Advocacy Officer for Technology and Human Rights at Liberty, her work on [Edward Snowden's Courage Foundation defence fund](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silkie_Carlo), and her co-authorship of *Information Security for Journalists* with Arjen Kamphuis (Centre for Investigative Journalism, July 2014) were built. Beyond Big Brother Watch's own publishing channels, her voice carries through the [Stop Facial Recognition](https://bigbrotherwatch.org.uk/campaigns/stop-facial-recognition/) campaign and the [UK Stop Live Facial Recognition Coalition](../campaigns/camp-bbw-uk-stop-live-facial-recognition-coalition-2023.md), the [Carlo and Thompson v.
