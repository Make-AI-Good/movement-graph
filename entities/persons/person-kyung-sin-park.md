# Kyung-sin Park

Korean lawyer and [Professor of Law at Korea University Law School](https://www.law.uci.edu/faculty/visiting/kspark/); [co-founder and Executive Director](https://globalfreedomofexpression.columbia.edu/about/experts/kyung-sin-park/) of [Open Net Korea](../organizations/org-open-net-korea.md) since its [4 February 2013 founding](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/OpenNet_(organization)). The organisation's principal public-facing legal advocate, widely referenced as **K.S. Park**, and the named author of the constitutional and policy critiques on which Open Net's recent AI work rests — including the [October 2025 constitutional complaint](https://www.opennetkorea.org/en/wp/7058) against Article 82-8(1) of the Public Official Election Act (the 90-day pre-election prohibition on AI-generated images, videos, and sounds for election campaigning) and the [Global Free Speech Summit critique of the Korean AI Basic Act](https://www.opennetkorea.org/en/wp/7061) as a structural opening to government censorship of "high impact AI".

Park holds an [A.B. in Physics from Harvard University (1992) and a J.D. from UCLA School of Law (1995)](https://www.law.uci.edu/faculty/visiting/kspark/). He was a [1995 Echoing Green Global Fellow](https://fellows.echoinggreen.org/fellow/kyung-sin-park/) with [Korean Immigrant Workers Advocates (KIWA)](https://fellows.echoinggreen.org/fellow/kyung-sin-park/) in Los Angeles, where the fellowship funded immigrant-worker organising and living-wage campaigning in Koreatown. He returned to Korea and [served as International Relations Counsel at the Korea Film Council from 2002 to 2007](https://www.law.uci.edu/faculty/visiting/kspark/), then led the People's Solidarity for Participatory Democracy (PSPD) Law Center as Executive Director — [the PSPD start date is recorded as 2006 on Park's GNI member profile and as 2008 on his UCI faculty page; sources differ](https://globalnetworkinitiative.org/member/ks-park/) — through the period in which the centre organised high-impact free-speech, privacy, and copyright litigation that fed directly into the founding-cohort agenda of Open Net.

At Korea University, Park [founded the Korea University Law Review and the Law Schools' Clinical Legal Education Center](https://www.law.uci.edu/faculty/visiting/kspark/) and spearheaded its `internetlawclinic.org` and `transparency.or.kr` programmes — the latter the centre's national transparency-reporting project on government surveillance and censorship requests. He is currently [Visiting Professor of Law at the University of California, Irvine School of Law](https://www.law.uci.edu/faculty/visiting/kspark/), holding the visiting position concurrently with his Korea University professorship and his Open Net executive directorship.

Park's Korean government and public-body service spans [Commissioner of the Korea Communications Standards Commission from 2011 to 2014](https://www.law.uci.edu/faculty/visiting/kspark/) — the presidentially appointed body that regulates internet content under Korean broadcasting and telecommunications law, and the body whose administrative censorship powers Open Net has [contested across the life of the organisation](https://ooni.org/partners/open-net-korea/) — and [membership of the Parliament-appointed National Media Commission in 2010](https://globalfreedomofexpression.columbia.edu/about/experts/kyung-sin-park/), the advisory body on newspaper-broadcasting co-ownership bans and other media and Internet regulations.

Internationally, Park [joined the Global Network Initiative in 2015](https://globalnetworkinitiative.org/member/ks-park/) and serves on its board, sits on the Advisory Network to the Freedom Online Coalition, and [served until 2020 on the High-Level Panel of Legal Experts on Media Freedom](https://www.law.uci.edu/faculty/visiting/kspark/) convened by the UK–Canada Media Freedom Coalition. He is a [named co-author of the Necessary and Proportionate Principles on Communications Surveillance and the Manila Principles on Intermediary Liability](https://globalfreedomofexpression.columbia.edu/about/experts/kyung-sin-park/), the two foundational international civil-society standards on government surveillance and platform liability that anchor Open Net's intermediary-liability and surveillance work and that have been adopted across the Association for Progressive Communications network in which Open Net sits.
