Eritrean-American computer scientist, [founder and Executive Director](https://www.dair-institute.org/team/) of the [Distributed AI Research Institute](../organizations/org-dair-institute.md) (DAIR), and a foundational voice of the algorithmic-accountability research field through her co-authorship of [*Gender Shades*](../publications/pub-gender-shades.md) with [Joy Buolamwini](../persons/person-joy-buolamwini.md) (2018) and of the 2021 "On the Dangers of Stochastic Parrots" paper with [Emily M. Bender, Angelina McMillan-Major, and Margaret Mitchell](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/On_the_Dangers_of_Stochastic_Parrots).

[Born in Addis Ababa to Eritrean parents and granted political asylum in the United States](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timnit_Gebru), Gebru holds a B.S., M.S., and Ph.D. (2017) in electrical engineering and computer vision from Stanford University, where she was advised by Fei-Fei Li. Before DAIR she worked as an Apple audio engineer (signal-processing work for the first iPad), as a postdoc at Microsoft Research's Fairness, Accountability, Transparency, and Ethics (FATE) in AI lab in 2017, and from 2018 until [her contested December 2020 departure](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/On_the_Dangers_of_Stochastic_Parrots) co-led Google's Ethical AI team with Margaret Mitchell, where the departure followed Google management's demand that she retract the Stochastic Parrots paper or remove Google-employee author names. She co-founded Black in AI with Rediet Abebe around 2016 as the principal Black-researcher affinity organisation at the NeurIPS conferences.

Gebru founded DAIR on [2 December 2021](https://www.dair-institute.org/press-release/) — the one-year anniversary of her Google exit — as the independent community-rooted research institute she had named as the appropriate home for algorithmic-accountability work that the in-house Big Tech research labs structurally could not support, with founding funding from the [Ford Foundation](../funders/fund-ford-foundation.md), the [John D. and Catherine T. MacArthur Foundation](../funders/fund-macarthur-foundation.md), the Kapor Center, the [Open Society Foundations](../funders/fund-open-society-foundations.md), and the Rockefeller Foundation. Her recognition record includes Fortune's World's 50 Greatest Leaders (2021), Nature's ten people who shaped science (2021), TIME 100 Most Influential People (2022), the Carnegie Corporation Great Immigrants Award (2023), and the BBC 100 Women list (2023).
