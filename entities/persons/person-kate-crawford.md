Scholar and artist-researcher working on the political economy and material infrastructure of artificial intelligence; [Senior Principal Researcher at Microsoft Research New York](https://katecrawford.net/atlas), [Research Professor at the University of Southern California](https://katecrawford.net/atlas), and [Director of the Knowing Machines Project](https://katecrawford.net/atlas) — the Sloan-Foundation-funded transatlantic research project tracing the histories and politics of how machine-learning systems are trained. [Co-founder and former director of research of the AI Now Institute at NYU](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kate_Crawford) and [inaugural Visiting Chair of AI and Justice at the École Normale Supérieure, Paris (2019)](https://katecrawford.net/atlas).

Author of [*Atlas of AI: Power, Politics, and the Planetary Costs of Artificial Intelligence*](../publications/pub-atlas-of-ai.md) (Yale University Press, 2021), the named book most often credited with moving the public AI conversation from abstract ethics frameworks toward a power-and-extraction analysis grounded in the minerals, labour, data, and energy the systems physically depend on. Her art-research collaborations include [*Anatomy of an AI System* with Vladan Joler (2018)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kate_Crawford) — subsequently acquired by MoMA and held in the permanent collections of the V&A, the Ars Electronica Center, and the Design Museum London — and [*Calculating Empires: A Genealogy of Technology and Power since 1500* with Vladan Joler, which won the Silver Lion at the 2025 Venice Architecture Biennale](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kate_Crawford), and *Training Humans* with Trevor Paglen (2019).
