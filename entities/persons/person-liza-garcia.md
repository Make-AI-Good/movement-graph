Filipina feminist and digital-rights advocate; [Executive Director](https://fma.ph/the-team/) of the [Foundation for Media Alternatives (FMA)](../organizations/org-foundation-for-media-alternatives.md), the Quezon City-headquartered Philippine civil-society digital-rights organisation that anchors national advocacy on internet rights, privacy, gender and ICT, and — since 2023 — artificial-intelligence governance and AI-related technology-facilitated gender-based violence. [Holding the executive directorship by at least June 2020](https://www.apc.org/en/news/fma-human-rights-groups-condemn-philippine-anti-terrorism-act-2020-call-mass-resistance), she succeeded the long-running leadership cycle through which FMA grew from its 1997 ICT-democratisation pivot into a senior Asia-Pacific civil-society node.

Garcia [holds](https://fma.ph/the-team/) an undergraduate degree in Sociology from the University of the Philippines, a Master's in International Relations from Kiev State University, and an MA in Women and Development Studies from the University of the Philippines, and she worked for an NGO focused on women and migration before joining FMA. Her [public FMA authorship record](https://fma.ph/author/lisa-garcia/) begins in October 2015 and includes the Philippine Declaration on Internet Rights and Principles, FMA's APrIGF and ASEAN civil-society engagement, RightsCon 2018 reporting on surveillance and gender-based violence, the Philippine Data Privacy Act implementing-rules consultations, and FMA's Universal Periodic Review submissions on the Philippines' digital-rights record; by [July 2015](https://fma.ph/ph-women-reps-in-macau-for-asias-annual-internet-governance-forum/) she was already an FMA delegate to the Asia Pacific Regional Internet Governance Forum. Before her elevation to the executive directorship she led FMA's Gender and ICT programme line — including [authorship of the 2017 FMA monograph](https://www.fma.ph/wp-content/uploads/2017/09/monograph_finalz.pdf) *Gender Violence: The Philippine Experience On the Internet*, the organisation's anchor long-form text on technology-facilitated gender-based violence — and continues to anchor FMA's TFGBV work alongside its broader digital-rights, AI-governance, and privacy portfolio.

As Executive Director Garcia is a regularly cited civil-society voice on Philippine digital-rights legislation and policy — including [FMA's 2020 critique](https://www.apc.org/en/news/fma-human-rights-groups-condemn-philippine-anti-terrorism-act-2020-call-mass-resistance) of the Anti-Terrorism Act of 2020 ("the Anti-Terror Bill disproportionately expands the State's surveillance powers by providing longer periods of permissible surveillance") and [the March 2025 cross-sectoral forum on technology-facilitated gender-based violence](https://www.bworldonline.com/spotlight/2025/03/04/657020/filipina-cybersecurity-expert-advocates-for-womens-awareness-in-combating-technology-abuse/) on women's political participation hosted at the Cybercrime Investigation and Coordinating Center in Taguig City. She also [co-moderates the Dynamic Coalition on Gender and Internet Governance](https://fma.ph/the-team/) at the UN Internet Governance Forum, anchoring FMA's named presence in the multilateral gender-and-internet-governance track that runs through the IGF and the APC Asia-Pacific subnetwork (of which [FMA is the fifth Asian member](../organizations/org-foundation-for-media-alternatives.md)).
