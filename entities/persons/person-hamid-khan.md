# Hamid Khan

Hamid Khan is a Pakistani-born community organizer based in Los Angeles whose 35-year career spans immigrant rights (founding and directing South Asian Network, 1990–2010, [the first grassroots South Asian community organization in Los Angeles](https://unequalcities.org/hamid-khan/)), workers' rights (founding member of the Los Angeles Taxi Workers Alliance), and police surveillance abolition (founder and Coalition Coordinator of the [Stop LAPD Spying Coalition](../local-groups/lg-stop-lapd-spying-coalition.md), 2011–present). Recipient of a 2011 Soros Justice Fellowship from the Open Society Foundations to challenge LAPD surveillance practices. Board affiliations: Political Research Associates; National Network for Immigrant and Refugee Rights; Youth Justice Coalition; Generation 5; May First Movement Technology. Campaign record, public output, and signature framings are recorded on the linked Voice entry.
