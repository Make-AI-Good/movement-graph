# Kashiefa Achmat
Cape Town housing organiser; chairperson of [Housing Assembly](../organizations/org-housing-assembly.md) since 2020, having [joined the movement in 2011](https://www.giraffe.org/kashiefa-achmat). Her organising background is in retail-sector trade unionism — [29 years at Pick n Pay and 23 years as a SACCAWU shop steward and gender coordinator](https://iopn.library.illinois.edu/scalar/constructing-solidarities-for-a-humane-urbanism/speaker-biographies.19), with parallel involvement in the Rita Edwards Collective women's organisation. As chairperson she has led Housing Assembly through its [COVID-era High Court challenge to City of Cape Town lockdown evictions, the August 2022 PatrickMustFall protest, and the Waiting List Campaign](https://www.giraffe.org/kashiefa-achmat) (the latter pursued with support from the [Legal Resources Centre](../organizations/org-legal-resources-centre.md)). In April 2026 she became the named Housing Assembly spokesperson on the [joint Housing Assembly / Foxglove objection](https://www.foxglove.org.uk/2026/04/29/foxglove-south-african-housing-movement-challenge-new-hyperscale-data-centre/) to Equinix's proposed 160 MVA hyperscale data centre at King Air Industria, framing the application as a redistributional choice between corporate land allocation and Cape Town's housing emergency.
