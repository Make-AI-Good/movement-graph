# Tania Rodríguez

Tania Rodríguez (age 54 as of 2026) is a founding member and principal public voice of [MOSACAT](../local-groups/lg-mosacat.md) — the Movimiento Socioambiental Comunal por la Tierra y el Agua — based in the Cerrillos commune of Santiago, Chile. A former school teacher, she became the group's most visible organiser during its successful campaign against Google's proposed second Chilean data centre in Cerrillos, which was [authorised to extract more than seven billion litres of water annually](https://restofworld.org/2024/data-centers-environmental-issues/) from a drought-stressed metropolitan commune.

Rodríguez characterises data-centre expansion as a form of ["digital extractivism"](https://www.techpolicy.press/many-latin-americans-living-near-data-centers-do-not-feel-welcome-in-the-future/) and has articulated MOSACAT's position in two recurring public framings: ["We end up being everybody's backyard"](https://restofworld.org/2024/data-centers-environmental-issues/) and ["We're not against Big Tech, but in favor of nature. We don't want our countries to get steamrolled by extractivism"](https://restofworld.org/2026/ai-pushback-chile-mexico-kenya-philippines/). In September 2024 she was [named to TIME100 AI 2024](https://time.com/collections/time100-ai-2024/7012849/tania-rodriguez/) — Time magazine's annual list of the 100 most influential people in AI — and appeared in [*Defensoras de la Tierra*](https://www.latercera.com/culto/2024/04/22/defensoras-de-la-tierra-estrenan-la-serie-documental-sobre-mujeres-ambientalistas-en-chile/) (premiered April 2024), a six-chapter Chilean web documentary on women environmental defenders.
