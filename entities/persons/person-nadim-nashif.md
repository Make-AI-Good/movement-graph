[Palestinian](https://al-shabaka.org/authors/nadim-nashif/) community organiser and digital-rights defender based in Haifa; [co-founder and Executive Director](https://www.newarab.com/author/71331/nadim-nashif) of [7amleh — The Arab Center for the Advancement of Social Media](../organizations/org-7amleh.md), the Haifa-headquartered Palestinian non-governmental organisation he [established in 2013](https://7amleh.org/annual23/eng/) alongside co-founders Laura Samara and Manar Ya'qoub and has led as its principal public-facing voice since founding. Nashif is 7amleh's lead interlocutor with the international platform-accountability field, the [public-facing spokesperson](https://www.civicus.org/index.php/media-resources/news/interviews/5843-palestine-they-label-us-antisemites-or-terrorists-to-silence-us-and-paralyse-our-human-rights-work) in the organisation's multi-year engagement with Meta's content moderation of Palestinian and Arabic-language content, and the convener of its annual Palestine Digital Activism Forum.

Nashif's [career as a community organiser](https://al-shabaka.org/authors/nadim-nashif/) predates 7amleh by more than a decade. He [founded Baladna — the Association for Arab Youth](https://www.journalismfestival.com/speaker/nadim-nashif) and served as its director, an organisation working with Palestinian Arab youth inside Israel on capacity-building and community development; founded and coordinated the youth wing of the [Balad political party](https://al-shabaka.org/authors/nadim-nashif/) (the National Democratic Assembly representing Palestinian citizens of Israel); previously directed the Committee for Educational Guidance for Arab Students; and is the [co-founder of Wusol Digital Academy](https://www.journalismfestival.com/speaker/nadim-nashif), a digital marketing educational centre. His broader public output sits alongside his 7amleh role as a [Policy Member of Al-Shabaka — The Palestinian Policy Network](https://al-shabaka.org/authors/nadim-nashif/), where his named expertise areas are Information & Technology; Society & Culture; and Zionism & Israeli Politics, and as a regular contributor to The New Arab and other Arabic-language and Anglophone publications on Palestinian digital rights.

Within the corpus's frame, Nashif anchors the Palestinian-civil-society and Arabic-language digital-rights register that [7amleh](../organizations/org-7amleh.md) has built since 2013, and is the corpus's first Palestinian Person entry — the principal public-facing voice on AI-mediated content moderation under wartime conditions, the asymmetric enforcement of Meta's Arabic and Hebrew moderation systems, and the operational case for grassroots Arabic-language civil-society building its own AI tooling (the Violence Indicator) to scale up monitoring of the platforms it is campaigning to reform. The Palestinian-citizen-of-Israel positioning of Nashif's career — Baladna, the Balad youth wing, the Committee for Educational Guidance, then 7amleh's Haifa headquarters with its Ramallah office — sits structurally distinct from the West-Bank and Gaza-Strip Palestinian civil-society register, and gives 7amleh's work its particular reach across the green line and into the international platform-accountability field where Nashif is its consolidated public voice.
