French public-law jurist and the [legal officer](https://www.laquadrature.net/en/2024/10/16/french-family-welfare-scoring-algorithm-challenged-in-court-by-15-organisations/) anchoring [La Quadrature du Net](../organizations/org-la-quadrature-du-net.md)'s litigation track against AI-and-automated-decision systems in French public administration. The principal French civil-society legal voice on the CAF and France Travail algorithmic-scoring files and the corpus's clearest French-language register on grassroots-civil-society strategic litigation against welfare-control and unemployment-profiling algorithms.

Le Querrec has [been a member of La Quadrature du Net since 2019](https://shs.cairn.info/publications-de-bastien-le-querrec--765495?lang=fr) and works in its [litigation group ("contentieux")](https://shs.cairn.info/publications-de-bastien-le-querrec--765495?lang=fr). He is currently a staff member of [LQDN's operational team ("équipe opérationnelle")](https://www.laquadrature.net/nous/), in which the organisation lists him with the title "Juriste"; his own public [self-description](https://mastodon.social/@blequerrec) is "Legal officer at La Quadrature du Net", the form LQDN uses in its English-language publications. He is in parallel a [PhD student in public law at Université Grenoble-Alpes](https://aboutintel.eu/profile/Bastien%20Le%20Querrec/), with a doctoral research programme on algorithmic decision-making by public-administration agencies and its impacts on human rights — the same substantive question that organises his LQDN litigation portfolio.

## Algorithmic-public-administration litigation

Le Querrec is the lead LQDN legal officer on the corpus's clearest French-language civil-society litigation portfolio against AI-and-automated-decision systems operated by the French state. The [Caisse nationale des Allocations Familiales (CNAF) scoring file](https://www.laquadrature.net/en/2024/10/16/french-family-welfare-scoring-algorithm-challenged-in-court-by-15-organisations/) — the 15-civil-society-organisation challenge to the algorithmic fraud-risk scoring of roughly 13 million French family-benefits households — is the centrepiece: Le Querrec is the LQDN spokesperson on the Conseil d'État filing, the named carrier of the discrimination-and-Article-22 GDPR argument that the algorithm "[is the manifestation of a policy of persecution of the poorest people](https://www.laquadrature.net/en/2024/10/16/french-family-welfare-scoring-algorithm-challenged-in-court-by-15-organisations/) — because you are in an economically vulnerable position, you will be suspect in the eyes of the algorithm, and therefore checked — it's a double punishment". The parallel [France Travail file](https://www.laquadrature.net/france-travail/) — LQDN's documentation and litigation track on the French unemployment agency's algorithmic profiling of jobseekers — is the second anchor of the portfolio, and Le Querrec's [June 2024 LQDN analysis "À France Travail, l'essor du contrôle algorithmique"](https://www.laquadrature.net/2024/06/25/a-france-travail-lessor-du-controle-algorithmique/) is the substantive case-build on the "anticipation of disengagement", "rupture risk", and "employability score" systems used to triage and prioritise unemployed-jobseeker support. The litigation portfolio extends across the Caisse nationale d'Assurance Maladie (CNAM) health-insurance system and the French tax administration; together these files constitute the algorithmic-public-administration track that LQDN's broader programme is anchored on.

## Posture and public output

Le Querrec's distinctive contribution to the make-AI-good movement is the translation of LQDN's grassroots-organising base — through the [Technopolice](../organizations/org-la-quadrature-du-net.md) campaign's municipal documentation of algorithmic surveillance — into French-court strategic litigation against the algorithmic systems that organise state social-control infrastructure. Beyond the public-administration litigation, his [2024 focus areas](https://mastodon.social/@blequerrec) extend to platform interoperability as a decentralisation lever, encryption rights, and digital-ecology questions. His [academic output](https://shs.cairn.info/publications-de-bastien-le-querrec--765495?lang=fr) — including his 2023 article in *Après-demain* on the failure of centralised social-networks and the case for federated alternatives — anchors the substantive scholarly register that runs alongside the litigation work.
