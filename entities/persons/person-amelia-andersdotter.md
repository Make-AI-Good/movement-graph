Swedish digital-rights activist and technical-standards specialist, based in Brussels; the corpus's primary Swedish-language anchor in the European civil-society digital-rights layer. Born [30 August 1987 in Uppsala](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amelia_Andersdotter), she studied mathematics, physics, Spanish, and business law at Lund University and completed an MSc in mathematical statistics at Uppsala University. She [joined the Swedish Pirate Party shortly after its 2006 founding](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amelia_Andersdotter) and served as international coordinator of its youth wing Ung Pirat from 2007 to 2010.

Andersdotter served as a Member of the European Parliament [from December 2011 to May 2014](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amelia_Andersdotter), representing Swedish Pirate Party as one of the youngest MEPs of that legislature. She sat on the Committee on Industry, Research and Energy and was the committee's [rapporteur on the Anti-Counterfeiting Trade Agreement (ACTA)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amelia_Andersdotter) — the European Parliament ultimately rejected ACTA in July 2012, a result [widely attributed in part to civil-society and parliamentary pressure in which she played a named role](https://amelia.andersdotter.cc/arkiv/about-amelia.html). She was [named one of the world's ten most important internet activists in 2012](https://amelia.andersdotter.cc/arkiv/about-amelia.html) for this work. She was also a member of the [IGF Multistakeholder Advisory Group from 2013 to 2016](https://amelia.andersdotter.cc/).

Her civil-society trajectory runs in parallel with and outlasts the parliamentary period. In 2015 she founded and chaired [Dataskydd.net Sverige](https://www.netnod.se/amelia-andersdotter-article19-and-anec) — a Swedish NGO focused on making data-protection law practical for ordinary organisations and citizens, and the home of [webbkoll](https://www.netnod.se/amelia-andersdotter-article19-and-anec), a publicly available tool that checks what tracking technology websites deploy against users. Post-parliament she joined [ARTICLE 19](../organizations/org-article-19.md) as [a technical standards consultant covering human rights in IEEE 802, IETF, and 3GPP network-hardware standards](https://www.netnod.se/amelia-andersdotter-article19-and-anec), and in parallel advised ANEC (the European consumer-voice in standardisation) on consumer rights in IoT under ISO PC 317. From 2020 to 2022 she was technical privacy advisor to the [Ada Lovelace Institute](../organizations/org-ada-lovelace-institute.md)'s [Rethinking Data project](https://amelia.andersdotter.cc/). Until May 2023 she was in the [response team](https://amelia.andersdotter.cc/) of [European Digital Rights (EDRi)](../organizations/org-edri.md), the Brussels-based network of civil-society organisations working to defend rights and freedoms in the digital environment. She has since held roles as Director of Strategic Initiatives at the Council of European National Top-Level Domain Registries (CENTR), risk and compliance officer at Safespring AB, and interim managing director of the Universal Privacy Alliance. Her distinctive position in the corpus is the continuous arc from early Pirate Party organising through technical standards work and into data-protection civil society — [applied over more than fifteen years across the Swedish, European, and international digital-rights landscape](https://amelia.andersdotter.cc/).
