Kenyan union organiser; committee secretary and primary spokesperson of the [African Content Moderators Union](../organizations/org-african-content-moderators-union.md). A [twenty-six-year-old international relations graduate](https://www.codastory.com/authoritarian-tech/kenya-content-moderators/), Oyange — widely known as "Mojez" — joined the Luxembourg-based outsourcer Majorel on what his [contract specified as a "customer service representative" role](https://www.techpolicy.press/checking-on-the-progress-of-content-moderators-in-africa/), discovering only after starting that the assigned work was in fact moderation of TikTok content. He worked there for [nine months](https://www.codastory.com/authoritarian-tech/kenya-content-moderators/) before his contract was not renewed in April 2023; he attributes the non-renewal to his speaking publicly about pay and conditions, and reports continuing post-employment effects including [severe insomnia and a recurring nightmare of mass beheading](https://www.codastory.com/authoritarian-tech/kenya-content-moderators/).

After losing the Majorel contract Oyange [connected](https://www.codastory.com/authoritarian-tech/kenya-content-moderators/) with [Foxglove](../organizations/org-foxglove.md) and attended the May 2023 Nairobi founding meeting at which more than 150 African content moderators voted to establish the ACMU; he became one of the [union's six-member founding committee](https://nonprofitquarterly.org/can-african-unions-beat-big-tech/) and its most-quoted public face. He has characterised the union's opposition as a three-part coalition of platforms, outsourcers, and the Kenyan state — ["like fighting the three-headed dragon in the Godzilla series"](https://nonprofitquarterly.org/can-african-unions-beat-big-tech/) — and has reported being [blacklisted](https://nonprofitquarterly.org/can-african-unions-beat-big-tech/) from further moderation employment, with one interviewer telling him that his visibility as a worker-rights advocate would "complicate things" for the company. Oyange is also [preparing litigation against TikTok and Majorel](https://www.techpolicy.press/checking-on-the-progress-of-content-moderators-in-africa/) over the conditions of the Sub-Saharan moderation pipeline.
