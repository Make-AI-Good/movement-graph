# Irene Mwendwa
Kenyan [tech lawyer](https://techcabal.com/2026/04/08/my-life-in-tech-irene-mwendwa/) and Executive Director of [Pollicy](../organizations/org-pollicy.md) [since 1 August 2023](https://neemaiyer.com/work/reflecting-on-my-journey-as-founder-and-leader), succeeding the organisation's founder [Neema Iyer](../persons/person-neema-iyer.md). The principal Kenyan / Nairobi-anchored counterpart, at the leadership of the corpus's first sub-Saharan African feminist-tech organisation, to Iyer's pan-African and Sydney-and-Kampala-anchored founding voice.

[From Machakos](https://techcabal.com/2026/04/08/my-life-in-tech-irene-mwendwa/) — a small Eastern Kenyan town about 63km southeast of Nairobi — Mwendwa [trained as a lawyer at Kenyatta University](https://techcabal.com/2026/04/08/my-life-in-tech-irene-mwendwa/) and [now advises governments, civil-society actors and multilateral organisations on digital inclusion, elections and technology policy](https://www.bmz-digital.global/en/irene-mwendwa-disinformation-data-literacy/). She joined Pollicy as [Program Manager for Feminist Movement Building and Advocacy](https://www.ned.org/forum-qa-irene-mwendwa-on-women-data-and-democracy/) before being promoted to Executive Director; Iyer's [retrospective on her own succession](https://neemaiyer.com/work/reflecting-on-my-journey-as-founder-and-leader) attributes the handover to Mwendwa's "strong feminist background", her partnership-building and funding-landscape expertise, and her understanding of the Kenyan "frontier market in the digital and data space".

Mwendwa's substantive line of work routes Pollicy's Afro-feminist civic-tech methodology into platform-governance, electoral-integrity, and gendered-disinformation registers. She self-identifies as a [tech lawyer rather than activist](https://techcabal.com/2026/04/08/my-life-in-tech-irene-mwendwa/) — "I work on tech and society. I'm working to ensure that people are still able to enjoy the use of technologies without being harmed" — and frames internet design itself as a women's rights issue, particularly for African women political leaders facing coordinated harassment and algorithmic bias. Through Pollicy she leads programme work including [the Afrofeminist Internet Scorecard, the Digital Safe Tea card game, the Voice Data Literacy Training Program, and the Fair Digital Kazi Manifesto](https://www.bmz-digital.global/en/irene-mwendwa-disinformation-data-literacy/), and has [publicly anchored the Pollicy research finding that 18% of female candidates in Kenya's elections encountered online violence](https://www.apc.org/en/blog/why-gendered-disinformation-dangerous-african-democracy) inside the wider African civil-society response to gendered disinformation in African elections. She is [deeply committed to advocating for a feminist internet](https://www.ned.org/forum-qa-irene-mwendwa-on-women-data-and-democracy/) — the public-facing throughline of her work at the post-Iyer leadership phase of Pollicy.
