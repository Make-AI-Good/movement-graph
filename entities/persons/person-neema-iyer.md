Indian-Ugandan technologist, artist, and researcher; [Founder](https://pollicy.org/about/) of [Pollicy](../organizations/org-pollicy.md), the Kampala-headquartered Ugandan feminist civic-technology collective she [established in 2016](https://pollicy.org/about/) and led as Executive Director through August 2023 when she [stepped down](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neema_Iyer) and continues as advisor and board member. The principal public-facing voice of the corpus's sub-Saharan African feminist-tech anchor, currently based [between Sydney and Kampala](https://neemaiyer.com/).

[Raised in Nigeria by Tanzanian and Indian parents](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neema_Iyer), Iyer trained as an epidemiologist — completing a Masters in Public Health at Emory University with a focus on epidemiology and statistics — and worked across [large-scale mobile and digital initiatives in Africa at TTC Mobile and Viamo](https://pacscenter.stanford.edu/person/neema-iyer/) before moving to Uganda in 2013 and founding Pollicy in 2016 to "reshape government service delivery in Africa through better, more responsible data practice". Her [self-description](https://neemaiyer.com/) as "Artist. Maker. Researcher." and "Creative Technologist" sits behind Pollicy's distinctive pairing of empirical research and policy advocacy with creative and speculative work — visible most directly in her illustration practice (under [@neemascribbles](https://pacscenter.stanford.edu/person/neema-iyer/)), her character design for Pollicy's *Choose Your Own Fake News* misinformation-awareness game, and the speculative-fiction commissioning of Pollicy's *Africana Futurism* programme.

Iyer's fellowship and recognition record routes Pollicy's East African Afro-feminist data and AI work into the wider Global North digital-rights field. She is a [Senior Fellow in Trustworthy AI](https://www.mozillafoundation.org/en/what-we-fund/programs/senior-fellows/fellow-profiles/neema-iyer/) at the Mozilla Foundation (since 2022) following a [Mozilla Creative Media Award](https://neemaiyer.com/) in 2020, was a [2021-22 Practitioner Fellow](https://pacscenter.stanford.edu/person/neema-iyer/) at Stanford PACS's Digital Civil Society Lab where she authored *Automated Imperialism, Expansionist Dreams* on digital extractivism in Africa, was [appointed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neema_Iyer) in July 2021 to Facebook (now Meta)'s Global Women's Safety Advisory Board, was [named](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neema_Iyer) a 2021 Quartz Africa Innovator, and received the 2021 Coalition for Digital Equality Research and Knowledge Builder award. Alongside the institutional fellowships she [appears as a guest practitioner](https://openrestitution.africa/access-for-who-podcast-launch/) on Open Restitution Africa's *Access For Who?* podcast series on digital restitution (hosted by Chao Tayiana Maina and Molemo Moiloa) and is a [PhD candidate at the University of Sydney](https://neemaiyer.com/) researching feminist game design — the doctoral extension of the Pollicy-anchored Afro-feminist speculative-and-creative methodological line. Iyer is the corpus's principal Ugandan / East African feminist-tech anchor and the African-side counterpart, in the make-AI-good corpus's regional shape, to [Coding Rights](../organizations/org-coding-rights.md)'s Latin American feminist-tech anchor at [Joana Varon](../persons/person-joana-varon.md).
