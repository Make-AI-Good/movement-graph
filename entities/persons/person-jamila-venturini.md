Brazilian journalist and researcher; [Co-Executive Director](https://www.derechosdigitales.org/en/about-us/) of [Derechos Digitales](../organizations/org-derechos-digitales.md), the Latin American digital-rights non-profit, alongside [J. Carlos Lara](../persons/person-juan-carlos-lara.md). The [co-direction model](https://www.derechosdigitales.org/16242/derechos-digitales-the-start-of-a-new-cycle/) was introduced on 1 September 2021, with Venturini and Lara — both then members of the organisation's managerial staff — taking joint stewardship after [Maria Paz Canales](https://www.derechosdigitales.org/16242/derechos-digitales-the-start-of-a-new-cycle/) stepped down from a four-year tenure as Executive Director. Derechos Digitales' own about page [frames](https://www.derechosdigitales.org/en/about-us/) Venturini as an "activist and researcher with over 15 years of experience in civil society organizations".

Venturini holds a [journalism degree](https://policyreview.info/users/jamila-venturini) from the University of São Paulo and a [Master's in Social Sciences](https://www.derechosdigitales.org/en/about-us/) with a focus on Education from FLACSO Argentina; she is a [doctoral candidate](https://lavits.org/en/a_rede/jamila-venturini/) in Social Sciences at Universidade Estadual de Campinas (Unicamp). Before joining Derechos Digitales' senior leadership she was a [researcher at the Center for Technology and Society](https://policyreview.info/users/jamila-venturini) at the Fundação Getulio Vargas (CTS-FGV) law school in Rio de Janeiro, where she coordinated the "Privacy in the Digital Age" project, and a 2016 Google Policy Fellow [placed with Access Now](https://policyreview.info/users/jamila-venturini). She has served as an [expert consultant](https://policyreview.info/users/jamila-venturini) to the Brazilian Internet Steering Committee (CGI.br) on its annual ICT and Education research since 2014 and is a [member](https://lavits.org/en/a_rede/jamila-venturini/) of the Latin American Network of Surveillance, Technology and Society Studies (LAVITS).

Under the [Lara/Venturini co-direction](https://www.apc.org/en/news/derechos-digitales-20-years-working-latin-america-our-region-forefront-assessment-impacts) Venturini [coordinates](https://aplusalliance.org/member/jamila-venturini/) Derechos Digitales' artificial-intelligence and inclusion programme line — analyses of how state-deployed AI and "data colonialism" shape Latin American publics, addressing the under-representation of Global South voices in AI governance debates. She is a co-author of the organisation's comparative regional study [Decisiones automatizadas en la función pública en América Latina](https://www.derechosdigitales.org/wp-content/uploads/CPC_informeComparado.pdf) on automated decision-making in Brazil, Chile, Colombia, and Uruguay, and works with the [A+ Alliance](https://aplusalliance.org/member/jamila-venturini/) on mapping feminist principles for AI development across Latin America alongside María Paz Canales.
