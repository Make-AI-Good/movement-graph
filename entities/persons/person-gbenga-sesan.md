# 'Gbenga Sesan
Nigerian social entrepreneur and Executive Director of [Paradigm Initiative](../organizations/org-paradigm-initiative.md), the pan-African digital-rights and digital-inclusion organisation he [founded in Lagos in 2007](https://paradigmhq.org/team/gbenga-sesan/) and which now operates out of six sub-regional offices (Cameroon, Kenya, Nigeria, Senegal, Zambia, Zimbabwe) with interventions across more than twenty-seven African countries. Lagos-based; the principal public-facing African civil-society voice on the make-AI-good corpus's Anglophone West African and pan-African digital-rights anchor.

Born ['Oluwagbenga Olabisi Sesan on 27 July 1977 in Akure, Ondo State](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gbenga_Sesan), Nigeria, Sesan trained as an Electronic and Electrical Engineer at Obafemi Awolowo University (2002) and completed executive education programmes at Lagos Business School, Oxford University, Harvard University, [Stanford University](https://pacscenter.stanford.edu/person/gbenga-sesan/), INSEAD, and King's College London. He was [named](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gbenga_Sesan) a 2007 [Ashoka Fellow](https://www.ashoka.org/en-us/fellow/gbenga-sesan), the 2014 Schwab Foundation Social Entrepreneur of the Year, an Archbishop Desmond Tutu Leadership Fellow, and recognised in 2012 as one of CNN's "10 Leading African Tech Voices" on Twitter and one of Ventures Africa's "40 African Legends Under 40".

Sesan's parallel institutional roles route African civil-society digital-rights and AI positions into the multilateral institutions where global AI rules are increasingly being negotiated. He was [appointed in 2022](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gbenga_Sesan) to the UN Secretary-General's inaugural Internet Governance Forum (IGF) Leadership Panel and serves on the [International Panel on the Information Environment](https://paradigmhq.org/team/gbenga-sesan/) launched at the 2023 Nobel Prize Summit. He was a [Practitioner Fellow at Stanford PACS's Digital Civil Society Lab](https://pacscenter.stanford.edu/person/gbenga-sesan/) across the 2019-2020 and 2020-2021 cycles, is an advisor to the World Economic Forum's Operationalising Trust Project, and advised the World Health Organization on data privacy during the early coronavirus pandemic. His named [framing](https://www.apc.org/en/news/interview-gbenga-sesan-what-important-digital-rights-campaign-nigeria-and-beyond-ensuring) of Paradigm Initiative's theory of change — that digital rights are made load-bearing by being grounded in people's day-to-day experiences rather than imported abstractly from Global North policy traditions — anchors the organisation's wider AI-and-human-rights line through its *Ayeta* toolkit, the annual [Digital Rights and Inclusion Forum (DRIF)](../organizations/org-paradigm-initiative.md), the annual *Londa* state-of-digital-rights-in-Africa report, and the November 2024 *Ethical Governance and Inclusive Legislation on Artificial Intelligence in East and Southern Africa* regional report.
