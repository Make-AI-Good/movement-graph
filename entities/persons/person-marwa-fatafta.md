# Marwa Fatafta


Palestinian writer, researcher, and policy analyst; [MENA Policy and Advocacy Director](https://www.accessnow.org/profile/marwa-fatafta/) at [Access Now](../organizations/org-access-now.md), leading the organisation's digital-rights work across the Middle East and North Africa since 2020 from her base in Berlin. The principal Palestinian-diaspora policy voice in the corpus's Access Now senior staff and the named Arabic-language anchor on platform-accountability, surveillance, and tech-in-armed-conflict in the make-AI-good movement's MENA register — paired in Access Now's leadership of the corpus's digital-rights coalition with [Felicia Anthonio](../persons/person-felicia-anthonio.md)'s internet-shutdowns register.

[Originally from Hebron](https://www.wikidata.org/wiki/Q116842753) and Berlin-based since 2017, Fatafta carries a Palestinian-diaspora biography across English, Arabic, and the German policy context. She [holds a BA in English from Birzeit University](https://www.wikidata.org/wiki/Q116842753) (2003–2007), was a [Fulbright scholar to the US](https://www.accessnow.org/profile/marwa-fatafta/) and holds an [MA in International Relations from the Maxwell School of Citizenship and Public Affairs at Syracuse University](https://www.accessnow.org/profile/marwa-fatafta/) (2009–2011), and a second [Master's in Development and Governance from the University of Duisburg-Essen](https://www.accessnow.org/profile/marwa-fatafta/) (2014). Before joining Access Now, she was [Communications Manager at the British Consulate-General in Jerusalem](https://www.wikidata.org/wiki/Q116842753) (2011–2014) and [MENA Regional Advisor at Transparency International Secretariat in Berlin](https://al-shabaka.org/profiles/marwa-fatafta/) (2017–2020), where she coordinated regional and global efforts to mainstream gender into anti-corruption policy and norms.

## Access Now portfolio

Fatafta's [published focus areas](https://www.accessnow.org/profile/marwa-fatafta/) at Access Now span content governance and platform accountability, online censorship, digital surveillance, and transnational repression, with substantial published output on the digital occupation in Palestine and the role of new technologies in armed conflicts and humanitarian contexts. The platform-accountability portfolio is the most public-facing strand: in her [Tech Policy Press interview](https://www.techpolicy.press/a-perspective-on-metas-moderation-of-palestinian-voices/) on Meta's handling of Palestinian voices she frames the central asymmetry as "Meta over moderates Palestine-related content, especially in Arabic language" while "under-moderates hate speech" — the substantive thesis that organises Access Now's MENA-region engagement with platform-governance institutions through Meta's Oversight Board, the EU Digital Services Act, and the UN multi-stakeholder tracks. On surveillance her on-record framing is that "[authoritarian governments and oppressive regimes use the same tactics — they all have the same oppressive handbook](https://al-shabaka.org/profiles/marwa-fatafta/)", which anchors her policy work across spyware export controls, mass-surveillance regulation, and the Pegasus, Predator, and adjacent commercial-spyware-targeting tracks. Her published authorship of the [Internet freedoms in Palestine: Mapping of digital rights violations and threats](https://www.apc.org/en/pubs/internet-freedoms-palestine-mapping-digital-rights-violations-and-threats) report — through [7amleh](../organizations/org-7amleh.md) in cooperation with the Association for Progressive Communications, framed by APC as "the first of its kind on the Palestinian level" — anchors the substantive Palestine digital-rights research register she carries alongside the Access Now policy track. Her public-output line runs through [+972 Magazine](https://www.972mag.com/writer/marwaf/), [Rest of World](https://restofworld.org/author/marwa-fatafta/), Al Jazeera, and the Arabic-language press; she also speaks regularly at RightsCon and on the UN, EU, and German federal policy circuits.

## External affiliations

Beyond Access Now, Fatafta sits on the [Editorial Committee and as a Policy Member of Al-Shabaka: The Palestinian Policy Network](https://al-shabaka.org/profiles/marwa-fatafta/) — the diaspora-Palestinian policy network on global policy, information and technology, Palestinian politics and governance, and the Arab world — and on the [Board of Advisors of the Tahrir Institute for Middle East Policy (TIMEP)](https://timep.org/author/marwafatafta/), the Washington-based MENA policy organisation. She is an [advisory committee member for Bread&Net](https://www.accessnow.org/profile/marwa-fatafta/), the Beirut-based annual MENA digital-rights gathering run by SMEX, and an [advisory-board member of 7amleh](https://al-shabaka.org/profiles/marwa-fatafta/) — the Palestinian-led Arab Center for the Advancement of Social Media that anchors much of the substantive Palestine-digital-rights research field she draws on at Access Now. The cluster of advisory roles is the connective tissue between the Palestinian-civil-society digital-rights field, the Arabic-language MENA-wide digital-rights field, and the international platform-accountability and surveillance-policy field that Access Now's policy work runs at — and is the substantive reason the senior MENA seat at Access Now sits with a diaspora-Palestinian holder rooted in those communities rather than with a Brussels- or Washington-anchored policy generalist.
