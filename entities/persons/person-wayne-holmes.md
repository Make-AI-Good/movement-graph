# Wayne Holmes

Wayne Holmes is Associate Professor at UCL's IOE (Faculty of Education and Society), where his research examines teaching and application of AI in educational contexts through lenses of ethics, human rights, and social justice. He [leads the Council of Europe's expert group on AI and Education](https://www.ei-ie.org/en/author/1610:wayne-holmes), developing human rights protections for students and teachers interacting with AI systems, and serves as a consultant for UNESCO's Technology and AI in Education unit and as a senior researcher at IRCAI (International Research Centre on Artificial Intelligence under UNESCO). He holds a PhD from the University of Oxford.

Holmes [authored](https://www.ei-ie.org/en/item/28115:the-unintended-consequences-of-artificial-intelligence-and-education) [*The Unintended Consequences of Artificial Intelligence and Education*](../publications/pub-education-international-ai-in-education.md) (Education International, October 2023), the flagship analytical publication in EI's campaign to position global education unions as active shapers of AI's trajectory in education. His prior publications include *AI and Education: Guidance for Policy-makers* (UNESCO), *Guidance for Generative AI in Education and Research* (UNESCO), and co-edited volume *The Ethics of AI in Education: Practices, Challenges and Debates* (Routledge, 2022).
