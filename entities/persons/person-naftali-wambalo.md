Kenyan former Samasource data labeller and [co-founder of the Africa Tech Workers Movement](https://thenextweb.com/news/meta-smart-glasses-sama-kenya-workers). A [mathematics graduate](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/ai-work-kenya-exploitation-60-minutes/) and father of two from Nairobi, Wambalo worked as a "human in the loop" for Samasource Kenya on contracts for Meta and OpenAI — eight-hour shifts drawing bounding boxes on images for AI training and reviewing pornography, hate speech, and graphic violence for content-moderation queues at [$1.50–$2 per hour gross](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/ai-work-kenya-exploitation-60-minutes/). He is one of [roughly 200 digital workers suing Sama and Meta in Kenya](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/ai-work-kenya-exploitation-60-minutes/) over the working conditions and psychological harm of that pipeline; a [psychiatric evaluation](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/ai-work-kenya-exploitation-60-minutes/) of the cohort confirmed sustained mental-health damage, which Wambalo himself attributes to the volume of sexual and violent material he was required to label.

As a co-founder of the [Africa Tech Workers Movement](../organizations/org-africa-tech-workers-movement.md) Wambalo has become one of the Movement's principal public voices on the Sama–Meta pipeline. After Meta abruptly terminated its content-moderation contract with Sama in April 2026 — the immediate trigger for a [redundancy notice covering 1,108 workers](https://thenextweb.com/news/meta-smart-glasses-sama-kenya-workers) — Wambalo, speaking for the Movement, alleged that ["Meta was retaliating against the workers who spoke out"](https://thenextweb.com/news/meta-smart-glasses-sama-kenya-workers) about the Ray-Ban Meta footage routed to Nairobi for review, and characterised Meta's stated rationale as ["standards of secrecy"](https://futurism.com/artificial-intelligence/meta-response-naked-footage-smart-glasses) rather than standards of quality. The Ray-Ban Meta footage scandal sits at the centre of the [Oversight Lab petition](../campaigns/camp-oversight-lab-meta-ray-ban-glasses-2026.md) that triggered the ODPC's 31 March 2026 own-motion investigation into Meta's smart-glasses pipeline; Wambalo's public commentary places the Movement on the worker-organising side of the same campaign.
