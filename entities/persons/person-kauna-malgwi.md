Nigerian clinical psychologist; [chairperson of the Steering Committee of the Nigeria chapter](https://time.com/collections/time100-ai-2024/7012799/kauna-malgwi/) of the [African Content Moderators Union](../organizations/org-african-content-moderators-union.md) and founder of the [Digital Rights and Mental Health Initiative Africa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kauna_Malgwi) (DRMHIA). Malgwi [fled northern Nigeria in 2012](https://time.com/collections/time100-ai-2024/7012799/kauna-malgwi/) during the Boko Haram insurgency with her mother and aunt, reached Abuja, and completed her education in Kenya, where she holds a [Master's in clinical psychology from United States International University-Africa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kauna_Malgwi) and is a PhD candidate in psychology at Pan Africa Christian University in Nairobi.

In 2019 she joined the Nairobi-based outsourcer [Samasource (Sama) believing the role was call-centre work](https://time.com/collections/time100-ai-2024/7012799/kauna-malgwi/) and discovered after starting that the assigned work was Facebook content moderation [in her native Hausa](https://www.codastory.com/authoritarian-tech/kenya-content-moderators/) under an NDA that prohibited her from disclosing she was reviewing Meta's content. She worked at Sama for [four years](https://restofworld.org/2023/meta-content-moderators-kenya-fired-unionize/) at approximately [$600 a month](https://restofworld.org/2023/meta-content-moderators-kenya-fired-unionize/), and reports that ["right from training, I started experiencing anxiety and panic attacks. I went into depression"](https://restofworld.org/2023/meta-content-moderators-kenya-fired-unionize/) — symptoms she later characterised, on the Coda Story record, as the period in which ["I don't even know how I survived. It was the worst time of my life."](https://www.codastory.com/authoritarian-tech/kenya-content-moderators/) Her tenure ended in January 2023 when Sama terminated its Meta contract and made [roughly 260 Kenyan-based Facebook moderators redundant](https://time.com/collections/time100-ai-2024/7012799/kauna-malgwi/) — the cohort whose mass dismissal became the basis for the [185-moderator petition](../campaigns/camp-foxglove-185-moderators-meta-kenya.md) now consolidated before Kenya's Employment and Labour Relations Court.

After losing the Sama role, Malgwi was among the moderators at the [May 2023 Nairobi summit](https://restofworld.org/2023/meta-content-moderators-kenya-fired-unionize/) at which more than 150 African content moderators voted to establish the African Content Moderators Union; she emerged as the chair of the union's Nigeria-chapter steering committee and one of the founding cohort's most internationally visible voices. In [February 2024 she testified at the European Parliament](https://time.com/collections/time100-ai-2024/7012799/kauna-malgwi/) in Brussels on the conditions of outsourced AI-supply-chain moderation work — including her continuing insomnia and intrusive imagery after viewing child-abuse material — and reported that growing worker awareness of organising rights was ["beginning to diminish the fear that you can just be laid off because you are raising concerns."](https://time.com/collections/time100-ai-2024/7012799/kauna-malgwi/) She was named to the [TIME100 AI 2024 list](https://www.thecable.ng/nigerias-chinasa-okolo-kauna-malgwi-named-among-time-100-most-influential-people-in-ai/) and the [BBC 100 Women 2024 cohort](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kauna_Malgwi) the same year, and is a [2026–2027 ICDE Fellow](https://platform.coop/people/kauna-malgwi/) at the Platform Cooperativism Consortium, where her research examines how AI-driven platform labour generates structural mental-health harm among content moderators in the Global South.
