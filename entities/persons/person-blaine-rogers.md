# Blaine William Rogers

Blaine William Rogers is the founder and organiser of [AI Safety 東京 (AI Safety Tokyo)](../local-groups/lg-ai-safety-tokyo.md), the Shibuya-headquartered Tokyo reading-group and conference community he [started from scratch in January 2023](https://manifund.org/projects/year-one-of-ai-safety-tokyo), and Research Communications Officer at the Tokyo-based AGI company [Noeon Research](https://manifund.org/blaineaisafetytokyo). He holds an MEng in Joint Mathematics and Computer Science from Imperial College London (2014–2018), where he [won the Donald Davies Prize for the best JMC master's thesis of 2018](https://www.linkedin.com/in/blainewilliamrogers/) for "pi-Actor: A pi-calculus abstraction for Erlang"; prior industry roles include Rapyuta Robotics and Five AI. He speaks Japanese at JLPT N2 level, which has been central to his effectiveness in building a domestic community in Tokyo.

Rogers began AI Safety Tokyo [having found no other person in Japan inclined to work on AI safety](https://manifund.org/projects/year-one-of-ai-safety-tokyo) after his own light research, and funded the group's first year out of pocket (visa constraints at the time prevented external grants). His stated ambition was "building a safety community in Tokyo from zero" with the ultimate aim of making Tokyo an AI-safety hub for Asia — identifying interested talent in Japan and routing it into international safety research. In practice, AI Safety Tokyo's central activity is its monthly *benkyoukai* (勉強会) reading-group-and-seminar, pitched at an interested technical layperson, and alongside that Rogers has built the annual [Technical AI Safety Conference (TAIS)](https://tais2024.cc/) as the group's international-facing research-conference surface — TAIS 2024 drew 105 in-person attendees and approximately 400 unique livestream viewers; TAIS 2025 followed in April 2025 at Tokyo Midtown Tower. The group's partnerships include the [AI Alignment Network](https://aisafety.tokyo/about) and [Effective Altruism Japan](https://forum.effectivealtruism.org/groups/pXKcrn3rsboDQL67K).

Beyond the reading group and conference, Rogers serves as Director of AI Safety at the [AI Industry Foundation](https://tais2024.cc/press/), and co-hosts the [Mindful Machines podcast](https://podcasts.apple.com/us/podcast/mindful-machines/id1749704881) with Van Nguyen — a show covering technical AI safety research. In March 2023 he [gave a TEDxOtemachi talk](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=irHTlQ99bVk) — "Is Chat-GPT trustworthy?" — introducing LLM limitations to a general audience, and in August 2024 he [spoke at the international student session of ICRES 2024 at Keio University](https://aialignmenttalkfromjapan.substack.com/p/ai-alignment-talk-from-japan-no11-4a4) on AI governance in Japan, covering Japan's soft-law preference, the Hiroshima AI Process, and the Basic Law for Promotion of Responsible AI.
