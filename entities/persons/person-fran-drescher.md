US actor, producer, and labour leader; [27th president of SAG-AFTRA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fran_Drescher) from 15 October 2021 to 12 September 2025, succeeding Gabrielle Carteris and succeeded by Sean Astin. She led the union — together with chief negotiator Duncan Crabtree-Ireland — through the [2023 actors' strike against the Alliance of Motion Picture and Television Producers](../campaigns/camp-wga-sag-aftra-ai-provisions-2023.md) and the subsequent 2024-25 video game strike that closed with AI protections in the Interactive Media Agreement.

Drescher was born on [30 September 1957](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fran_Drescher) in Queens, New York City, attended Hillcrest High School (graduating 1975) and Queens College (CUNY), and built a four-decade screen career — *Saturday Night Fever* (1977), *This Is Spinal Tap* (1984), and most centrally co-creating and starring in the CBS sitcom *The Nanny* (1993-1999) as Fran Fine — before turning to union politics. She founded the [Cancer Schmancer Movement](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fran_Drescher) on 21 June 2007 as a women's-cancer early-detection non-profit, and ran for SAG-AFTRA president in 2021 on the Unite for Strength slate, [winning](https://variety.com/2021/film/news/fran-drescher-president-sag-aftra-joely-fisher-1235054675/) 16,958 votes to Matthew Modine's 15,371 in the 2 September 2021 announcement and [being re-elected](https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/business/business-news/fran-drescher-reelected-sag-aftra-president-1235584524/) by 23,080 to 5,276 against Maya Gilbert-Dunbar in the 8 September 2023 second-term vote.

It is in her capacity as SAG-AFTRA president that Drescher belongs in this corpus. On [13 July 2023](https://deadline.com/2023/07/actors-strike-sag-aftra-hollywood-studios-1235436118/) the SAG-AFTRA National Board voted unanimously at the union's Los Angeles headquarters to call the union's first strike against the AMPTP since 1980 — the second leg of the joint 2023 Hollywood writers-and-actors stoppage and the [opening of the bargaining round](../campaigns/camp-wga-sag-aftra-ai-provisions-2023.md) that bound generative AI to enforceable contract language for the first time in major US collective bargaining. In the joint message to guild members ahead of the vote, Drescher and Crabtree-Ireland framed AI publicly [as](https://deadline.com/2023/07/actors-strike-sag-aftra-hollywood-studios-1235436118/) "an existential threat to creative professions" — the rhetorical anchor that the press cycle around the strike then attached to Drescher personally. Her [strike-call press conference](https://deadline.com/2023/07/fran-drescher-the-complete-sag-aftra-strike-speech-video-hollywood-1235437816/) that same day — with its "if we don't stand tall right now, we are all going to be in jeopardy of being replaced by machines and big business" framing and its "wrong side of history" rebuke of the studio CEOs — became the most-circulated mainstream-media articulation to that point of generative AI as a labour question, and the rhetorical pivot the corpus's later sectoral creative-industry organising ([Concept Art Association](../organizations/org-concept-art-association.md), [Authors Guild](../organizations/org-authors-guild.md), Graphic Artists Guild) has invoked since.

Outside the strike itself, Drescher [signed](https://deadline.com/2024/09/jane-fonda-shonda-rhimes-pedro-pascal-urge-gavin-newsom-sign-ai-safety-bill-open-letter-1236099942/) the 25 September 2024 Artists 4 Safe AI open letter urging California Governor Gavin Newsom to sign SB 1047, alongside Jane Fonda, Shonda Rhimes, Mark Ruffalo, Pedro Pascal, J.J. Abrams, Mark Hamill, Ava DuVernay, Mahershala Ali, Janelle Monáe, and Sean Astin among 125+ Hollywood signatories — placing her in the public record of the corpus's AI-safety / state-policy cluster as well as its creative-industry labour cluster. She [declined to run](https://variety.com/2025/film/news/fran-drescher-sag-aftra-president-step-aside-1236482680/) for a third SAG-AFTRA term in August 2025 and left office on 12 September 2025.
