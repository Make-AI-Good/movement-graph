Cape Town public-interest lawyer; Regional Director of the [Legal Resources Centre](../organizations/org-legal-resources-centre.md) Cape Town office and the organisation's [Programme Lead on Democratising Big Tech](https://lrc.org.za/who-we-are-lrc/). Her earlier career was in refugee, education-rights, and philanthropy-sector lawyering — [from 2007 to 2013 she managed the Refugee and Migrant Rights Programme at Lawyers for Human Rights in Durban](https://lrc.org.za/who-we-are-lrc/), before serving as senior attorney at the Equal Education Law Centre and as managing attorney at Harris Nupen Molebatsi Inc. (Philanthropy Law). She has [run the LRC's Cape Town regional office since at least 2019](https://twitter.com/lrc_southafrica/status/1166338995074281472) and through the COVID-era lockdown [represented the South African Human Rights Commission against the City of Cape Town](https://www.sahrc.org.za/index.php/sahrc-media/news/item/2432-legal-resources-centre-says-coct-has-a-track-record-of-being-anti-poor-racist) on eviction conduct she described as systemically anti-poor. On the platform-accountability side she sits on the Steering Group of the [Global Coalition for Tech Justice](https://digitalaction.co/projects/global-coalition-for-tech-justice-protecting-people-and-elections-from-big-tech-harms/) representing the LRC, and led the consortium investigation with Global Witness, Mozilla, and CIPIT into the major platforms' approval of harmful political ads during South Africa's 2024 election. In April 2026 she became [lead counsel](https://www.foxglove.org.uk/2026/04/29/foxglove-south-african-housing-movement-challenge-new-hyperscale-data-centre/) on the [Housing Assembly](../organizations/org-housing-assembly.md) and [Foxglove](../organizations/org-foxglove.md) formal objection to Equinix's 160 MVA hyperscale data-centre rezoning at King Air Industria, Cape Town, framing the application as a planning decision that "reproduce[s] inequality rather than redress[es] it."
