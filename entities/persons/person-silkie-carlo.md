# Silkie Carlo

UK civil-liberties campaigner and [Director](https://bigbrotherwatch.org.uk/team/) of [Big Brother Watch](../organizations/org-big-brother-watch.md) since [January 2018](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silkie_Carlo), the London-based civil-liberties organisation that has become the British anchor of the European civil-society campaign against live facial recognition. Carlo leads Big Brother Watch's work on state surveillance, policing technology, big data, internet regulation, and AI through what the organisation [describes](https://bigbrotherwatch.org.uk/team/) as a four-pillar mode of "parliamentary lobbying, strategic litigation, investigations and public campaigns to successfully change policies and laws", and is the corpus's most consistent named UK civil-society spokesperson on automated facial recognition, biometric mass surveillance, and AI-driven systems in policing and retail.

Before Big Brother Watch she was [Senior Advocacy Officer for Technology and Human Rights at Liberty](https://bigbrotherwatch.org.uk/team/), where she led the legal challenge to the Investigatory Powers Act 2016, and earlier worked on [Edward Snowden's official legal defence fund](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silkie_Carlo) at the Courage Foundation. She is the [co-author with Arjen Kamphuis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silkie_Carlo) of *Information Security for Journalists* (July 2014, commissioned by the Centre for Investigative Journalism) and has organised the CryptoPartyLDN series of public information-security workshops, alongside CryptoParties across the UK and Europe, since 2013. She holds a degree in [Politics and Psychology from the University of Cambridge](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silkie_Carlo).

Under Carlo, Big Brother Watch's [Stop Facial Recognition](https://bigbrotherwatch.org.uk/campaigns/stop-facial-recognition/) campaign — anchored on the [May 2018 *Face Off*](https://bigbrotherwatch.org.uk/wp-content/uploads/2018/05/Face-Off-final-digital-1.pdf) report on the Metropolitan Police's automated facial-recognition trials, the [May 2023 *Biometric Britain*](https://bigbrotherwatch.org.uk/2023/05/new-report-says-government-should-urgently-stop-live-facial-recognition-in-the-uk/) report on the 89%-wrong-match rate across the Metropolitan Police and South Wales Police operational deployments, and the [6 October 2023 *Joint Statement on UK Live Facial Recognition*](https://bigbrotherwatch.org.uk/press-releases/65-parliamentarians-call-for-immediate-stop-to-live-facial-recognition-surveillance/) signed by 65 cross-party parliamentarians and 31 rights and race-equality organisations — has become the [British anchor](../campaigns/camp-bbw-uk-stop-live-facial-recognition-coalition-2023.md) of the European civil-society position on remote biometric identification in publicly accessible spaces. Carlo is the campaign's named on-the-record voice, framing the 23 May 2023 *Biometric Britain* launch as putting "Britain at great risk of following in the footsteps of China and Russia's high-tech surveillance states, with no democratic mandate", and the 6 October 2023 cross-party joint statement as "the greatest involvement parliamentarians have ever had in Britain's approach to facial recognition surveillance".

She is also a named co-claimant alongside [Shaun Thompson](https://bigbrotherwatch.org.uk/campaigns/stop-facial-recognition/) — the Black anti-knife-crime community volunteer misidentified by Metropolitan Police live facial recognition at London Bridge in February 2024 — in the resulting legal challenge against the Metropolitan Police, the campaign's named individual-claimant test case.
