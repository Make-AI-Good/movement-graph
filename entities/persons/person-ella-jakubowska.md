# Ella Jakubowska

Brussels-based digital-rights policy advocate and [Head of Policy](https://edri.org/about-us/our-team/) at [European Digital Rights (EDRi)](../organizations/org-edri.md), the pan-European network of civil-society organisations defending rights and freedoms in the digital environment. Jakubowska leads the EDRi Brussels secretariat's [policy team](https://edri.org/about-us/our-team/) on state surveillance, platform power, and tech and society — with named specialisms in biometrics advocacy, the [EU Artificial Intelligence Act](../campaigns/camp-edri-eu-ai-act-fundamental-rights-coalition-2021-2024.md), the Child Sexual Abuse Regulation, and Prüm II — and is the corpus's named EDRi spokesperson on the Act's biometric provisions.

Jakubowska holds an [MSc in Human Rights](https://be.linkedin.com/in/ellajakubowska) from the London School of Economics and Political Science (distinction, 2018-2019), with [a focus on intersectional feminist sociology of science and technology](https://edri.org/about-us/our-team/); EDRi names her prior career as digital-transformation work at a global engineering company before her move into European digital-rights advocacy.

She has been the [coalition's most consistent public spokesperson](https://europeanaifund.org/newspublications/european-civil-society-on-the-ai-act-deal/) on the biometric track of the EDRi-coordinated EU AI Act civil-society coalition through the legislative cycle. She is named as a Policy Advisor contributor on the coalition's [30 November 2021 founding joint civil-society statement](https://edri.org/our-work/civil-society-calls-on-the-eu-to-put-fundamental-rights-first-in-the-ai-act/) *Civil society calls on the EU to put fundamental rights first in the AI Act*, was promoted to Senior Policy Advisor by the time of [EDRi's 9 December 2023 political-agreement public verdict](https://edri.org/our-work/eu-ai-act-deal-reached-but-too-soon-to-celebrate/) on the trilogue deal, and is now Head of Policy. Her verbatim public statement on the December 2023 political agreement — "it's hard to be excited about a law which has, for the first time in the EU, taken steps to legalise live public facial recognition across the bloc" — was the coalition's principal on-the-record framing of the Act's law-enforcement and migration-context carve-outs from the prohibition on remote biometric identification, and is cited in the [campaign body](../campaigns/camp-edri-eu-ai-act-fundamental-rights-coalition-2021-2024.md) as the coalition's reference public verdict on the biometric file.

Alongside the inter-institutional advocacy, Jakubowska's biometric-rights work at EDRi sits inside the [Reclaim Your Face](https://reclaimyourface.eu/goodbye-eci-hello-ai-act-negotiations/) coalition — the [EDRi-coordinated](../organizations/org-edri.md) European Citizens' Initiative against biometric mass surveillance in European public space, registered on 7 January 2021, running signature collection from 17 February 2021 through 1 August 2022 and gathering nearly 80,000 verified signatures, and the campaign whose framings ("ban", "remote biometric identification") are widely credited inside the coalition with entering the AI Act proposal text as a direct consequence of the mobilisation.
