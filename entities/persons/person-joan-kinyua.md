Kenyan data labeller, AI-supply-chain organiser, and [founding president](https://platform.coop/people/joan-kinyua/) of the [Data Labellers Association (DLA)](../organizations/org-data-labellers-association.md) — the Nairobi-headquartered worker-led representative body of African data annotators that [officially launched on 13 February 2025](https://data-workers.org/DLA/) under the theme "Empowering the People Powering AI." Kinyua entered the data-labelling sector in [2017](https://www.bmz-digital.global/en/5-questions-joan-kinyua/) and is identified across the launch-cycle press as a [former Sama data labeller](https://www.computerweekly.com/news/366619321/Kenyan-AI-workers-form-Data-Labelers-Association) with [over five years' working experience](https://platform.coop/people/joan-kinyua/) on assignments spanning self-driving cars, medical diagnostics, and content moderation.

The collective that became the DLA [began informally in late 2023](https://www.martijnarets.com/en/blog-en/data-labelers-association-speaks-up-for-invisible-workers-ultimately-its-about-respect-and-human-decency/) around a nucleus of ten Kenyan data annotators, and Kinyua's organising work in the months running up to its public surfacing established the DLA's posture inside Kenyan and international labour fora. She was a named signatory to the [May 2024 open letter from Kenyan tech workers to US President Biden](../publications/pub-open-letter-kenyan-tech-workers-biden-2024.md) — convened by [Foxglove](../organizations/org-foxglove.md) and timed to the US–Kenya Strategic Trade and Investment Partnership announced during President Ruto's state visit — with the [signature framing](https://www.foxglove.org.uk/2024/05/22/kenyan-tech-workers-president-rutos-us-visit/) "use your power to ensure protections for digital gig workers like us are baked into this new trade partnership." In [December 2024 she attended an International Labour Conference preparatory meeting in Naivasha](https://www.bmz-digital.global/en/5-questions-joan-kinyua/), and in the same window the still-forming DLA submitted proposals on Kenya's Business Laws (Amendment) Bill 2024 — the bill that thirty-five Kenyan tech workers would shortly take to the High Court as part of the wider [Africa Tech Workers Movement](../organizations/org-africa-tech-workers-movement.md) effort.

The 13 February 2025 launch in Nairobi [installed Kinyua as president](https://www.computerweekly.com/news/366619321/Kenyan-AI-workers-form-Data-Labelers-Association) of an association that drew 339 members in its first week alongside vice-president Ephantus Kanyugi and secretary Michael Geoffrey Abuyabo Asia. Her launch-day frame — ["AI does not exist on its own. Behind every algorithm, every dataset, and every technological advancement, there is invisible labor"](https://data-workers.org/DLA/) — consolidated the DLA's organising posture, and her [interview register](https://www.bmz-digital.global/en/5-questions-joan-kinyua/) has carried the politics of the work plainly ("We are the labour behind AI but remain excluded from its profits"; "We are no longer silent—we shape policy, demand change, and make the world see us"). Across press coverage she is the [DLA's most-quoted public face](https://www.computerweekly.com/news/366619321/Kenyan-AI-workers-form-Data-Labelers-Association) — "The workers power all these technological advancements, but they're paid peanuts and not even recognised" — and is identified in [continental coverage of the African data-worker movement](https://datasyn.substack.com/p/invisible-hands-visible-futures-the) as the founding president of a deliberately transnational rather than purely Kenyan formation. Her distinctive contribution to the make-AI-good movement is the consolidation, under her presidency, of an organisational vehicle through which Kenyan and pan-African data labellers — the non-AI public whose hand-classified examples underwrite the training of commercial AI systems — collectively shape the conditions of their work.
