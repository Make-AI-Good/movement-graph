Pakistani [lawyer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nighat_Dad), [founder and Executive Director](https://www.oversightboard.com/meet-the-board/) of the [Digital Rights Foundation](../organizations/org-digital-rights-foundation.md) (DRF) — the Lahore-headquartered Pakistani feminist digital-rights non-profit she [established in 2012](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nighat_Dad) as the country's first civil-society organisation specifically dedicated to feminist digital rights, technology-facilitated gender-based violence, and AI-and-gender concerns, and has continued to lead since.

[Born in Lahore in 1981](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nighat_Dad), Dad holds a Bachelor of Laws and a Master of Laws from the [University of the Punjab](https://www.oversightboard.com/meet-the-board/) and practised criminal and family law before founding DRF. Her working languages are [Urdu, Punjabi, English, and Sindhi](https://www.oversightboard.com/meet-the-board/), and her named expertise areas are digital rights, online safety, women's rights in South Asia, and gender and law. At DRF she anchors the organisation's Cyber Harassment Helpline, the [Hamara Internet](../messages/msg-hamara-internet.md) women's-digital-rights capacity-building campaign, the Digital 50.50 feminist e-magazine, the AI-and-gender research portfolio, and the policy-advocacy line on Pakistan's Prevention of Electronic Crimes Act and successive draft national AI policies.

Dad's international civil-society and platform-governance roles route DRF's Pakistani national feminist-tech register into the international institutional field. She [joined The Tor Project's board of directors in November 2018](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nighat_Dad); was [appointed to Facebook's content Oversight Board on 6 May 2020](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nighat_Dad); was [named to the United Nations Secretary-General's High-Level Advisory Body on AI](https://www.un.org/en/ai-advisory-body/members) in 2023; and sits on the Reporters Without Borders Information and Democracy Commission. Her awards include [TIME magazine's *Next Generation Leaders* listing in 2015](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nighat_Dad), the Atlantic Council Digital Freedom Award and the Dutch government's [Human Rights Tulip award in 2016](https://www.dawn.com/news/1299175) — whose €100,000 prize money was pledged within weeks of receipt to launch DRF's Cyber Harassment Helpline — and a [TED Fellowship](https://www.oversightboard.com/meet-the-board/).
