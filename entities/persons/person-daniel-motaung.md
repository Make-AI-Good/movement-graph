South African content-moderation worker turned whistleblower and labour organiser; lead plaintiff in the first major lawsuit brought against Meta in Kenya over the conditions of its outsourced Facebook content moderation. From the [Free State province](https://researchictafrica.net/2023/12/01/daniel-motaung-how-a-man-from-the-free-state-came-to-take-on-facebook/) of South Africa and a [2018 Rhodes University graduate](https://researchictafrica.net/2023/12/01/daniel-motaung-how-a-man-from-the-free-state-came-to-take-on-facebook/), he relocated to Nairobi in [March 2019](https://researchictafrica.net/2023/12/01/daniel-motaung-how-a-man-from-the-free-state-came-to-take-on-facebook/) to moderate Zulu-language Facebook content for Samasource (now Sama) and was fired after [co-founding the Alliance](https://www.foxglove.org.uk/2023/05/15/nairobi-content-moderation-summit/), an in-facility workers' organisation pressing for better mental-health support and pay. In [May 2022](https://www.opendemocracy.net/en/5050/whistleblower-daniel-motaung-facebook-kenya-court-case/) he filed suit in Nairobi against Meta and Sama alleging forced labour, human trafficking, and union-busting, represented in Kenya by [Mercy Mutemi](../persons/person-mercy-mutemi.md) of [Nzili & Sumbi Advocates](../organizations/org-nzili-sumbi-advocates.md) with backing from [Foxglove](../organizations/org-foxglove.md); a Kenyan judge declined Meta's bid for a gag order against him. The Kenyan Court of Appeal [rejected Meta's jurisdictional challenge on 20 September 2024](https://www.foxglove.org.uk/2024/09/23/facebook-content-moderators-kenya-meta-appeal/), allowing his case to proceed to trial alongside a parallel suit by 185 mass-dismissed moderators; both rulings were [postponed in early 2026](https://www.business-humanrights.org/en/latest-news/kenya-court-postpones-ruling-in-two-cases-against-meta-brought-by-former-content-moderators-delaying-trial-2/). At the [May 2023 Nairobi summit](https://www.foxglove.org.uk/2023/05/15/nairobi-content-moderation-summit/) of more than 150 African moderators for Facebook, TikTok, and ChatGPT, Motaung — by then in exile from Kenya — returned to address the gathering as workers voted to establish the first African Content Moderators Union, telling them: "I never thought, when I started the Alliance in 2019, we would be here today."
