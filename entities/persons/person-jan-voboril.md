# Jan Vobořil

Mgr. et Mgr. Jan Vobořil, Ph.D. is [Executive Director and principal lawyer](https://edri.org/our-work/for-the-right-to-analog-iure-strengthens-the-digital-freedoms-program/) at [Iuridicum Remedium (IuRe)](../organizations/org-iure.md), Prague, [where he has worked since 2009](https://www.ochranademokracie.cz/lide/jan-voboril). Trained at Charles University Prague in law and economic and social history, he leads the Czech Republic's sole digital-rights organisation across strategic litigation, AI Act enforcement, and public-mobilisation campaigns. He holds a seat on the [Government Council for Human Rights' Committee for Human Rights and Modern Technology](https://www.ochranademokracie.cz/lide/jan-voboril), where he advocates for analogue alternatives in state digitalisation, and on the Committee for the Rights of Older People.

Vobořil's most consequential file is IuRe's two-decade campaign against blanket metadata retention. In [January 2021 IuRe crowdfunded a civil lawsuit](https://edri.org/our-work/lure-data-retention-czech-republic/) with journalist Jan Cibulka as plaintiff and Vobořil as lead counsel, challenging six-month retention of communications metadata by Czech telecoms operators; Vobořil named the documented stakes: "Data has been misused in the past, either to obtain information about contacts of influential people, to identify participants of anti-government demonstrations, or to identify journalistic sources." After a 2024 [Czech Supreme Court grant of appeal](https://edri.org/our-work/the-blanket-collection-of-metadata-on-communications-in-the-czech-republic-is-illegal-iuridicum-remedium-wins-data-retention-dispute/), an April 2025 Municipal Court ruling found Czech data retention directly contradicts European law — judge's summary: ["The state has failed"](https://edri.org/our-work/the-blanket-collection-of-metadata-on-communications-in-the-czech-republic-is-illegal-iuridicum-remedium-wins-data-retention-dispute/) — and found the processing itself constitutes individual harm, opening the path for financial compensation claims by other affected Czech citizens. Alongside the data-retention file, Vobořil directs IuRe's Big Brother Awards Czech Republic (running annually since 2005), the [Right to Analogue campaign](https://edri.org/our-work/for-the-right-to-analog-iure-strengthens-the-digital-freedoms-program/) using strategic litigation to protect access to cash payments and non-digital public services, and public education work on biometric surveillance including the ["Czech Republic is not China"](https://edri.org/our-work/biometric-surveillance-in-the-czech-republic-the-ministry-of-the-interior-is-trying-to-circumvent-the-artificial-intelligence-act/) campaign opposing the Czech Ministry of the Interior's attempt to extend AI Act–prohibited facial recognition authority beyond airports.
