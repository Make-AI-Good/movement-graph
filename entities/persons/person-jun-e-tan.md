# Jun-E Tan

Jun-E Tan is a Kuala Lumpur-based policy researcher with a PhD in Communication from Nanyang Technological University (NTU), Singapore, and a research focus on AI governance, digital rights, and human rights in Southeast Asia. She is a Senior Research Associate at Khazanah Research Institute (KRI), the Malaysian policy research institute, [focusing on digital and tech policy](https://jun-etan.com/research/) in Malaysia and the wider Southeast Asian region, and sits on the Board of Directors of CETDEM (Centre of Environment, Technology & Development, Malaysia) since 2015 and on the Feminist Principles for Digital Justice Working Group.

Tan's collaboration with [EngageMedia](../organizations/org-engagemedia.md) on AI governance began with a [three-part article series on Coconet](https://www.apc.org/en/news/coconet-artificial-intelligence-and-human-rights-southeast-asia-overview) co-published from December 2019 to March 2020, which laid the analytical scaffolding — digital-authoritarianism patterns, dataset representation gaps, civil-society participation gaps in AI governance — that the 2021 report consolidated. She authored *[Governance of Artificial Intelligence (AI) in Southeast Asia](../publications/pub-engagemedia-governance-of-ai-southeast-asia.md)* as Research Consultant to EngageMedia; the report [developed the ethics-washing critique](https://jun-etan.com/posts/2021-12-15-new-report-out/) of AI governance frameworks across ASEAN and called on regional civil society to push for binding legal regulation alongside non-binding ethics principles. A revised edition was issued in June 2023 and the report continues to circulate as the standing reference document in EngageMedia's AI-and-human-rights research line.
