# Juliet Nanfuka

Juliet Nanfuka is Research and Communications Officer at CIPESA (Collaboration on International ICT Policy for East and Southern Africa), based in Kampala, Uganda. She conducts digital rights research, training, advocacy, and communications work at CIPESA, with a professional focus on the intersections of society, gender, media, and digital content with tech policy in Africa. She holds a Bachelor of Journalism from Rhodes University in South Africa and a Post Graduate Certificate in Social Innovation Management from the Amani Institute in Nairobi.

Nanfuka [authored](https://cipesa.org/2025/09/state-of-internet-freedom-in-africa-report/) the [*State of Internet Freedom in Africa 2025: Navigating the Implications of AI on Digital Democracy in Africa*](../publications/pub-cipesa-state-of-internet-freedom-africa-2025.md) — a 14-country qualitative survey of AI's implications for digital democracy published in September 2025 as the 12th annual edition of CIPESA's State of Internet Freedom in Africa report series — and authored the Uganda Digital Rights Landscape Report for the African Digital Rights Network (ADRN), where she serves as Treasurer. She was named a [2018 Open Internet Leader](https://openinternet.global/leaders/2018-leaders/juliet-nanfuka) by the Open Internet for Democracy initiative.
