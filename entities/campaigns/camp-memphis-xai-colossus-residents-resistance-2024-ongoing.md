# Memphis Colossus Resistance (2024–ongoing)

From June 2024 onward, residents and environmental-justice organisations in South Memphis have mounted a sustained multi-front campaign against xAI's Colossus supercomputing facilities — first Colossus 1 in the historically Black Boxtown neighbourhood, then the Colossus 2 expansion in Southaven, Mississippi — challenging the facilities' use of dozens of methane gas turbines operated without air quality permits in communities already carrying a severe industrial pollution burden. The campaign is led by Memphis Community Against Pollution (MCAP), with Young, Gifted & Green, the NAACP, and the Sierra Club as key coalition partners, and is backed by the Southern Environmental Law Center and Earthjustice in the legal channels. It sits within the broader U.S. community resistance to AI data-centre infrastructure that [Kairos Fellowship](../organizations/org-kairos.md) and [MediaJustice](../organizations/org-mediajustice.md) have built out nationally through the [Fight Data Centers](camp-kairos-mediajustice-fight-data-centers-2024-ongoing.md) programme, and is identified in the MediaJustice programme as its most prominent active local fight.

## Boxtown and the sacrifice-zone context

Boxtown is a working-class neighbourhood in South Memphis, approximately 90% Black, first settled by formerly enslaved people in 1863. By the time xAI arrived, [the neighbourhood already hosted 22 of Tennessee's 30 largest industrial polluters](https://insideclimatenews.org/news/17072025/elon-musk-xai-data-center-gas-turbines-memphis/), and the [cancer risk for Boxtown residents is four times the national average](https://tennesseelookout.com/2025/07/07/a-billionaire-an-ai-supercomputer-toxic-emissions-and-a-memphis-community-that-did-nothing-wrong/). State Rep. Justin J. Pearson — a Memphis Democrat whose brother KeShaun Pearson directs MCAP — has noted that African Americans in the U.S. are 75% more likely to live near a toxic hazardous waste facility, and has called the xAI siting a direct continuation of that pattern. The neighbourhood is the kind of community that environmental-justice advocates call a "sacrifice zone" — whose residents bear the concentrated costs of industrial operations whose benefits flow elsewhere.

## The arrival of Colossus and the unpermitted turbines

In June 2024 the Greater Memphis Chamber of Commerce [announced xAI's Colossus as the "largest capital investment by a new-to-market company" in the city's history](https://www.memphisflyer.com/a-peoples-history-of-the-fight-against-xai/), siting the facility at the former Electrolux plant in South Memphis. MCAP began hosting community meetings that same month. Community members and watchdog organisations were the first to notice rows of methane gas turbines appearing on site without any air quality permits. Aerial photography and thermal imaging eventually confirmed 33 of 35 turbines running under heat — far more than the 15 that xAI and Memphis Mayor Paul Young publicly acknowledged, with the mayor's office initially characterising the turbines as backup power only. On 2 September 2024 Musk announced that xAI had brought "the world's biggest supercomputer" online.

The turbine numbers mattered because [methane gas turbines are major emitters of nitrogen oxides (NOx)](https://www.selc.org/news/inside-memphis-fight-against-xai/), a family of toxic chemicals harmful to the lungs and a key contributor to smog. The Southern Environmental Law Center later characterised Colossus 1 as "likely the largest industrial emitter" of nitrogen oxides in Memphis — a city that already fails federal air quality standards. MCAP [raised $250,000 to conduct its own community-science response](https://www.memphiscap.org/mcapcampaigns/blog-post-title-three-4frah), installing nine independent air-quality monitoring devices around the facility; readings consistently showed dangerous pollution levels.

## The permit dispute

SELC wrote to the Shelby County Health Department (SCHD) in 2024 on behalf of MCAP, Young Gifted & Green, and the Sierra Club, calling for an investigation and enforcement action against the unpermitted turbines. In July 2024 a coalition of environmental groups separately urged the Tennessee Valley Authority to deny xAI power contracts. On 2 July 2025 the SCHD issued an air quality permit allowing 15 turbines to operate 24 hours a day, seven days a week — despite hundreds of public comments opposing the draft permit and without addressing the dozens of additional unpermitted turbines the community had documented. SELC called the decision a failure of the regulating agency charged with protecting Memphis families.

SELC filed a formal appeal of the permit with the Memphis and Shelby County Air Pollution Control Board on behalf of the NAACP and Young, Gifted & Green. The appeal sought to have xAI's permit rescinded and the unpermitted turbines addressed. In December 2025 the board dismissed the appeal. KeShaun Pearson, executive director of MCAP, described the regulator's posture as allowing communities whose children are already hospitalised for asthma at the highest rate in Tennessee to be exposed to yet more industrial pollution: ["Our local leaders are entrusted with protecting us from corporations violating our right to clean air, but we are witnessing their failure to do so."](https://insideclimatenews.org/news/17072025/elon-musk-xai-data-center-gas-turbines-memphis/)

## Escalation to federal litigation: Colossus 2

While the Memphis permit fight proceeded, xAI expanded south across the state line, constructing Colossus 2 in Southaven, Mississippi, with the same pattern: 27 methane gas turbines installed and operated without any air permits. On 13 February 2026 SELC and Earthjustice sent xAI a notice of intent to sue on behalf of the national NAACP and the Mississippi State Conference of the NAACP, citing violations of the federal Clean Air Act. The Colossus 2 power plant has the potential to emit [more than 1,700 tons of NOx per year](https://earthjustice.org/case/xai-illegal-gas-power-plant-data-center-colossus) — likely making it the largest industrial source of NOx in the greater Memphis metropolitan area when the two sites are considered together.

On 14 April 2026 the NAACP filed a federal Clean Air Act lawsuit against xAI and its subsidiary MZX Tech, represented by SELC and Earthjustice. The suit asks the court to declare xAI in violation of the Clean Air Act, to order the company to stop operating unpermitted turbines at the Southaven facility, to require installation of best available control technology, and to assess financial penalties for each day of illegal operation. In April 2026 NAACP also asked for emergency court action to stop the illegal pollution immediately. Senator Sheldon Whitehouse (D-RI) wrote in the same month [calling for answers about xAI's pattern of operating illegal data-centre gas plants](https://www.epw.senate.gov/public/index.cfm/2026/4/whitehouse-calls-for-answers-about-musk-backed-xai-s-pattern-of-operating-illegal-data-center-gas-plants) across its facilities.

## Direct action and student organising

The legal and regulatory channels have run in parallel with community direct action. On 5 May 2026 a multi-group coalition [blocked the main driveway into Colossus 1 for two hours](https://mlk50.com/2026/05/05/this-is-not-acceptable-memphis-activists-block-xai-operations-to-protest-pollution/) in a die-in — protesters lying across the entrance and wearing matching grey respirator masks, both to protect themselves from the turbines' emissions and to make visible what living next to the facility means. Activist Jessica Wainfor stated: "We need to make it known that this is not acceptable — having no regulation, polluting water, polluting air is not acceptable." Participants represented environmental and social-justice groups from across the metro area; their stated goal was for xAI and other major polluters to leave Memphis entirely.

Tigers Against Pollution — a university student coalition in Memphis — organised separately, holding a march toward the Colossus 2 / Whitehaven site and holding anti-xAI rallies in protest of the second facility. The student coalition's naming of Whitehaven (an additional South Memphis neighbourhood) as the site for Colossus 2 amplified the geographic spread of the campaign's concern beyond the original Boxtown fight.

A distinct coalition win on the water dimension: MCAP, Young Gifted & Green, Protect Our Aquifer, and the Sierra Club Chickasaw Group [secured a commitment from xAI and Memphis Light, Gas and Water (MLGW)](https://www.sierraclub.org/wastewater-to-cool-xai) for a tertiary water treatment plant to be completed within eight months — a discrete result on the facilities' water-use impacts.

## Place in the make-AI-good movement

The Memphis Colossus Resistance is the corpus's most sustained single-community fight against a specific AI company's physical infrastructure, and the clearest case in the U.S. where a frontline environmental-justice community — rather than a national campaign organisation — is the primary driver of resistance to AI infrastructure. It extends the `strat-local-fight-against-ai-data-centers` strategy into a register the national Kairos / MediaJustice programme names but does not itself run: the inside-the-fence, multi-year fight by the affected neighbourhood directly against a named AI operator's specific named facilities. The combination of community science (independent air monitoring), regulatory challenge (permit appeal), federal litigation (Clean Air Act suit), and nonviolent direct action (die-in blockade) in one sustained campaign is a template for how a place-based fight combines pressure channels.

The xAI dimension also matters for the corpus's framing. Colossus 1 and 2 are not generic Big Tech data centres; they are explicitly xAI's AI training supercomputer infrastructure. The campaign's target is named AI infrastructure with a named AI company, making it one of the few U.S. campaigns in the corpus in which the "AI" label in the target is primary rather than incidental. State Rep. Justin J. Pearson's ["death sentence"](https://mlk50.com/2026/04/17/residents-say-musks-ai-supercomputer-is-a-death-sentence-for-memphis-communities/) framing — that Musk's AI supercomputer is a "death sentence" for Memphis communities — supplies the campaign's headline rhetorical frame connecting the AI-infrastructure narrative to the life-and-death environmental stakes the Boxtown residents face.
