# Calouste Gulbenkian Foundation

The Calouste Gulbenkian Foundation is a Portuguese private foundation of public utility [established on 18 July 1956](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Calouste_Gulbenkian_Foundation) per the will of Armenian-British oil magnate Calouste Sarkis Gulbenkian and headquartered in Lisbon, with a UK branch in London and a science institute (Instituto Gulbenkian de Ciência). With total assets of approximately [€3.5 billion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Calouste_Gulbenkian_Foundation) and annual programme spending of approximately €100 million, the Foundation operates under four statutory aims — arts, social welfare, education, and science — and since 2023 has organised its programme work into seven multi-year portfolios: Access to Care, Access to Culture, Access to Education, Climate and Ocean, [Democracy and Civil Society](https://gulbenkian.pt/en/democracy-and-civil-society/), Armenian Communities, and Partnerships with Africa. The Foundation is led by President António M. Feijó, who assumed the presidency in May 2022 as the first president drawn from arts and humanities. The Democracy and Civil Society programme is the entry point for this corpus: it runs through a co-managed civil-society grant fund (the European Media and Information Fund), a domestic civil-society capacity-building programme (the Active Citizens Fund), and a partnership with the [Civitates](fund-civitates.md) European philanthropy network.

## European Media and Information Fund — the AI-democracy throughline

The [European Media and Information Fund (EMIF)](https://gulbenkian.pt/emifund/) is a civil-society-facing grant programme co-managed by the Calouste Gulbenkian Foundation and the European University Institute (Florence), [established with an initial €25 million, five-year contribution from Google](https://gulbenkian.pt/emifund/news/emif-awards-e5-7m-for-projects-fighting-disinformation-in-2022/). EMIF's stated mission is to support fact-checking and media literacy initiatives in Europe and address online disinformation across the European information ecosystem, with four priority areas: fact-checking initiatives, multidisciplinary investigations on disinformation, research studies and sandboxes, and media literacy programming. The AI connection runs explicitly through EMIF's research mandate — the fund explicitly [funds work examining the effects of digital media and AI technologies on human cognition, civic behavior, social interactions, and democratic participation](https://gulbenkian.pt/emifund/), including projects that use algorithmic tools to detect and map disinformation campaigns. In its [2022 grant round EMIF awarded €5.7 million](https://gulbenkian.pt/emifund/news/emif-awards-e5-7m-for-projects-fighting-disinformation-in-2022/) to projects fighting disinformation, with civil society organisations and academic research groups as the primary recipient categories. The coordination architecture connects EMIF to the European Digital Media Observatory (EDMO), the EU's institutional hub for coordinating disinformation-fighting civil society across member states, which embeds EMIF inside the broader structure of European civil-society AI accountability work. The Foundation's role is co-management — not merely capital contribution — making it structurally embedded in EMIF's design and delivery rather than a passive funder.

## Active Citizens Fund — domestic grassroots engagement

The [Active Citizens Fund](https://gulbenkian.pt/cidadaos-ativos/en/about-the-programme/) (2018–2024) is the Gulbenkian Foundation's direct domestic civil-society-building programme in Portugal, disbursing [€11.5 million across 182 civil society organisations](https://gulbenkian.pt/en/news/11-million-euros-to-strengthen-portuguese-civil-society/) and reaching over 65,000 individuals. The fund was co-financed through the European Economic Area Financial Mechanisms (the EEA and Norway Grants programme channelling funds from Iceland, Liechtenstein, and Norway to EU member-state civil society), with the Gulbenkian Foundation serving as the national fund manager for Portugal. Its four focus areas are: democracy, active citizenship, good governance, and transparency; human rights, equality, and anti-discrimination; social justice and inclusion of vulnerable groups; and NGO capacity-building. The programme's participatory design philosophy — embedding beneficiaries in initiative design rather than imposing programme structure from above — was documented by Philea (the European philanthropy network) as a case study in participatory grant management. Taken alone, the ACF is a civil-society-building programme without a strong AI axis; its relevance to this corpus is as the grassroots-engagement dimension of the two-axis funder test, paired with EMIF's AI-democracy throughline.

## Civitates partnership

The Gulbenkian Foundation is a [foundation partner of Civitates](https://civitates-eu.org/foundation-partners/), the Brussels-based pooled European civil-society philanthropy vehicle that operates three sub-funds: Civic Power (strengthening civil society in EU priority countries), Tech and Democracy (supporting civil society enforcement of the Digital Services Act and EU AI and platform regulations), and Media (independent media and investigative journalism). [Civitates](fund-civitates.md) pools contributions from approximately 30 European foundations specifically to fund civil-society enforcement of EU digital and AI regulation; Gulbenkian's membership in the partnership pool is an institutional alignment signal alongside and reinforcing the EMIF co-management relationship. The overlap between EMIF (disinformation and media literacy, AI effects on democratic participation) and Civitates' Tech and Democracy sub-fund (EU AI regulation, DSA enforcement, platform accountability) makes the Gulbenkian Foundation one of the more consistently AI-democracy-oriented of the large European private foundations in the corpus.

## Position in the corpus funder slice

Within the corpus's funder slice, the Calouste Gulbenkian Foundation closes the Iberian Peninsula and Southern Europe geo gap — no other entry is headquartered in Portugal, Spain, or the broader Iberian region — and fills a structurally distinct institutional slot among the European funder cluster. The European funder cluster in the corpus divides principally between Brussels-pooled re-granting vehicles ([Civitates](fund-civitates.md), [European AI & Society Fund](fund-european-ai-society-fund.md)), multi-programme continental foundations ([Robert Bosch Stiftung](fund-robert-bosch-stiftung.md), [Stiftung Mercator](fund-stiftung-mercator.md)), and Nordic freedom-of-expression foundations. The Gulbenkian Foundation sits between these clusters: it participates in the pooled vehicle ([Civitates](fund-civitates.md)) while co-managing its own European-level civil-society grant fund (EMIF) and running a separate domestic grassroots-capacity programme (ACF). The combination of co-managed European-level civil-society fund, pooled European philanthropy partnership, and domestic grassroots investment makes it the most structurally complex of the European funder entries, and the only one anchored in Southern Europe.

The Foundation's AI-good footprint is concentrated on AI's effects on democratic participation and civil-society media literacy — a European civil-society media-and-democracy vector distinct from the algorithmic-accountability vector (strategic litigation, regulatory enforcement), the AI-safety movement vector (PauseAI, Center for AI Safety), and the AI-labour-rights vector (content moderator organizing, gig worker rights) that dominate other portions of the corpus. The EMIF and its EDMO coordination position the Gulbenkian Foundation at the intersection of European AI governance (the regulatory architecture of the DSA, the EU AI Act) and civil-society capacity to engage that architecture — the demand side of the make-AI-good movement's European institutional engagement.
