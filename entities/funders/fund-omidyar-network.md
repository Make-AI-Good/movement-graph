# Omidyar Network

Omidyar Network is a US-headquartered "philanthropic investment firm" [established in 2004 by eBay founder Pierre Omidyar and his wife Pam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Omidyar_Network), [dedicated to "harnessing the power of markets to create opportunities for people to improve their lives"](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pierre_Omidyar). It operates as [a 501(c)(3) and a Limited Liability Company, and part of The Omidyar Group](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Omidyar_Network) — a dual structure that pairs traditional grantmaking with mission-related investment in for-profit ventures, distinguishing it sharply from the endowed private foundations that anchor most of the rest of the corpus's funder slice. The Network operates from [Redwood City, California, with a Washington, DC office](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Omidyar_Network), reports having [awarded "almost $2 billion to more than 700 organizations"](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Omidyar_Network) by 2025, and is led by CEO Mike Kubzansky (since 2018) and President Michele Jawando (from 2025; previously Senior Vice President for Programs). The cumulative scale stretches across both arms of the dual structure: Pierre Omidyar's [Wikipedia biographical article](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pierre_Omidyar) reports more than $992 million committed to for-profit companies and nonprofit organisations.

## A network-philanthropy parent template

Omidyar Network's most consequential structural contribution to the AI-good funder field is its role as a *parent template* — the originating vehicle from which several of the corpus's most relevant grantmaking organisations were spun out as independent foundations. The pattern is consistent: an in-house programmatic initiative reaches the scale and clarity to operate on its own, and Omidyar Network launches it as a structurally independent entity. The [Democracy Fund](fund-democracy-fund.md) is the corpus's clearest example: [established by Pierre Omidyar in 2011](https://democracyfund.org/who-we-are/financials/) inside Omidyar Network and incubated for three years under Joe Goldman as Investment Director before [launching as an independent private foundation in July 2014](https://democracyfund.org/who-we-are/financials/) focused on US democracy. Luminate is the second clearest: [spun off in 2018 from Omidyar Network's Governance & Citizen Engagement Initiative](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Omidyar_Network) as an independent international foundation with a Civic Empowerment, Data + Digital Rights, Independent Media, and Financial Transparency remit. Three sectoral spin-offs followed — [Flourish (2019, from Financial Inclusion), Imaginable Futures (2020, from the education portfolio), and PlaceFund (January 2020, on property rights)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Omidyar_Network) — alongside [Spero Ventures](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Omidyar_Network) (March 2018, a generalist venture capital firm). Each spin-off inherits a portion of Omidyar Network's grantmaking authority and operates under its own programme staff and governance; collectively, they make Omidyar Network the closest thing in modern US philanthropy to an *incubator* for civil-society-facing single-issue foundations.

Within The Omidyar Group, the Network has also operated regional and sectoral arms directly. Omidyar Network [India was established in 2010 to invest in Indian start-ups](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Omidyar_Network), managing approximately $673 million across 1mg, Bounce, Vedantu, Bijak, DealShare, Doubtnut, Entri, HealthKart, Indifi, M2P, and Pratilipi by July 2023; [its closure was announced in December 2023, with operations ceasing by end of 2024](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Omidyar_Network). The wind-down concentrates the Network's present-day operational footprint on its US base while leaving the global tech-governance and digital-rights remit to Luminate.

## AI-good footprint

Omidyar Network's AI-good footprint is built around four named threads. The most visible is the [1 November 2023 ten-foundation Public Interest AI initiative](https://www.opensocietyfoundations.org/newsroom/open-society-and-other-funders-launch-new-initiative-to-ensure-ai-advances-the-public-interest) — a coalition announced in coordination with Vice President Kamala Harris during the global AI Safety Summit week. Omidyar Network is [named among the ten co-launching foundations](https://www.opensocietyfoundations.org/newsroom/open-society-and-other-funders-launch-new-initiative-to-ensure-ai-advances-the-public-interest), alongside the [Ford Foundation](fund-ford-foundation.md), the [MacArthur Foundation](fund-macarthur-foundation.md), the [Mozilla Foundation](fund-mozilla-foundation.md), the [Open Society Foundations](fund-open-society-foundations.md), [Democracy Fund](fund-democracy-fund.md), the David and Lucile Packard Foundation, Heising-Simons Foundation, Kapor Foundation, and the Wallace Global Fund, with a [collective commitment of more than $200 million toward public-interest efforts to mitigate AI harms and promote responsible use and innovation](https://www.fordfoundation.org/news-and-stories/news-and-press/news/philanthropies-launch-new-initiative-to-ensure-ai-advances-the-public-interest/). The coalition organises its grantmaking around the five focus areas Harris identified: protecting democracy and rights, public-interest innovation, workers' rights and labour standards, transparency and accountability of AI models and companies, and the development of international AI governance frameworks and norms. The Public Interest AI initiative places Omidyar Network inside the same coordinated philanthropic field as five of its own spin-offs' and grantees' funders — and ties its AI-good commitment explicitly to the democracy-and-rights pillar that aligns with Democracy Fund's standing programme architecture and Luminate's Data + Digital Rights pillar.

The second named thread is the [2024 launch of the Data Empowerment Fund](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Omidyar_Network), a new programmatic vehicle to support initiatives promoting greater individual and community control over information privacy. The Data Empowerment Fund is the Network's principal in-house AI-and-data-governance grantmaking line and the most recent expression of its responsible-technology agenda. The third named thread is the [2024 purchase, alongside the Ford Foundation and the Cummings Foundation, of a stake in generative AI firm Anthropic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Omidyar_Network) — a mission-related investment that distinguishes Omidyar Network's LLC arm from the pure-grantmaking posture of the corpus's other funders and tests the responsible-AI-investment thesis directly at the foundation-model layer.

The fourth thread runs through Omidyar Network's prior support of pooled and partnership infrastructure that the corpus already tracks. [CIPESA's March 2025 Round Nine announcement of the African Digital Rights Fund](https://cipesa.org/2025/03/african-digital-rights-funding-crisis/) names Omidyar Network among the [African Digital Rights Fund](fund-african-digital-rights-fund.md)'s historical supporters alongside the [Open Society Foundations](fund-open-society-foundations.md), the Hewlett Foundation, CIPE, Sida, GIZ, and the New Venture Fund. And the [Nuffield Foundation's 28 March 2018 launch announcement for the Ada Lovelace Institute](https://www.nuffieldfoundation.org/news/the-nuffield-foundation-announces-new-5-million-ada-lovelace-institute) names "Omidyar Network's Governance & Citizen Engagement Initiative (now Luminate)" among the [Ada Lovelace Institute](../organizations/org-ada-lovelace-institute.md)'s founding partner organisations — anchoring Omidyar Network's pre-Luminate role in seeding the UK's principal AI-and-society research and policy institute. (Continuing Ada Lovelace funder relationships travel with Luminate, the structural successor; Omidyar Network's `funded_orgs[]` is left empty per the schema's canonical-direction rule that Org ↔ Funder is stored on the org side.)

## Position in the corpus funder slice

Within the funder slice of this corpus Omidyar Network fills a specific structural slot that none of the existing entries occupies. It is the first *tech-fortune-anchored network philanthropy* parent template — distinct from the [Mozilla Foundation](fund-mozilla-foundation.md) (a tech-product-anchored nonprofit whose AI work runs through its commercial-technology sister), distinct from the [Ford Foundation](fund-ford-foundation.md), [MacArthur Foundation](fund-macarthur-foundation.md), and [Open Society Foundations](fund-open-society-foundations.md) (large endowed multi-decade foundations operating with diversified historical capital bases), and distinct from [Democracy Fund](fund-democracy-fund.md) (itself an Omidyar Network spin-off, but operating as a US-incorporated single-issue private foundation rather than as a network philanthropy parent template). It is also the first funder in the corpus structured as a dual 501(c)(3) and LLC vehicle, the first that pursues impact through *mission-related investment* alongside grantmaking, and the first whose primary contribution to the AI-good field travels as much through its independent spin-offs as through its direct grantmaking. The [European AI & Society Fund](fund-european-ai-society-fund.md) is the structural sibling on the pooled-fund / re-granting-vehicle side; Omidyar Network is the structural sibling on the parent-template / spin-off-incubator side. Where the [African Digital Rights Fund](fund-african-digital-rights-fund.md) is the corpus's principal window onto how organised philanthropy flows capital to regional civil-society infrastructure, Omidyar Network is the corpus's principal window onto how a single tech-fortune-anchored philanthropy *spawns* the independent foundations that subsequently take up named positions inside the AI-good funder field.
