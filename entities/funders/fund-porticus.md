# Porticus

Porticus is an Amsterdam-headquartered philanthropic organisation [established in 1995](https://www.porticus.com/en/our-roots) to coordinate and advise the charitable activities of the Brenninkmeijer family business owners — the founding family of the global clothing retailer C&A. When brothers Clemens and August Brenninkmeijer [opened their first store in Sneek in 1841](https://www.porticus.com/en/our-roots), they recorded a donation "to the poor" in the family ledger within months of launching, embedding a model of profit-sharing with local communities from the outset; their philanthropy was [rooted in Catholic faith](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brenninkmeijer_family) and a conviction of partnership and brotherhood. By 1963 the family's growing philanthropic activity across multiple national foundations required [a full-time coordinator in Amsterdam](https://www.porticus.com/en/our-roots); in 1995 these efforts were [consolidated into Porticus](https://www.porticus.com/en/about-us) as the central advisory body, advising and supervising grants from four family foundations — Clementia Verein, Stichting Benevolentia, Stiftung Auxilium, and Good Energies Foundation — now organised under the family's Constanter charitable holding. The Brenninkmeijer family's cumulative charitable giving is reported to have [exceeded one billion euros to the Catholic church alone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brenninkmeijer_family), with the family directing approximately [10% of profit through some 58 foundations worldwide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brenninkmeijer_family). Charlotte Brenninkmeijer became CEO in [November 2024](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brenninkmeijer_family).

## Scale and structure

Porticus [operates from 14 global offices with approximately 150 staff](https://www.porticus.com/who-we-are/), maintaining partnerships in over 100 countries — a footprint that places it among the largest family-foundation philanthropic networks in continental Europe by geographic reach. The organisation is not an endowed grantmaking foundation in its own right; it advises and supervises grants made by the family foundations noted above, co-designing programmes and grant strategies with partner organisations, evaluating proposals, and managing multi-year philanthropic commitments across a portfolio of roughly [2,000 projects annually in nearly 90 countries](https://www.fundraiso.com/en/organisations/porticus-foundation). Grantmaking is [invite-only](https://www.porticus.com/how-we-work/) — "Porticus cannot accept unsolicited requests for grants" — with programme managers proactively identifying and approaching aligned civil-society organisations. Grant sizes are [explicitly flexible](https://www.porticus.com/how-we-work/): "some programmes require large, multi-year funding to realise change, whereas other projects only require limited one-time funding," and co-design with partners — including participatory monitoring and iterative evaluation — is a stated operating principle.

## Grantmaking programmes

Porticus [organises its grantmaking across six areas](https://www.porticus.com/what-we-do/): Earth (clean energy, regenerative agriculture, living forests, and purposeful business); Education (children in displacement and on the margins); Faith (building vital church communities, child protection, and vital Catholic thought); Society (criminal justice reform and people on the move); Community Arts Lab; and poverty-related work. The Society programme's two tracks reflect the concerns most closely adjacent to the AI-governance movement: the [Criminal Justice challenge](https://www.porticus.com/what-we-do/society/criminal-justice/) supports partners working on rehabilitation, racial-bias reform in sentencing, and community-driven models of incarceration (with active programmes including Justice Reinvented in North America, Rescaled in Belgium, and APACs in Brazil); the [People on the Move programme](https://www.porticus.com/latest/stories-and-insights/2024/learning-from-our-participatory-programme-design-on-immigration-detention/) supports partners in Africa, Asia, and Europe working to reduce the use of arbitrary immigration detention and promote alternatives — a portfolio that directly intersects with the biometric-surveillance and algorithmic-decision-making accountability work funded through the European AI & Society Fund.

## AI and civil society

Porticus is a [current contributing partner of the European AI & Society Fund](https://europeanaifund.org/our-partners/) — the Brussels-hosted pooled philanthropic vehicle that has channelled more than €14.5 million to roughly 70 civil-society organisations and 14 fellows working to shape AI policy and defend digital rights across Europe since 2020. The Fund's [18 current contributing foundations](https://europeanaifund.org/our-partners/) also include the [Adessium Foundation](fund-adessium-foundation.md), Fondation de France, the [Ford Foundation](fund-ford-foundation.md), the [King Baudouin Foundation](fund-king-baudouin-foundation.md), Luminate, the [Mozilla Foundation](fund-mozilla-foundation.md), the [Oak Foundation](fund-oak-foundation.md), the [Open Society Foundations](fund-open-society-foundations.md), Postcode Loterij, the [Robert Bosch Stiftung](fund-robert-bosch-stiftung.md), [Stiftung Mercator](fund-stiftung-mercator.md), and the William & Flora Hewlett Foundation. Porticus's interest in this space is consistent with its Society programme's concern for algorithmic decision-making in migration and criminal-justice contexts, and with its 2023 public framing — articulated through a [feature on "Humanising AI: Dignity through technological innovation"](https://www.porticus.com/latest/stories-and-insights/2023/humanising-ai-dignity-through-technological-innovation/) — that AI must serve collective social purpose and protect human dignity rather than erode it.

## Position in the corpus funder slice

Porticus is the corpus's second Dutch funder entry after [Adessium Foundation](fund-adessium-foundation.md), but fills a sharply distinct slot: where Adessium is a mid-sized focused Dutch family foundation (€15–20M annual grantmaking, invite-only, digital-rights-and-society focus) operating principally at European scale, Porticus is a globally-operating family-foundation network of substantially larger reach — 100+ countries, ~150 staff — whose philanthropy extends from faith and education through criminal justice and environmental work, with an AI/civil-society footprint that runs primarily through its partner role in the European AI & Society Fund rather than through bilateral grants to individual digital-rights organisations in the corpus. Both Dutch foundations are partners in the EAISF alongside [Robert Bosch Stiftung](fund-robert-bosch-stiftung.md) (Germany), [Stiftung Mercator](fund-stiftung-mercator.md) (Germany), and [King Baudouin Foundation](fund-king-baudouin-foundation.md) (Belgium) — making the EAISF the principal pooled vehicle through which the corpus's continental European family-foundation cluster channels resources into the grassroots AI-good civil-society layer.
