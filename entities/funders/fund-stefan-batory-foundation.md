# Stefan Batory Foundation

The Stefan Batory Foundation (in Polish: *Fundacja im. Stefana Batorego*) is an independent Polish private foundation headquartered in Warsaw at [Sapieżyńska 10a](https://www.batory.org.pl/en/about-us/), [established in May 1988 by George Soros and a group of Polish opposition leaders of the 1980s](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stefan_Batory_Foundation) and named after the 16th-century Polish king Stephen Báthory. The foundation entered Poland's National Court Register under [KRS 0000101194 and acquired public-benefit-organization status in 2003](https://www.batory.org.pl/en/about-us/about-us/); it is financed through a mix of [endowment income, foreign grants, individual donations, and the 1.5 per cent personal-income-tax designation route available to Polish PBO-status NGOs](https://www.batory.org.pl/en/about-us/about-us/), and it [cooperates with the Open Society European Network](https://www.batory.org.pl/en/about-us/) while operating as an independent legal entity rather than as a constituent body of the [Open Society Foundations](fund-open-society-foundations.md).

Across its first two decades the foundation played a central role in Poland's post-1989 democratic transition — supporting scholarships, institutional reform, and the founding of the Institute of Public Affairs think tank in 1995 — and [by the end of 2007 had distributed approximately $95.9 million cumulatively, of which $71 million in direct grantmaking](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stefan_Batory_Foundation), positioning itself as one of the principal civil-society grantmakers in Central and Eastern Europe. The current mission framing is to build [an "open, democratic society" of citizens aware of their rights and responsibilities, with Poland understood as "law-abiding and just, open and tolerant,"](https://www.batory.org.pl/en/about-us/about-us/) and three named operational priorities: improving the quality of Polish democracy, strengthening the role of citizens and civic institutions in public life, and developing international cooperation and solidarity in the European Union and Central and Eastern Europe.

## Governance and current leadership

The foundation is governed by a Council and a Board of Directors, both rotating from the founding-era figures (the foundation was originally led by Council Chair Marcin Król and Board Chair Aleksander Smolar). The [current Council](https://www.batory.org.pl/en/about-us/council-and-board/) is chaired by Mikołaj Cześnik — a sociologist and political scientist, professor at SWPS University, and director of its Institute of Social Sciences — with members including Rafał Dutkiewicz (mayor of Wrocław 2002–2014), Anna Giza, Aleksandra Hnatiuk, Hanna Machińska, Joanna Mytkowska (director of the Museum of Modern Art in Warsaw), Krzysztof Obłój, and Jan Olbrycht (Member of the European Parliament across the 6th–9th terms). The [current Board](https://www.batory.org.pl/en/about-us/council-and-board/) is chaired by Edwin Bendyk — journalist, writer, and longtime *Polityka* columnist — with members Szymon Gutkowski, Krzysztof Izdebski (Director of Advocacy and Development), Anna Materska-Sosnowska, and Sylwia Sobiepan (Director of Grant Programmes).

The [senior staff line-up](https://www.batory.org.pl/en/about-us/staff/) places Maria Cywińska as Chief Operating Officer, with programmatic leadership distributed across Sylwia Sobiepan (Grant Programmes), Piotr Kosiewski (ideaForum), Joanna Załuska (Local Democracy), and Krzysztof Izdebski (Democratic Institution Programme).

## Programmes and current grantmaking vehicles

The foundation's grantmaking runs through several named vehicles. The [Active Citizens Fund – National](https://www.batory.org.pl/en/grants/active-citizens/) is the foundation's largest recent grantmaking vehicle: Batory leads a three-organisation operator consortium (alongside the Shipyard Foundation and the Academy of Civic Organizations Foundation) for the Polish National strand of the European Economic Area Financial Mechanism and Norwegian Financial Mechanism (the "EEA and Norway Grants") 2014–2021 financial period, with a €30 million budget and a 2019–2024 implementation window, structured around four programmatic outcomes — citizen participation in civic activities and environmental protection; human rights and anti-discrimination work; empowerment of vulnerable groups; and civil-society organisational capacity and sustainability.

The successor vehicle is the [Civil Society Fund 2025–2031](https://www.batory.org.pl/en/), an €83,471,033 grantmaking line for which Batory is again the operator-consortium lead, this time alongside the Shipyard Foundation, the Education for Democracy Foundation, the Rural Development Foundation, and Frivillighet Norge. Alongside these donor-government-backed vehicles the foundation operates the Helpers Aid Fund and the Ukraine Solidarity Fund (the latter rapidly scaled following Russia's 2022 full-scale invasion of Ukraine, consistent with Batory's long-standing Central and Eastern European regional remit), and runs several in-house operational programmes — Your Vote, Your Choice (election engagement); Local Government Workshop; Public Awareness Campaigns; Election Monitoring; and ideaForum, the foundation's [think-tank function on democracy, rule of law, citizens' rights, and European integration](https://www.batory.org.pl/en/our-pages/).

The Active Citizens Fund and Civil Society Fund vehicles together identify Batory's distinctive operational shape in the European philanthropic landscape — its largest current capital flows arrive as a Norwegian and EEA Grants operator-consortium contract rather than as endowment-funded grantmaking, placing the foundation institutionally adjacent to the bilateral-aid mechanism rather than purely inside the privately endowed-foundation slice that the other corpus funders occupy.

## AI-good footprint

Stefan Batory's AI-good footprint in the corpus is mediated rather than direct: the foundation does not operate a named AI-policy, algorithmic-accountability, or platform-accountability grant programme, and it does not appear in the contributing-partner roster of the [European AI & Society Fund](fund-european-ai-society-fund.md). The corpus-relevant connections instead run through two named channels.

The first is the foundation's status as a contributing partner of [Civitates](fund-civitates.md). The Brussels-headquartered pooled philanthropic fund [lists Stefan Batory among its roughly thirty contributing partner foundations](https://civitates-eu.org/foundation-partners/) and [maintains a dedicated page for the relationship](https://civitates-eu.org/stefan-batory/), and the Civitates [Tech & Democracy sub-fund](fund-civitates.md) is the principal AI-and-platform-accountability programmatic line under the Civitates umbrella — covering algorithm transparency, DSA enforcement, and platform accountability across Europe. Batory's relationship to Civitates is therefore a capitalisation route into a Brussels-hosted pooled vehicle whose Tech & Democracy grantees include AI Forensics (France), [AlgorithmWatch](../organizations/org-algorithmwatch.md) (Germany), and [European Digital Rights](../organizations/org-edri.md) (Brussels-headquartered network) — corpus-relevant grantee relationships that Batory underwrites by partnership rather than directly. The corpus's existing [Civitates entry](fund-civitates.md) names the partner-foundation overlap with the European AI & Society Fund (Adessium, Open Society Foundations, Stiftung Mercator, Robert Bosch Stiftung, Luminate, Ford, MacArthur, King Baudouin); Batory sits outside that overlap and supports Civitates exclusively rather than the EAISF as well, illustrating a different shape of European partner-foundation participation.

The second is Krzysztof Izdebski, who simultaneously holds the foundation's [Director of Advocacy and Development role and a Board seat](https://www.batory.org.pl/en/about-us/council-and-board/) and leads the Democratic Institution programmatic line. Izdebski is a lawyer specialising in digital policies, freedom of information, public-sector information re-use, and the relationship between technology and democracy. His substantive corpus of work on algorithmic transparency in government-citizen relations — including the alGOVrithms and alGOVrithms 2.0 cross-country reports on automated-decision-making systems in Czechia, Georgia, Hungary, Kosovo, North Macedonia, Poland, Serbia, and Slovakia — was [produced under his earlier Fundacja ePaństwo affiliation](https://medium.com/fundacja-epa%C5%84stwo/algorithms-of-fairness-2a30c2f93622) and is not formally a Batory work product, but Izdebski brings that profile into Batory's institutional voice. Externally he has authored a Civitates-published [piece on Polish-elections-era polarisation](https://civitates-eu.org/bridging-the-divide/) framing civil-society non-partisanship and credibility as the sector's distinctive asset, and Batory's institutional [September 2025 submission to the European Commission's EU Civil Society Strategy consultation](https://www.batory.org.pl/en/2025/09/08/designing-impact-stefan-batory-foundations-perspective-on-the-eu-civil-society-strategy/) places digital rights, oversight of spyware, support for CSO digital capacity, and CSO disinformation work inside its PROTECT pillar — without engaging the AI Act or DSA enforcement specifics, but signalling the institutional posture into which Izdebski's profile is read.

## Position in the corpus funder slice

Within the funder slice of this corpus Stefan Batory is the first Central / Eastern European funder entry and the first Polish foundation in any slice — distinct from each of the existing Continental European national foundations ([Stiftung Mercator](fund-stiftung-mercator.md), the [Robert Bosch Stiftung](fund-robert-bosch-stiftung.md), [Adessium Foundation](fund-adessium-foundation.md)) by region, operating language, scale, and operational shape. Where Stiftung Mercator and the Robert Bosch Stiftung are large Germany-headquartered family-fortune-anchored grantmakers that both capitalise the European AI & Society Fund and pursue direct multi-year strategic grantmaking on AI-good work, Batory is a Soros-lineage Warsaw-headquartered foundation whose largest current grantmaking flows are public-funder operator-consortium contracts (EEA / Norway Grants) rather than private-endowment outflow, and whose AI-good exposure is mediated through Civitates capitalisation and a single Director-of-Advocacy profile rather than through a dedicated programme line.

The closest sibling pairing in the existing corpus is [Civitates](fund-civitates.md) — both as the pooled vehicle Batory contributes to and as the structurally analogous European-democracy-focused funder whose AI-and-platform work sits inside a broader civic-space remit rather than constituting its principal mission. Batory's distinctive contribution to the corpus's funder slice is geographic — closing the previously unrepresented Central / Eastern European national-foundation gap on the partner-foundation side of the European-democracy philanthropic infrastructure that Civitates anchors and that this corpus already tracks in detail. The synth-flagged rationale for adding Batory — "first Central / Eastern European funder; Polish private foundation with civic-society / digital-rights grantmaking; Soros-lineage" — sits in this gap: the Soros-lineage framing is what makes Batory analytically interesting as a Central European foundation, the civic-society grantmaking is the foundation's primary identity, and the digital-rights line of work is the mediated thread by which the entry connects to the rest of the corpus's funder slice rather than its grantmaking centre of gravity.
