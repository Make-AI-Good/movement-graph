# GMCDP / Foxglove launch of the legal challenge to the DWP's secret benefit-fraud algorithm (1 December 2021)

On Wednesday 1 December 2021, the [Greater Manchester Coalition of Disabled People](../organizations/org-greater-manchester-coalition-of-disabled-people.md) and [Foxglove](../organizations/org-foxglove.md) [publicly opened the judicial-review challenge](https://www.foxglove.org.uk/2021/12/01/secret-dwp-algorithm/) to the Department for Work and Pensions's secret benefit-fraud algorithm — the system later disclosed as the General Matching Service. The launch was a coordinated movement-actor moment: Foxglove published a same-day blog post setting out the case under the headline *"NEW CASE: secret algorithm targets disabled people unfairly for benefit probes — cutting off life-saving cash and trapping them in call centre hell"*; GMCDP set out its own framing in its [joint-challenge explainer](https://gmcdp.com/gmcdp-foxglove-legal-challenge-department-work-and-pensions-dwp-fraud-algorithm); and a [CrowdJustice page](https://www.crowdjustice.com/case/dwp-algorithm/) opened alongside the legal letter to fund the case at a £5,000 initial target with a £15,000 stretch goal. The launch put the case on the public record, started a 28-day clock on the Department's response to GMCDP's pre-action correspondence, and is the bookend opening of the [near-four-year DWP campaign](camp-foxglove-gmcdp-dwp-2021-ongoing.md) the corpus tracks through to its [6 May 2025 closing-disclosure post](https://www.foxglove.org.uk/2025/05/06/dwp-explain-benefits-fraud-algorithm/).

## Context

Through the late 2010s and into the early pandemic, GMCDP members had been reporting an experience the Coalition's later framing came to call ["fear of the brown envelope"](https://www.foxglove.org.uk/2021/12/01/secret-dwp-algorithm/) — repeated benefit reassessments triggered without explanation, automated phone-tree investigations lasting up to a year, and benefit cuts that took away cash needed for food, rent, and energy. The DWP had publicly described its fraud-detection work as using ["cutting-edge artificial intelligence to crack down on organised criminal gangs committing large-scale benefit fraud"](https://www.foxglove.org.uk/2021/12/01/secret-dwp-algorithm/) but had not put the system's design, the data feeding into it, or any disparate-impact analysis on the public record. By late November 2021 GMCDP had collected enough member testimony for [Disability Rights UK](https://www.disabilityrightsuk.org/news/2021/november/dwp-urged-reveal-algorithm-%E2%80%98targets%E2%80%99-disabled-people-benefit-fraud) and [other movement-press outlets](https://www.benefitsandwork.co.uk/news/secret-algorithm-unfairly-targets-disabled-claimants-for-fraud-investigation) to begin reporting that the Coalition believed the algorithm was "over-picking" disabled claimants for invasive investigation; the Coalition took the case to Foxglove on the strength of the [2017–2020 visa-streaming Home Office case](camp-foxglove-jcwi-visa-streaming-2020.md) that had established a working method of disclosure pressure inside a judicial-review claim. The 1 December 2021 launch was the moment that working method, adapted for a much longer time arc and a different constituency, was put on the public record against the Department.

## What was launched on 1 December 2021

Three artefacts went out simultaneously. [Foxglove's launch post](https://www.foxglove.org.uk/2021/12/01/secret-dwp-algorithm/) framed the case in the constituency's own register — a system that "decides who eats and who goes hungry," that traps claimants in "call centre hell," and that the Department had refused to explain on the public record. The post named the demands of the [pre-action correspondence](https://www.localgovernmentlawyer.co.uk/litigation-and-enforcement/400-litigation-news/49739-dwp-faces-legal-action-over-use-of-algorithm-in-decisions-over-fraud-investigations) GMCDP had sent the same day: that the Department disclose how the algorithm worked, what evidence it had that the system was not discriminating against disabled people, and what checks and balances were in place to ensure benefit claimants were not being unfairly investigated. The legal letter set a 28-day response window and warned that, absent disclosure, the Coalition and Foxglove would seek judicial review. [GMCDP's own page on the joint challenge](https://gmcdp.com/gmcdp-foxglove-legal-challenge-department-work-and-pensions-dwp-fraud-algorithm) carried the constituency-led version of the same framing — "disabled people think it is unfair" — and anchored the case on the Coalition's collected member testimony rather than on a digital-rights-first frame. The [CrowdJustice page](https://www.crowdjustice.com/case/dwp-algorithm/) opened a public funding pipeline at a £5,000 initial target with a £15,000 stretch goal, putting the public-mobilisation track on the same launch day as the legal track. The [Disability News Service write-up](https://www.disabilitynewsservice.com/legal-letter-asks-dwp-for-information-on-discriminatory-secret-algorithm/) of the launch was the first movement-press account to pull the three artefacts together, naming the Coalition's view that the algorithm was "over-picking" disabled people and setting out the case's grounds: alleged breaches of rights under the European Convention on Human Rights and the General Data Protection Regulation, plus the Department's obligations under the Equality Act's public sector equality duty.

## Significance

The launch is the corpus's first Event of the "movement-actor opens the case" sub-shape, and it pairs structurally with the [10 January 2024 Couling admission Event](event-couling-dwp-bias-admission-2024-01-10.md) as the DWP campaign's bookend pair: the 1 December 2021 launch is the movement-actor side that put the case on the public record under its own framing, and the January 2024 oral-evidence session is the counterparty side at which the Department first conceded on the parliamentary record that the system "do[es] have biases in." Read together, the two Events anchor the disclosure-pressure register the Coalition and Foxglove sustained for three years and three months — from a pre-action letter on 1 December 2021 through to the [General Matching Service operational disclosure on 6 May 2025](https://www.foxglove.org.uk/2025/05/06/dwp-explain-benefits-fraud-algorithm/). The launch is also the corpus's first multi-vehicle case-opening Event: a single-day coordinated artefact set across a movement legal NGO's website, a constituency-led organisation's own materials, and a public-funding platform, with movement-press coverage threaded through. That coordination shape — pre-action letter plus public framing plus public funding pipeline plus movement-press distribution — is one the Foxglove cluster has used at varying scales across its UK strategic-litigation casework, and the December 2021 launch is the cleanest example of all four channels firing on a single day. In broader corpus terms, the launch put the GMCDP-side voice — anchored later in [Rick Burgess's "algorithmic injustice" framing](https://www.gmlaw.org.uk/2022/03/31/algorithmic-injustice-an-interview-with-rick-burgess-from-gmcdp/) — on the public record before the Coalition's case had any of the parliamentary or operational admissions that would later anchor the case's accountability artefacts, and in that sense it is the moment at which the disability-justice constituency's experience of automated decision-making in the UK welfare system became a publicly-recorded movement claim against the Department.
