# Founding vote of the African Content Moderators Union, Nairobi (1 May 2023)

On Monday 1 May 2023 — Kenyan Labour Day — more than 150 current and former content moderators gathered at the [Mövenpick Hotel](https://www.foxglove.org.uk/2023/05/15/nairobi-content-moderation-summit/) in Nairobi and [voted to establish](https://time.com/6275995/chatgpt-facebook-african-workers-union/) the [African Content Moderators Union](../organizations/org-african-content-moderators-union.md) — the first continental trade-union body for tech workers in Africa, and the first union anywhere built around the labour pipeline that polices and trains the platforms and frontier-AI models of Meta, ByteDance, and OpenAI. The moderators represented the [outsourced workforces of Sama, Majorel, and Teleperformance](https://nonprofitquarterly.org/can-african-unions-beat-big-tech/) reviewing content for [Facebook, YouTube, TikTok, and ChatGPT](https://www.business-humanrights.org/en/latest-news/kenya-facebook-youtube-tiktok-content-moderators-form-labour-union-to-push-for-better-working-conditions/), and worked in [fourteen different African languages](https://www.business-humanrights.org/en/latest-news/kenya-facebook-youtube-tiktok-content-moderators-form-labour-union-to-push-for-better-working-conditions/). The vote installed a [founding committee of six former tech-outsourcing employees](https://nonprofitquarterly.org/can-african-unions-beat-big-tech/) with [James Oyange](../persons/person-james-oyange.md) — the former Majorel/TikTok moderator widely known as "Mojez" — as committee secretary and the union's primary public spokesperson. [Daniel Motaung](../persons/person-daniel-motaung.md), the South African whistleblower whose [2019 in-facility Alliance organising at Samasource](../persons/person-daniel-motaung.md) and [May 2022 constitutional petition against Meta and Sama](event-foxglove-motaung-meta-sama-petition-filing-2022-05-10.md) had made the gathering possible, delivered the founding-vote address in person.

## Context

The 1 May 2023 vote was the culmination of a four-year arc that ran from Motaung's [original Samasource Alliance organising effort](../organizations/org-african-content-moderators-union.md) — for which he was fired in 2019 — through the [10 May 2022 Petition E071 of 2022](event-foxglove-motaung-meta-sama-petition-filing-2022-05-10.md) in which [Mercy Mutemi](../persons/person-mercy-mutemi.md) of [Nzili & Sumbi Advocates](../organizations/org-nzili-sumbi-advocates.md), backed by the UK tech-justice community-interest company [Foxglove](../organizations/org-foxglove.md), filed Motaung's "world-first" suit against Meta in Kenya. The intervening twelve months had moved the labour conditions of African content moderation onto the public record under Kenyan constitutional protection and built a network of organising-experienced former moderators across the major Nairobi outsourcers.

The immediate organising trigger was the [January 2023 Sama redundancies](https://restofworld.org/2023/meta-content-moderators-kenya-fired-unionize/), in which Sama terminated its Facebook content-moderation contract with Meta and made [roughly 260 Kenyan-based moderators redundant](https://time.com/collections/time100-ai-2024/7012799/kauna-malgwi/) — a cohort that supplied much of the union's founding constituency and that simultaneously launched a parallel [185-moderators constitutional petition](../campaigns/camp-foxglove-185-moderators-meta-kenya.md) against Meta, Sama, and Majorel two months before the summit. The summit was [organised by Foxglove](https://www.codastory.com/authoritarian-tech/kenya-content-moderators/) — Martha Dark's note in subsequent coverage placing Foxglove's role as convenor of the May meeting — with the worker-organiser side led on the ground by the cohort of former Sama moderators whose January redundancy had freed them from non-disclosure restrictions to organise openly.

## The May Day vote

The summit ran across the holiday weekend with the [decisive vote held on Labour Day, 1 May 2023](https://www.business-humanrights.org/en/latest-news/kenya-facebook-youtube-tiktok-content-moderators-form-labour-union-to-push-for-better-working-conditions/). Foxglove's [15 May write-up](https://www.foxglove.org.uk/2023/05/15/nairobi-content-moderation-summit/) records the moment of the result: "When the vote to form the new union was announced, confetti rained from the ceiling and the moderators erupted into applause." [Nonprofit Quarterly's one-year-on review](https://nonprofitquarterly.org/can-african-unions-beat-big-tech/) describes the same scene in similar terms — registration of the union approved on the floor, "the crowd quickly erupted in fits of laughter and applause."

[Motaung delivered the founding-vote address in person](https://www.foxglove.org.uk/2023/05/15/nairobi-content-moderation-summit/) — the Foxglove account rejoicing at seeing him "back ... standing side by side with moderators" — with the line that the rest of his public corpus has [carried as its closing or near-closing sentence](../voices/voice-daniel-motaung.md): "I never thought, when I started the Alliance in 2019, we would be here today – with moderators from every major social media giant forming the first African moderators union." In [accompanying press appearances](https://www.business-humanrights.org/en/latest-news/kenya-facebook-youtube-tiktok-content-moderators-form-labour-union-to-push-for-better-working-conditions/) timed to the vote, Motaung framed the union's reason for being in the broader register the case file had installed: "content moderation is in a state of crisis and that content moderators are paying for it with their lives."

The room itself converted the lawsuit's allegation of structural harm into a collective claim. [James Oyange](../persons/person-james-oyange.md), whose Majorel TikTok-moderation contract had not been renewed in April 2023 and who had only connected with the organising effort in the weeks before, described being [stunned by the universality of the challenges](https://www.codastory.com/authoritarian-tech/kenya-content-moderators/) once the discussion began, and another attendee known publicly as Wabe captured the affective shift the summit produced: "I was happy to come and realize I was not alone." [Kauna Malgwi](../persons/person-kauna-malgwi.md), the Nigerian clinical psychologist whose four-year Sama tenure had ended in the January redundancies, was [present in the room](https://restofworld.org/2023/meta-content-moderators-kenya-fired-unionize/) and would emerge from the founding cohort as the [chairperson of the union's Nigeria-chapter steering committee](https://time.com/collections/time100-ai-2024/7012799/kauna-malgwi/). Nathan Nkunzimana, a former moderator-plaintiff in the parallel 185-moderators dismissal suit, was [also among the founding participants](https://restofworld.org/2023/meta-content-moderators-kenya-fired-unionize/).

## Founding committee and demands

The vote installed a [committee of six former employees of tech outsourcing firms](https://nonprofitquarterly.org/can-african-unions-beat-big-tech/) to carry the union's work forward, with Oyange as committee secretary and primary spokesperson and the broader founding cohort including Malgwi and Nkunzimana. The union's stated demands tracked the conditions that had produced it: [substantially higher pay, profession-specific psychological support, recognition of content moderation as a legitimate profession in Kenyan labour law, transparent contracts, insurance, and the right to organise without retaliation](../organizations/org-african-content-moderators-union.md). The union's organising materials named the opposition as a three-part coalition — [the platforms (Meta, ByteDance, OpenAI), the outsourcers (Sama, Majorel, Teleperformance), and the Kenyan state](https://nonprofitquarterly.org/can-african-unions-beat-big-tech/) — and the formal registration push with Kenya's Registrar of Trade Unions began in the weeks after the vote.

[Foxglove's role](https://www.codastory.com/authoritarian-tech/kenya-content-moderators/) at the summit and afterward was supporting rather than principal: convenor of the May meeting, external legal and communications partner, and home of the [Cori Crider](../persons/person-cori-crider.md) and [Martha Dark](../persons/person-martha-dark.md) public-facing capacity through which the union's first months of press engagement ran. [Al Jazeera's 22 May 2023 podcast](https://www.aljazeera.com/podcasts/2023/5/22/can-africas-first-content-moderators-union-change-big-tech) framed the union's significance for international audiences in the register the summit had set: "the first union of its kind on the continent."

## Significance

The 1 May 2023 founding vote is the corpus's first mapped Event of an African worker-controlled trade body coming into existence around the labour pipeline that sits underneath every major commercial social-media platform and frontier-AI model. Three structural features of the founding shape what follows from it. The first is the [continental-first character](https://www.aljazeera.com/podcasts/2023/5/22/can-africas-first-content-moderators-union-change-big-tech) of the union itself — distinct, in the corpus's existing record, from the artist-side [SAG-AFTRA strike call](event-sag-aftra-strike-call-press-conference-2023-07-13.md) ten weeks later (an existing US union exercising its bargaining power over AI provisions in an entertainment-industry contract), in that this is a sectoral trade union being brought into existence for the first time, from below, in a region where no comparable worker body had previously existed. The second is the [vote's lineage relationship to the May 2022 Motaung filing](event-foxglove-motaung-meta-sama-petition-filing-2022-05-10.md): the petition's allegation that Meta and Sama had run a regime of union-busting against the in-facility Alliance Motaung tried to convene in 2019 was, twelve months later, the political precondition of an open-floor vote held in a Nairobi hotel by 150 of the workers the suit was filed on behalf of, with the original Alliance organiser delivering the address. The third is the [Labour Day timing](https://www.business-humanrights.org/en/latest-news/kenya-facebook-youtube-tiktok-content-moderators-form-labour-union-to-push-for-better-working-conditions/) — a deliberate framing of the vote within the trade-union tradition of 1 May rather than the tech-industry tradition of launch events — which has shaped how the union has been received internationally by trade-union counterparts (the [Communication Workers Union of Kenya](../organizations/org-african-content-moderators-union.md) and the [Global Trade Union Alliance of Content Moderators](../organizations/org-african-content-moderators-union.md) subsequently launched in Nairobi in 2025) and which marks the founding as a moment of explicit continuity with twentieth-century industrial labour organising rather than a tech-sector-specific event.

In the months and years after the vote, the union has remained [unregistered with Kenya's Registrar of Trade Unions](../organizations/org-african-content-moderators-union.md) — a registration impasse organisers attribute to the Kenyan state's reluctance to confront the outsourcing-investor coalition — but the founding-vote day's three principal effects on the corpus's record are durable irrespective of the registration outcome. It put a self-organised worker collective at the centre of a Nairobi accountability stack the corpus had previously tracked only through its litigation, NGO, and coalition vehicles. It supplied the [African Content Moderators Union](../organizations/org-african-content-moderators-union.md) with its founding constituency, leadership, and demand set. And it produced the [single most-cited speech](../voices/voice-daniel-motaung.md) in Motaung's public record — the Alliance-to-union arc compressed into a one-sentence frame — that the corpus's Nairobi-anchored content-moderation cluster has carried in nearly every subsequent press account of its work.
