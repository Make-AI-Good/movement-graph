# Bits of Freedom Big Brother Awards 2019 — Expert Award to SyRI (29 November 2019)

On Friday 29 November 2019, [Bits of Freedom](../organizations/org-bits-of-freedom.md) staged the fifteenth edition of the Dutch [Big Brother Awards](https://www.bitsoffreedom.nl/dossiers/big-brother-awards/) at the Hertz-zaal in TivoliVredenburg in Utrecht — the first edition of the awards [held outside Amsterdam](https://www.bitsoffreedom.nl/2019/12/11/dit-waren-de-big-brother-awards-2019/) — and awarded the Expert Award to the Dutch government's Systeem Risico Indicatie (SyRI) welfare-fraud risk-scoring system. The ceremony made SyRI the corpus's earliest civil-society privacy-awards-ceremony-shape Event from continental Europe, and crystallised in public the substantive argument against the system that the [Hague District Court would uphold in its 5 February 2020 judgment](https://algorithmwatch.org/en/syri-netherlands-algorithm/) finding the SyRI legislation incompatible with Article 8 of the European Convention on Human Rights.

## Context

The Dutch [Big Brother Awards](https://www.bitsoffreedom.nl/dossiers/big-brother-awards/) are the national edition of the Privacy International-originated international Big Brother Awards franchise, [administered by Bits of Freedom](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bits_of_Freedom) — the Netherlands' principal digital-rights organisation, itself in its twentieth year of existence at the 2019 ceremony — under a standing three-prize structure of an Expert Award decided by a jury of named subject specialists, a Public Award decided by audience vote, and the positive Felipe Rodríguez Award named for the XS4ALL co-founder who was central to the 1999–2000 founding of Bits of Freedom. The fifteenth-edition framing and the move from Amsterdam to Utrecht were [explicitly marked](https://www.bitsoffreedom.nl/2019/12/11/dit-waren-de-big-brother-awards-2019/) by the organisation as a deliberate broadening of the ceremony's geographic and audience footprint.

The 2019 shortlists had been [published on 26 November 2019](https://www.bitsoffreedom.nl/2019/11/26/big-brother-awards-2019-dit-zijn-de-nominaties/), three days before the ceremony. The Expert Award shortlist comprised the Dutch student-loan administrator DUO (nominated by Alex Tess Rutten of the Landelijke Studentenvakbond on tracking-software and passport-confiscation grounds), the Dutch government's SyRI welfare-fraud profiling system (nominated by Cihan Ugural of FNV and the University of Amsterdam neuroinformatician Sennay Ghebreab), and the public-health-research-funding agency ZonMW's children's health programme (nominated by Bureau Jeugd en Media founder Justine Pardoen on flawed-software and privacy-safeguard grounds). The Public Award shortlist comprised Google (for the Google Home listening practices and ecosystem-integration coverage), Justice Minister Sander Dekker (for proposed cross-agency payment-profiling at the CJIB debt-collection authority), and the social-media-monitoring vendor Coosto (for archived-post surveillance of the Dutch public).

## The ceremony

The [29 November 2019 ceremony](https://www.bitsoffreedom.nl/2019/12/11/dit-waren-de-big-brother-awards-2019/) was presented by the Dutch journalist Roel Maalderink, opened by Bits of Freedom director [Evelyn Austin](../organizations/org-bits-of-freedom.md), and interleaved with musical performance from the duo Marley Me and a spoken-word piece by Zaïre Krieger. The three awards were announced on stage in succession.

The Public Award went to Minister of Legal Protection [Sander Dekker](https://www.bitsoffreedom.nl/pers/minister-sander-dekker-en-syri-winnen-big-brother-awards-2019/), accepting in person, for the proposed cross-agency data-linkage programme at the Centraal Justitieel Incassobureau (CJIB) that would have combined personal-debt records to construct ability-to-pay profiles of citizens with outstanding fines. The minister acknowledged in his acceptance that the prize had put him "on the defendants' bench" — using the courtroom register the ceremony deliberately mobilises — but signalled willingness to reflect on the criticisms voiced.

The Expert Award went to the Ministry of Social Affairs and Employment's [SyRI system](https://algorithmwatch.org/en/syri-netherlands-algorithm/) and was [accepted by Carsten Herstel](https://www.bitsoffreedom.nl/pers/minister-sander-dekker-en-syri-winnen-big-brother-awards-2019/), Director-General of Social Security and Integration at the Ministry, who defended the system on the floor of the ceremony — "I find it logical that the government gets alerted when someone gets a rental allowance and owns the house at the same time" — and was joined by a video response from State Secretary Tamara van Ark acknowledging that the Ministry sat "in the suspect's seat" but reaffirming the commitment to targeted-benefits delivery. The jury rationale [carried by the nomination](https://www.bitsoffreedom.nl/2019/11/26/big-brother-awards-2019-dit-zijn-de-nominaties/) was that SyRI's combination of multi-database personal-data linkage with undisclosed scoring rules — applied through deployments [concentrated in named low-income and minority-population neighbourhoods](https://algorithmwatch.org/en/syri-netherlands-algorithm/) — treated whole populations as default suspects without provision for affected individuals to learn of, contest, or correct the assignments under which they were investigated.

The Felipe Rodríguez Award — the ceremony's standing positive privacy prize — went to internet pioneer and [Waag Futurelab](https://www.bitsoffreedom.nl/pers/minister-sander-dekker-en-syri-winnen-big-brother-awards-2019/) founder Marleen Stikker, recognised for two decades of advocacy for digital freedom and the human-stakes-first framing of internet policy in the Netherlands.

## Significance

The ceremony's load-bearing function on the SyRI public record was to convert the documentary case that the [Platform Bescherming Burgerrechten and PILP-NJCM-led civil-society coalition](https://algorithmwatch.org/en/syri-netherlands-algorithm/) — including the trade union FNV, Stichting Privacy First, Stichting KDVP, and the Landelijke Cliëntenraad as co-claimants, with authors Tommy Wieringa and Maxim Februari joining in individual capacity, and the *Bij Voorbaat Verdacht* (Suspected From The Outset) media campaign as the public-facing edge from early 2018 — had built into a national-press argument with a named jury, named experts, and the Ministry physically in attendance. Three months later, on 5 February 2020, the [Hague District Court ruled](https://algorithmwatch.org/en/syri-netherlands-algorithm/) that the SyRI legislation violated Article 8 of the European Convention on Human Rights for processing too much data with insufficiently specific purposes and for opaque algorithmic targeting of poor neighbourhoods, and ordered the system's immediate halt. The award sat in the public sequence between the *Bij Voorbaat Verdacht* coalition campaign and the court ruling — not as a legal instrument, but as the civil-society-ceremony moment at which the substantive criticism crystallised in the press in the run-up to the judgment.

As an Event-type entry, the ceremony is the corpus's first civil-society privacy-awards-ceremony-shape entry — distinct in register from the [Polish constitutional-court judgment](event-polish-constitutional-tribunal-k-53-16-2018-06-06.md) and the petition-filing and case-launch Events that have so far anchored the welfare-algorithm-litigation corpus, and distinct in shape from a protest, hearing, or coalition launch. The Big Brother Awards franchise — Privacy International-originated, run nationally by the principal digital-rights organisation in each participating country, and structured around the same Expert / Public / positive triad in each national edition — is the corpus's first instance of a recurring multi-country civil-society ceremony as a campaign tactic against state and corporate surveillance, and the Dutch fifteenth-edition record is the longest-running national instance in continental Europe on the public record. The ceremony also sits as the corpus's first Netherlands-located Event, opening a country register that the upcoming Hague District Court judgment, the [Reclaim Your Face](../campaigns/camp-edri-reclaim-your-face-eu-citizens-initiative-2020-2022.md) coalition, and the Dutch *toeslagenaffaire* childcare-benefits scandal will extend.
