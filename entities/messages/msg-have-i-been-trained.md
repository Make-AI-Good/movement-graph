# Have I Been Trained

**Have I Been Trained** is the question-as-framing of the visual-artist [consent-and-data-dignity register](https://www.artbasel.com/stories/ai-holly-herndon-mat-dryhurst-data-training-art-making) inside the 2022–2024 U.S. and European pushback on generative-AI training-data ingestion. The phrase, fixed by [the tool of the same name launched in mid-September 2022](https://petapixel.com/2022/09/19/you-can-now-check-if-your-photos-were-used-to-train-ai-image-generators/) by the Spawning AI team of Jordan Meyer, Mat Dryhurst, and Holly Herndon, names the harm of unconsented training-data ingestion at the level of the individual artist's dignity rather than at the level of the bargaining-table demand: the question itself makes the harm legible — *am I in there?* — and the [Do Not Train registry](https://haveibeentrained.com/about) the tool surfaces instantiates the default the question presupposes, *no, until I say so*. The framing runs in parallel with, and is the visual-artist data-dignity sister of, the legal-pleading [Consent, Credit, Compensation](msg-consent-credit-compensation.md) register that the *Andersen v. Stability AI* class-action complaint and [Karla Ortiz](../voices/voice-karla-ortiz.md)'s Senate Judiciary testimony anchor.

## The tool and the registry

The Have I Been Trained search interface lets any user type a query, paste a URL, or upload an image, and [search the LAION-5B image-text dataset](https://techcrunch.com/2022/09/21/who-fed-the-ai/) — the open ~5.85-billion-image scrape that anchors Stable Diffusion and most other widely deployed open generative-image models — for matches against their own work or likeness. Matched images can then be added to a Do Not Train registry, which Spawning maintains as a portable opt-out list any downstream model-trainer can ingest before its next training run. The tool's first headline find, [a patient discovering private medical photographs of herself inside LAION-5B](https://petapixel.com/2022/09/26/shocked-artist-finds-private-medical-photos-in-ai-training-data-set/) within days of launch, fixed the dignity-harm argument behind the framing: that the question "have I been trained?" is not abstract, because the answer for many people is yes-without-knowledge-or-consent on artefacts ranging from copyrighted artwork to private medical records.

## The Stability AI opt-out proof point — December 2022

The framing's working credibility as a campaign — and the registry's existence as a mechanism rather than a posture — was fixed by the December 2022 agreement under which Stability AI [committed to honour the Do Not Train registry](https://www.technologyreview.com/2022/12/16/1065247/artists-can-now-opt-out-of-the-next-version-of-stable-diffusion/) ahead of training the next iteration of Stable Diffusion. The [VentureBeat write-up](https://venturebeat.com/ai/stability-ai-to-honor-artist-opt-out-requests-for-stable-diffusion-3) carried Stability AI's commitment alongside the working two-week opt-out window the partnership opened. By [7 March 2023 Spawning reported](https://spawning.substack.com/p/spawning-opts-out-78-million-artworks) that the registry had collected opt-outs on roughly 78 million artworks ahead of the Stable Diffusion 3 training run — a figure [independently carried by The Decoder](https://the-decoder.com/artists-remove-80-million-images-from-stable-diffusion-3-training-data/) and broken down as approximately 40,000 individual artworks opted out via Have I Been Trained itself plus tens of millions more via partner platforms (the ArtStation NoAI tag, Shutterstock's default opt-out for non-contributing content).

## The artistic-practice register and the data-dignity argument

Spawning's working framing of the registry as ["a creator consent layer for AI"](https://spawning.substack.com/p/our-seed-round-of-funding-to-build) anchors on an artistic-practice register that distinguishes the Have I Been Trained framing from the parallel U.S. legal-pleading register of [Consent, Credit, Compensation](msg-consent-credit-compensation.md). Holly Herndon and Mat Dryhurst — Berlin-based musicians whose prior practice included the [Holly+ vocal-deepfake consent project](https://www.artbasel.com/stories/ai-holly-herndon-mat-dryhurst-data-training-art-making) — have framed the company's working posture as pro-AI-and-pro-consent: the position that the right response to the unconsented-ingestion question is not to halt model development but to insist that training is a relationship a creator can decline, accept, or shape. That stance puts the Have I Been Trained framing at a distance from both the SAG-AFTRA [existential-threat](msg-existential-threat-to-creative-professions.md) register (which anchors on displacement of creative work) and the [Concept Art Association](../organizations/org-concept-art-association.md)–led Consent-Credit-Compensation legal register (which anchors on a copyright cause of action) — same wave, distinct argumentative shape.

## The contested edge — opt-out as default

The framing's strongest published critique is structural rather than rhetorical. The [3 May 2023 TechCrunch seed-round write-up](https://techcrunch.com/2023/05/03/spawning-lays-out-its-plans-for-letting-creators-opt-out-of-generative-ai-training/) carried the working European-creator position that opt-out — even backed by a registry as well-built as Spawning's — fails the GDPR actively-given-consent standard and so cannot be the right legal baseline for an EU-applicable training-data regime. The [European Guild for Artificial Intelligence Regulation (EGAIR)](https://www.egair.eu/), the organised European creators' coalition, has carried the structurally adjacent position that the underlying harm is the inability to deny consent at all and that opt-in by default — not opt-out by default — is the correct regulatory and ethical baseline. The two positions are working sisters of each other inside the same training-data-consent debate, with Have I Been Trained operating as the working artist-side instantiation of the opt-out posture and the EGAIR / EU AI Act push operating as the opt-in counterweight.

## Carrying the framing into consensual training — Source.Plus and Public Diffusion

The framing's working continuation since 2024 has run through Spawning's own move from opt-out (defensive) to opt-in / consensual curation (constructive). The [11 June 2024 Source.Plus launch](https://techcrunch.com/2024/06/11/spawning-wants-to-build-more-ethical-ai-training-data-sets/) put roughly 40 million public-domain and Creative-Commons-zero images behind a curation tool that pays creators a tenth-of-a-penny per training download, and the parallel [Public Diffusion / PD12M project](https://spawning.substack.com/p/a-fireside-chat-with-the-creators) trained an image generator only on public-domain and CC0 content as proof-of-concept that the data-dignity framing is buildable rather than only refusable. The argumentative arc those two projects close is the through-line of the Have I Been Trained framing as a whole: the question that opened the campaign in September 2022 — *have I been trained?* — is followed by the registry that lets a creator answer no, and then by the curation tool and the model that show what training looks like when the answer is yes-with-consent.
